ADR-007 — FORECAST TRUTH BOUNDARY

1. TITLE

ADR-007 — Forecast Truth Boundary

2. STATUS

Final

3. CONTEXT

Forge operará con escenarios futuros, brechas, producción proyectada, riesgo de conservación, probabilidad de cierre, cumplimiento de metas, ingresos esperados, persistencia, actividad futura, business planning, compensation outlook, manager alerts y economic motivation.

Forecast Intelligence existe para responder:

¿Qué escenarios futuros son razonables bajo evidencia, supuestos, periodo, incertidumbre y límites explícitos?

Forecast Intelligence no responde:

- qué ocurrirá con certeza
- qué ingreso será pagado
- qué producción será confirmada
- qué cliente comprará
- qué póliza se conservará
- qué asesor calificará oficialmente
- qué resultado futuro ya es hecho

Sin Forecast Truth Boundary, Forge puede:

- presentar forecast como hecho
- presentar riesgo como evento confirmado
- presentar escenario como resultado garantizado
- presentar ingreso proyectado como ingreso real
- presentar producción esperada como producción confirmada
- presentar probabilidad de cierre como cierre esperado
- presentar riesgo de cancelación como cancelación segura
- crear presión comercial basada en futuro no confirmado
- contaminar NBA, Business Planning, Manager Intelligence, Compensation y Economic Motivation
- generar falsa certeza
- crear ansiedad numérica
- inducir decisiones incorrectas

ADR-007 protege la frontera entre escenario y hecho.

4. DECISION

Forecast Intelligence puede producir escenarios, estimaciones, proyecciones, riesgos, gaps y probabilidades.

Forecast Intelligence owns scenarios, not facts.

Forecast no puede redefinir Product Truth, Policy Truth, Metric Truth, Compensation Truth, client intent, confirmed events, paid income, actual production or official qualification.

Todo forecast debe declarar:

- evidencia base
- métricas consumidas
- supuestos
- periodo
- horizonte
- escenario
- incertidumbre
- límites
- qué cambiaría el forecast
- qué no debe interpretarse como hecho

Forecast puede informar decisiones.

Forecast no decide.

Forecast no presiona.

5. DEFINITIONS

Forecast

Representación futura condicionada por evidencia, supuestos, periodo, horizonte e incertidumbre.

Scenario

Caso futuro posible bajo supuestos explícitos.

Projection

Estimación numérica o cualitativa hacia adelante.

Estimate

Aproximación limitada basada en evidencia disponible.

Risk

Posibilidad de resultado negativo no confirmado.

Gap

Diferencia estimada entre estado actual y objetivo, bajo supuestos definidos.

Probability

Medida o clasificación de posibilidad. No es certeza.

Assumption

Condición explícita que permite construir un forecast.

Forecast Input

Dato consumido por Forecast Intelligence con source, owner, periodo, freshness y límites.

Forecast Output

Escenario, rango, gap, riesgo o proyección declarada como no factual.

Forecast Owner

Dominio responsable de producir y limitar escenarios.

Forecast Consumer

Dominio que usa forecast como contexto sin convertirlo en verdad.

Forecast Horizon

Ventana temporal futura del forecast.

Forecast Period

Periodo base de datos usado para construir el forecast.

Forecast Confidence

Confianza cualitativa sobre la evidencia y supuestos del forecast.

Forecast Boundary

Límite que impide convertir futuro no confirmado en hecho.

Confirmed Fact

Evento, valor o estado validado por evidencia oficial o fuente correspondiente.

Actual Result

Resultado ocurrido y confirmado.

Projected Result

Resultado futuro estimado. No es resultado real.

Forecast Drift

Deterioro cuando un forecast se usa como hecho, mandato, presión o truth de otro dominio.

Forecast Staleness

Pérdida de vigencia de forecast por cambio de evidencia, periodo, supuestos, reglas, contexto o resultado real.

No Forecast Possible

Estado donde no existe evidencia o supuesto suficiente para producir escenario responsable.

6. VALID FORECAST INPUTS

Forecast Intelligence may consume:

- official metrics
- confirmed production
- confirmed activity
- Policy Truth
- Product Truth
- compensation rules
- business goals
- CRM records
- historical performance
- system-observed behavior
- manager-confirmed context
- advisor-confirmed context
- external official economic indicators validated
- rule packs
- rule snapshots
- period snapshots
- prior actual results
- explicit user assumptions

Each input must preserve:

- source
- owner
- period
- context
- freshness
- uncertainty
- limits
- evidence state

7. INVALID OR WEAK FORECAST INPUTS

Invalid or weak inputs include:

- invented metrics
- unknown treated as zero without explicit rule
- stale CRM records
- unvalidated assumptions
- client silence treated as intent
- advisor optimism treated as evidence
- manager pressure treated as probability
- AI-generated estimate without source
- compensation desire treated as production likelihood
- single anecdote treated as trend
- correlation treated as causation
- outdated rules
- mixed periods
- mixed snapshots
- product facts not validated by Product Truth
- policy facts not validated by Policy Truth
- economic values without Economic Evidence

8. FORECAST OUTPUT TYPES

Forecast may output:

- scenario
- range
- estimated gap
- projected path
- risk flag
- early warning
- trend interpretation
- conditional projection
- what-if scenario
- validation need
- planning input
- stale forecast warning
- no forecast possible

Forecast must not output as fact:

- confirmed fact
- guaranteed result
- actual income
- actual production
- final qualification
- confirmed cancellation
- confirmed client intent
- paid compensation
- actual policy event

9. FORECAST TRUTH HIERARCHY

Forecast must respect this hierarchy:

Level 1: confirmed facts and official metrics
Level 2: official rule packs and snapshots
Level 3: documented history and system-observed patterns
Level 4: validated assumptions
Level 5: scenario-specific assumptions
Level 6: inference

Rules:

- facts beat forecast
- official metrics beat local projections
- forecast cannot rewrite history
- scenarios must remain scenarios
- inference never becomes future truth
- unknown cannot become zero unless an explicit rule allows it
- actual results supersede projected results

10. INPUT BOUNDARIES

Forecast must not consume evidence in a way that erases its original owner.

Product facts remain owned by Product Truth.

Policy facts remain owned by Policy Truth.

Economic values remain governed by Economic Evidence.

Compensation rules remain governed by the applicable compensation domain and RuleSnapshot.

Productivity metrics remain owned by Productivity.

Mick behavior signals remain behavior signals, not forecast certainty.

Manager pressure is not evidence.

Advisor hope is not evidence.

Client silence is not intent.

Unknown remains unknown unless explicitly modeled as a scenario assumption.

11. OUTPUT BOUNDARIES

Forecast output must be labeled as:

- scenario
- estimate
- projection
- risk
- gap
- what-if
- conditional
- stale
- provisional
- unknown

Forecast output must not be labeled as:

- fact
- confirmed result
- actual income
- actual production
- paid compensation
- official qualification
- client decision
- policy event
- product truth

If a consumer domain uses forecast, it must preserve forecast limits.

12. FORECAST STALENESS

Forecast becomes stale when:

- period changes
- actual results arrive
- production changes
- activity changes
- policy status changes
- product rule changes
- compensation rule changes
- rule snapshot changes
- economic indicator changes
- goal changes
- team context changes
- manager context changes
- advisor context changes
- assumptions no longer hold
- CRM data is incomplete or outdated
- source evidence is superseded

Stale forecast must be marked stale before being reused.

Stale forecast may support review.

Stale forecast must not support pressure, enforcement or confident recommendation.

13. FORECAST DRIFT HANDLING

Forecast Drift occurs when:

- scenario becomes fact
- risk becomes confirmed event
- estimate becomes actual result
- projected income becomes paid income
- projected production becomes confirmed production
- probability becomes certainty
- gap becomes blame
- forecast becomes manager sentence
- forecast becomes compensation truth
- forecast becomes NBA mandate
- forecast becomes NASH pressure language
- forecast becomes client pressure
- unknown becomes zero without rule

When drift is detected, Forge must:

- restore forecast label
- restore uncertainty
- restore assumptions
- restore period and horizon
- identify source evidence
- mark stale or provisional where appropriate
- block factual language
- prevent automatic enforcement
- preserve Authority Boundary
- preserve Client First

14. PARTIAL EVIDENCE HANDLING

With partial evidence, Forecast may:

- produce a limited scenario
- mark assumptions explicitly
- mark confidence as low or provisional
- show missing evidence
- request validation
- narrow the forecast horizon
- produce no forecast possible
- show multiple what-if cases

With partial evidence, Forecast must not:

- produce confident forecast
- invent missing metrics
- invent economic values
- invent probabilities
- hide missing evidence
- convert unknown into zero without rule
- imply future certainty
- pressure advisor or client

Partial evidence should produce partial forecast.

It must not produce fake certainty.

15. FORECAST CONFIDENCE

Forecast may express qualitative confidence:

- alta confianza contextual
- confianza moderada
- confianza baja
- provisional
- validation needed
- stale
- conflicting
- unknown

Forecast confidence describes evidence and assumption strength.

It does not describe advisor worth, client intent or guaranteed outcome.

16. FORECAST VS FACTS

Forecast is not fact.

Predicted income is not paid income.

Projected qualification is not confirmed qualification.

Expected production is not production.

Estimated cancellation risk is not cancellation.

Likely client action is not client decision.

Forecast may inform review.

Forecast may not replace evidence.

17. FORECAST VS AUTHORITY BOUNDARY

Forecast may guide human review.

Forecast may inform planning.

Forecast may identify risk.

Forecast may not decide for advisor.

Forecast may not decide for manager.

Forecast may not decide for client.

Forecast may not automate consequences.

Human authority remains governed by ADR-003.

18. FORECAST VS CLIENT FIRST

Forecast must not create pressure against Client First.

A projected gap does not justify unsuitable recommendation.

A projected income opportunity does not justify client pressure.

A projected conservation risk does not justify manipulation.

Forecast must preserve client trust, suitability, autonomy and truth.

19. CROSS-DOMAIN CONSUMPTION

Business Planning may use Forecast as planning context, not as guaranteed path.

NBA may use Forecast as prioritization context only when ADR-004 and ADR-009 are satisfied.

Manager Intelligence may use Forecast as coaching context, not managerial sentence.

Economic Motivation may use Forecast only with evidence, uncertainty and Client First.

Compensation Intelligence may use Forecast only as scenario, not compensation truth.

NASH may explain Forecast but must not use it as pressure language.

Relationship Intelligence may consume Forecast only without converting relationship timing into certainty.

Advisor Experience may explain Forecast without hiding uncertainty.

Mick may not convert Forecast into behavior judgment.

Productivity may not replace actual metrics with Forecast.

20. SURVIVAL TEST

If ADR-007 disappears:

- forecast can become fact
- projected income can become paid income
- projected production can become actual production
- projected qualification can become official qualification
- risk can become confirmed event
- probability can become certainty
- Business Planning can become false promise
- NBA can become pressure based on future fiction
- Manager Intelligence can turn forecast into sentence
- Compensation Intelligence can mix projection with compensation truth
- Economic Motivation can pressure against Client First
- NASH can use future uncertainty as persuasion

ADR-007 survives because no prior ADR fully governs future-state truth, forecast staleness, forecast drift, assumption visibility or scenario labeling.

21. ABSORPTION TEST

ADR-007 cannot be fully absorbed into ADR-001.

ADR-001 governs evidence, but not future-state scenarios.

ADR-007 cannot be fully absorbed into ADR-002.

ADR-002 governs metric ownership, but not projections or assumptions.

ADR-007 cannot be fully absorbed into ADR-003.

ADR-003 governs authority, but not forecast truth.

ADR-007 cannot be fully absorbed into ADR-004.

ADR-004 governs recommendation validity, but not scenario construction.

ADR-007 cannot be fully absorbed into ADR-005 or ADR-006.

Product Truth and Policy Truth govern existing truth, not future projections.

ADR-007 remains an independent constitutional boundary because future estimates require separate truth rules.

22. ALLOWED BEHAVIOR

Forecast may:

- produce scenarios
- estimate gaps
- show risks
- project under assumptions
- explain uncertainty
- compare scenarios
- generate what-if analysis
- suggest validation
- mark forecast stale
- use forecast as planning input
- recommend actions only if ADR-004 is satisfied
- mark no forecast possible
- preserve source, period and assumptions

23. DISALLOWED BEHAVIOR

Forecast must not:

- present forecast as fact
- present risk as confirmed event
- present projection as real income
- present expected production as confirmed production
- present probability as certainty
- present client likelihood as confirmed intent
- create undue commercial pressure
- hide assumptions
- hide uncertainty
- mix periods
- mix snapshots
- redefine official metrics
- invent rates
- invent economic assumptions
- invent probabilities
- invent scenarios without evidence
- convert unknown into zero unless an explicit rule allows it
- use forecast as policy event
- use forecast as compensation truth
- use forecast as manager enforcement

24. CONSEQUENCES

Positive consequences:

- Forecast becomes more reliable
- false certainty decreases
- Business Planning improves
- Manager Intelligence becomes safer
- Economic Motivation becomes safer
- NBA receives clearer limits
- fact vs future remains separated
- undue commercial pressure decreases
- uncertainty handling improves
- Client First is protected
- assumption traceability improves

Tradeoffs:

- uncertainty is more visible
- fewer magical answers
- forecasts may feel less decisive
- more explicit assumptions are required
- more conditional scenarios are needed
- data validation increases
- speed decreases when inputs are missing
- users who want certainty may feel friction

28. FINAL VERDICT

ADR-007 establishes that Forge may project scenarios, risks, gaps and estimates.

Forge may not convert future not confirmed into truth.

Forecast Intelligence owns scenarios.

Forecast Intelligence does not own facts.

Forecast may inform decisions.

Forecast does not decide.

Forecast does not pressure.

Projected income is not paid income.

Projected production is not production.

Projected qualification is not confirmed qualification.

A projection without evidence, assumptions, period, uncertainty and limits is not Forecast Intelligence.

It is fiction with numbers.
