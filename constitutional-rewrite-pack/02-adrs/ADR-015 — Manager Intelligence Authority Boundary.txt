ADR-015 — MANAGER INTELLIGENCE AUTHORITY BOUNDARY

1. TITLE

ADR-015 — Manager Intelligence Authority Boundary

2. STATUS

Final

3. CONTEXT

Forge ya cuenta con ADR-001 a ADR-014.

ADR-015 define cómo Manager Intelligence puede apoyar coaching, observación, desarrollo, seguimiento y claridad gerencial sin convertirse en enforcement automático, juicio laboral, vigilancia, ranking humano o sustituto de autoridad humana.

Manager Intelligence existe para responder:

What does a manager need to see, understand or review to coach responsibly?

Manager Intelligence is not:

- Mick
- Productivity
- Business Planning
- Advisor Experience
- HR
- disciplinary system
- automatic authority

Manager Intelligence supports managerial judgment.

It does not replace managerial judgment.

Sin Manager Intelligence Authority Boundary, Forge puede:

- convertir alertas en castigos
- convertir métricas en rankings humanos
- convertir Forecast en sentencia gerencial
- convertir Mick en diagnóstico personal
- convertir Productivity en valor humano
- convertir Business Planning en mandato
- convertir Advisor Experience signals en vigilancia
- tratar manager pressure como evidencia
- convertir dashboards en tableros punitivos
- convertir coaching en obediencia
- convertir economic gaps en presión
- producir lenguaje punitivo disfrazado de insight
- automatizar consecuencias humanas
- dañar Authority Boundary
- dañar Advisor Capability Growth
- dañar Client First

ADR-015 protege la frontera entre inteligencia gerencial, coaching responsable, observabilidad y autoridad humana.

4. DECISION

Manager Intelligence is the conceptual owner of manager-facing coaching context, attention signals and team-level interpretation.

Manager Intelligence may help managers observe, understand, prepare conversations, review risks and detect support needs.

Manager Intelligence must not decide human consequences.

Manager Intelligence must not automate sanctions, promotions, separation, punishments, human rankings or final labor evaluations.

Manager Intelligence supports coaching.

Manager Intelligence does not automate consequences.

Manager Intelligence helps managers see better.

It does not help them punish faster.

5. DEFINITIONS

Manager Intelligence

Dominio responsable de producir contexto gerencial, señales de atención, patrones de equipo, preparación de coaching y observaciones útiles para desarrollo responsable.

Manager-Facing Interpretation

Interpretación destinada al manager para ayudarlo a entender contexto, riesgos, patrones o necesidades de apoyo.

Coaching Context

Información contextual para preparar coaching sin dictar castigo, mandato o consecuencia automática.

Attention Needed

Señal que indica posible área de revisión humana por parte del manager.

Human Review Recommended

Estado donde Forge recomienda revisión humana antes de interpretar o actuar.

Manager Authority

Autoridad humana del manager para tomar decisiones de coaching, desarrollo, acompañamiento y revisión dentro de límites éticos y organizacionales.

Manager Enforcement

Uso de señales para activar castigo, sanción, presión jerárquica, consecuencia laboral o mandato de obediencia.

Manager Intelligence Drift

Deterioro cuando una señal gerencial se convierte en castigo, ranking, vigilancia, presión, diagnóstico o consecuencia automática.

Manager Intelligence Staleness

Pérdida de vigencia de una señal por cambio de periodo, equipo, manager, campaña, metas, contexto humano, evidencia o fuente.

Team Pattern Summary

Resumen agregado de patrones de equipo, sin convertirlo en ranking humano.

Advisor Support Signal

Señal de posible necesidad de apoyo, ayuda, revisión, coaching o contexto humano.

No Manager Conclusion Possible

Estado donde no existe evidencia suficiente para producir conclusión gerencial responsable.

6. MANAGER INTELLIGENCE OWNERSHIP RULES

Manager Intelligence may own:

- coaching context
- attention needed signals
- advisor support signals
- team pattern summaries
- development context
- one-on-one preparation context
- planning review suggestions
- productivity context summaries
- behavior context summaries
- evidence gap warnings
- uncertainty warnings
- human review recommendations
- no manager conclusion possible states

Manager Intelligence does not own:

- behavior truth
- productivity truth
- forecast truth
- business planning truth
- compensation truth
- economic truth
- product truth
- policy truth
- advisor worth
- advisor potential
- employment evaluation
- disciplinary authority
- promotion authority
- termination authority
- human ranking
- HR decision authority

Manager Intelligence may consume official signals from other domains without redefining them or converting them into consequences.

8. INPUT BOUNDARIES

Productivity Metrics

Can show activity, throughput, conversion and output.

Must not prove human worth, total quality, labor merit or advisor potential.

Mick Behavior Signals

Can show observable behavioral patterns.

Must not prove personality, motivation, character, moral discipline or internal commitment.

Business Planning Outputs

Can show routes, gaps, constraints and plan states.

Must not prove real execution, automatic progress or human failure.

Forecast Scenarios

Can show scenarios, risks and projected gaps.

Must not prove future, failure, success or managerial sentence.

NBA Outputs

Can show prioritized action candidates.

Must not become mandates or expected obedience.

NASH Usage / Context

Can show use, adaptation or rejection of conversational guidance.

Must not prove fixed conversational talent or resistance.

Advisor Experience Signals

Can show friction, usage, abandonment or support need.

Must not prove lack of capacity, bad attitude or resistance.

Economic Evidence

Can show valid economic values, scenarios or gaps.

Must not justify commercial or hierarchical pressure.

Compensation Context

Can show rules, bonuses, commissions or snapshots when applicable.

Must not pressure the advisor against Client First.

Policy Truth

Can provide policy states or events.

Must not become pressure on advisor without context.

Product Truth

Can provide product rules or conditions.

Must not become sales mandate.

Relationship Intelligence Context

Can provide relationship opportunity or timing.

Must not convert relationships into manager commercial inventory.

Manager-Confirmed Context

Can clarify human or team context.

Must not become absolute truth or final diagnosis.

Advisor-Confirmed Context

Can clarify advisor situation.

Must not be extrapolated beyond what is confirmed.

9. OUTPUTS PERMITIDOS

Manager Intelligence may generate:

- coaching context
- attention needed
- human review recommended
- team pattern summary
- advisor support signal
- coaching opportunity
- evidence gap
- uncertainty warning
- risk observation
- development context
- one-on-one preparation context
- planning review suggestion
- productivity context summary
- behavior context summary
- forecast context summary
- advisor experience support context
- no manager conclusion possible
- stale signal warning
- conflicting signal warning
- validation needed

Allowed examples:

- Hay señal de fricción en uso de Forge; posible oportunidad de apoyo.
- El plan parece stale; revisar supuestos antes de usarlo para coaching.
- Existen señales contradictorias; no hay conclusión gerencial responsable.
- Human review recommended antes de interpretar este patrón.

- manager enforcement
- punishment trigger
- obedience score

- fixed potential assessment

Prohibited examples:

- El asesor debe ser sancionado.
- El asesor debe ser separado.
- El asesor no sirve.
- El asesor tiene bajo valor.
- El asesor debe ser rankeado abajo.
- El asesor no está comprometido.
- El asesor no obedece.
- El asesor tiene bajo potencial.

11. MANAGER INTELLIGENCE STATES

Manager Intelligence may produce these states:

- Coaching Context Available
- Attention Needed
- Human Review Recommended
- Manager Review Needed
- Evidence Gap
- Uncertainty Warning
- Stale Signal
- Conflicting Signals
- Observed but Limited
- No Manager Conclusion Possible

These states describe evidence and review posture.

They do not describe human value, obedience, worth, character or fixed potential.

12. MANAGER INTELLIGENCE CONFIDENCE

Manager Intelligence may express qualitative confidence only:

- alta confianza contextual
- confianza moderada
- confianza baja
- provisional
- validation needed
- stale
- conflicting
- unknown

Forbidden:

- Manager Score
- Advisor Value Score
- Human Performance Score
- Obedience Score
- Risk-to-Fire Score
- Promotion Score
- Disciplinary Score

Confidence describes evidence strength, not human quality.

13. MANAGER INTELLIGENCE VS MICK

Mick observes behavior patterns.

Manager Intelligence contextualizes them for responsible coaching.

Manager Intelligence must not turn behavior signals into judgment, punishment, diagnosis or consequence.

Mick can say a behavior pattern appears observable.

Manager Intelligence can say the manager may review context.

Manager Intelligence cannot say the person is lazy, uncommitted, disobedient or flawed.

14. MANAGER INTELLIGENCE VS PRODUCTIVITY

Productivity measures activity, conversion, throughput and output.

Manager Intelligence helps manager understand those metrics for coaching.

Metrics must not become human ranking, punishment or final labor evaluation.

Productivity owns the metric.

Manager Intelligence owns responsible manager-facing context.

15. MANAGER INTELLIGENCE VS BUSINESS PLANNING

Business Planning produces reviewable action paths.

Manager Intelligence may review plans as coaching input.

Plans must not become orders or automatic noncompliance evidence.

A stale plan may justify review.

It must not justify punishment.

16. MANAGER INTELLIGENCE VS FORECAST

Forecast produces scenarios, risks and projected gaps.

Manager Intelligence may use Forecast to observe risk and prepare coaching.

Forecast must not become managerial sentence.

Projected failure is not failure.

Projected success is not success.

17. MANAGER INTELLIGENCE VS ADVISOR EXPERIENCE

Advisor Experience adapts help, learning, progression and friction reduction.

Manager Intelligence supports manager coaching.

Advisor Experience signals must not become surveillance, pressure or human judgment.

Friction may indicate support need.

It must not prove resistance or incapacity.

18. MANAGER INTELLIGENCE VS COMPENSATION / ECONOMIC MOTIVATION

Compensation Intelligence interprets economic rules, bonuses, commissions and snapshots.

Economic Motivation uses economics as motivational context with Client First.

Manager Intelligence may see limited economic context for coaching.

Manager Intelligence must not use compensation, bonuses or economic forecast as undue pressure.

Income opportunity is not permission to pressure.

Economic gap is not managerial threat.

19. MANAGER INTELLIGENCE VS RELATIONSHIP INTELLIGENCE

Relationship Intelligence identifies relationship opportunities and timing.

Manager Intelligence may understand relationship context for coaching.

Manager Intelligence must not use advisor relationships as commercial inventory or hierarchical pressure.

A relationship opportunity is not manager-owned inventory.

Client trust must remain protected.

20. COACHING VS ENFORCEMENT

Coaching seeks development, clarity, support, learning and improvement.

Enforcement seeks punishment, obedience, pressure or consequence.

Manager Intelligence must remain on responsible coaching side.

Manager Intelligence may support coaching conversations.

Manager Intelligence must not convert coaching into obedience.

Manager Intelligence must not use punitive language disguised as insight.

A manager may decide to intervene.

Forge must not automate that consequence.

21. OBSERVABILITY VS SURVEILLANCE

Manager Intelligence may show signals necessary for managerial clarity and responsible coaching.

Manager Intelligence must not become surveillance.

Responsible observability:

- preserves source
- preserves context
- preserves period
- declares uncertainty
- limits inference
- preserves human authority
- orients toward support and development

Improper surveillance:

- maximizes control
- hides criteria
- creates fear
- turns dashboards into punishment
- reduces advisors to numbers
- converts signals into obedience
- produces human rankings
- activates automatic consequences

22. PARTIAL EVIDENCE HANDLING

With partial evidence, Manager Intelligence may:

- mark observed but limited
- mark manager review needed
- suggest human context required
- declare uncertainty
- mark evidence gap
- mark conflicting signals
- mark stale signal
- request validation
- show possible attention area

With partial evidence, Manager Intelligence must not:

- conclude performance
- recommend sanction
- trigger enforcement
- rank advisor
- infer human worth
- infer intention or attitude
- convert unknown into conclusion
- hide uncertainty

23. MANAGER INTELLIGENCE STALENESS

Manager signal can become stale when:

- period changed
- team context changed
- manager changed
- campaign changed
- goal changed
- plan changed
- productivity changed
- observed behavior changed
- forecast changed
- policy status changed
- economic evidence changed
- confirmed personal context changed
- evidence is incomplete
- CRM is incomplete
- signals conflict

Stale manager signal must not be used as strong basis without warning.

Stale signal should produce review, not enforcement.

24. MANAGER INTELLIGENCE DRIFT HANDLING

Manager Intelligence Drift occurs when:

- alerts become punishments
- metrics become ranking
- Forecast becomes sentence
- Mick becomes personal diagnosis
- Productivity becomes human worth
- plan becomes mandate
- Advisor Experience signal becomes surveillance
- manager pressure becomes evidence
- dashboard becomes punitive board
- coaching becomes obedience
- economic gap becomes pressure
- relationship context becomes exploitation
- unknown becomes managerial conclusion

When drift exists, Forge must:

- mark boundary risk
- separate signal from interpretation
- declare uncertainty
- preserve source, owner, period and context
- recommend human review
- avoid automatic enforcement
- block automatic human judgment
- preserve unknown

25. AUTHORITY BOUNDARY

Manager Intelligence does not decide for manager.

Manager Intelligence does not decide for advisor.

Manager Intelligence does not decide for client.

Manager Intelligence does not automate human consequences.

Manager Intelligence does not create enforcement.

Manager Intelligence does not replace managerial judgment.

Manager Intelligence may inform.

Manager Intelligence may contextualize.

Manager Intelligence may suggest human review.

Human authority remains with manager, advisor and client under ADR-003.

26. CLIENT FIRST

Manager Intelligence must not use metrics, forecast, relationships, productivity or economics to pressure actions that violate Client First.

Commercial development does not justify undue pressure on clients.

Team goal does not justify client manipulation.

Compensation does not justify bad recommendation.

Manager must not receive signals that orient toward sacrificing client suitability for production.

Client First remains above production pressure.

27. ADVISOR CAPABILITY GROWTH

Manager Intelligence must strengthen advisor capability through better coaching, not generate fear, obedience or dependency.

A useful manager signal helps understand:

- what pattern is observed
- what evidence supports it
- what uncertainty exists
- what context is missing
- what conversation may help
- what must not be concluded
- what support may be offered

Manager Intelligence must help managers develop people.

Not administer punishments faster.

28. SURVIVAL TEST

If ADR-015 disappears:

- Manager Intelligence can become algorithmic boss.
- Dashboards can become punitive boards.
- Metrics can become rankings.
- Mick signals can become diagnoses.
- Productivity can become human value.
- Forecast can become sentence.
- Business Planning can become mandate.
- Advisor Experience signals can become surveillance.
- Compensation context can become pressure.
- Relationship context can become exploitation.
- Client First can be harmed by production pressure.
- Advisor Capability Growth can become obedience.
- Authority Boundary can be weakened.

29. ABSORPTION TEST

ADR-015 cannot be absorbed into ADR-003 because Authority Boundary says who decides, but does not define manager-facing coaching context, observability vs surveillance, or coaching vs enforcement.

ADR-015 cannot be absorbed into ADR-012 because Business Planning defines action paths, not managerial interpretation.

ADR-015 cannot be absorbed into ADR-013 because Mick observes behavior, but does not define how managers consume behavior signals responsibly.

ADR-015 cannot be absorbed into ADR-014 because Productivity owns metrics, but not managerial coaching context.

ADR-015 cannot be absorbed into ADR-016 because Advisor Experience is advisor-facing, while Manager Intelligence is manager-facing.

ADR-015 survives independently because it protects coaching, observability, human authority and non-enforcement.

30. ALLOWED BEHAVIOR

Manager Intelligence may:

- consume official metrics
- consume Productivity Metrics
- consume Mick Behavior Signals
- consume Business Planning outputs
- consume Forecast scenarios
- consume Advisor Experience signals
- consume relationship context when relevant
- consume Economic Evidence
- consume Compensation context
- detect coaching opportunities
- signal attention needed
- suggest human review
- explain uncertainty
- show team context
- identify aggregate patterns
- identify operational friction
- detect missing evidence
- support coaching conversations
- help manager prepare one-on-ones
- show risks without turning them into condemnation
- deny manager conclusion when evidence is insufficient

31. DISALLOWED BEHAVIOR

Manager Intelligence must not:

- automate sanctions
- automate promotions
- automate separation
- decide hiring or firing
- create final labor judgment
- create human ranking
- create human worth score
- create advisor value score
- use metrics as punishment
- use Mick as diagnosis
- use Productivity as human worth
- use Forecast as sentence
- use Business Planning as mandate
- convert alerts into enforcement
- convert coaching into obedience
- replace manager judgment
- manipulate advisor through hierarchical pressure
- produce punitive language disguised as insight
- convert dashboards into punitive boards
- convert incomplete signals into managerial conclusions
- act as HR
- create disciplinary process

32. CONSEQUENCES

Positive Consequences:

- more responsible Manager Intelligence
- better contextual coaching
- better separation coaching vs enforcement
- better use of Productivity Metrics
- better use of Mick Behavior Signals
- better use of Business Planning
- better use of Forecast
- less punitive dashboard risk
- less human ranking risk
- less automated hierarchical pressure
- stronger Authority Boundary
- stronger Client First
- stronger Advisor Capability Growth

Tradeoffs:

- more human review
- more visible uncertainty
- less managerial automation
- fewer punitive dashboards
- fewer quick conclusions
- more limits on manager alerts
- more care with incomplete signals
- less ability to use Forge as automatic boss

These tradeoffs are acceptable because Forge must help managers develop people, not replace them with algorithmic enforcement.

36. FINAL VERDICT

ADR-015 establishes that Manager Intelligence may support coaching, observation, development, follow-up and managerial clarity.

Manager Intelligence supports coaching.

Manager Intelligence does not automate consequences.

Manager Intelligence helps managers see better.

It does not help them punish faster.

Alert is not punishment.

Metric is not ranking.

Forecast is not sentence.

Mick signal is not diagnosis.

Plan is not mandate.

Dashboard is not tribunal.

Coaching is not obedience.

Manager pressure is not evidence.

Manager Intelligence must develop people, not reduce them to signals.

A manager signal without evidence, context, uncertainty and authority boundary is not Manager Intelligence.

It is algorithmic boss in a suit.
