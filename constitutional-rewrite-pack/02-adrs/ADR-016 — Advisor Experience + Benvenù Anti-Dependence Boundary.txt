ADR-016 — ADVISOR EXPERIENCE + BENVENÙ ANTI-DEPENDENCE BOUNDARY

1. TITLE

ADR-016 — Advisor Experience + Benvenù Anti-Dependence Boundary

2. STATUS

Final

3. CONTEXT

Forge ya cuenta con ADR-001 a ADR-015.

ADR-016 define cómo Advisor Experience y Benvenù pueden ayudar al asesor a aprender, usar Forge, reducir fricción, lograr first value y desarrollar capacidad sin crear dependencia, ocultar razonamiento, manipular comportamiento o convertir Forge en muleta operativa.

Advisor Experience existe para responder:

¿Cómo debe vivir, entender, aprender y usar Forge el asesor para mejorar su capacidad?

Benvenù existe para responder:

¿Cómo debe ser la primera experiencia de valor para que el asesor entienda Forge y actúe sin sentir formulario, onboarding corporativo o CRM tradicional?

Advisor Experience y Benvenù protegen la frontera entre experiencia útil y dependencia operativa.

Forge debe hacer al asesor más capaz, no más dependiente.

Sin este boundary, Forge puede:

- explicar recomendaciones ocultando incertidumbre
- enseñar workflows sin evidencia suficiente
- convertir ayuda contextual en instrucciones obligatorias
- convertir Benvenù en captura inicial excesiva
- pedir datos antes de entregar valor
- crear dependencia del asesor hacia Forge
- esconder razonamiento detrás de lenguaje bonito
- convertir first value en onboarding genérico
- confundir simplicidad con pérdida de verdad
- confundir guía con mandato
- confundir fricción reducida con criterio reemplazado
- convertir señales de aprendizaje en vigilancia
- usar gamificación para presionar conducta
- enseñar funciones que el asesor ya domina
- repetir ayuda irrelevante
- convertir claridad de producto, póliza, forecast o economía en afirmaciones no validadas

ADR-016 protege la frontera entre experiencia, aprendizaje, adopción y autoridad profesional.

4. DECISION

Advisor Experience es el owner conceptual de advisor-facing explanation, contextual help, learning support, friction reduction, capability-building experience y feature learning state.

Benvenù es el owner conceptual de first-value experience.

Advisor Experience puede explicar, contextualizar, enseñar, orientar, revelar capacidades, reducir fricción y ayudar al asesor a entender outputs de Forge.

Advisor Experience no puede ocultar evidencia, ocultar incertidumbre, manipular conducta, reemplazar criterio profesional, crear dependencia operativa o convertir recomendaciones en mandatos.

Benvenù puede crear una primera experiencia útil, clara, mínima, responsable y accionable.

Benvenù no puede convertirse en formulario, CRM intake, onboarding corporativo, captura excesiva, falsa personalización o condición previa para que Forge entregue valor.

Advisor Experience aumenta capacidad del asesor.

Advisor Experience no aumenta dependencia.

Benvenù crea first value.

Benvenù no crea first friction.

5. DEFINITIONS

Advisor Experience

Dominio responsable de cómo el asesor descubre, entiende, aprende y usa Forge para desarrollar mayor capacidad comercial y operativa.

Benvenù

Primera experiencia de valor de Forge. Debe producir claridad o acción útil antes de exigir captura extensa.

First Value

Primer resultado durable que ayuda al asesor a entender, decidir o actuar mejor.

First Friction

Fricción inicial que aparece antes de que Forge haya producido valor claro para el asesor.

Capability Building

Proceso por el cual Forge ayuda al asesor a mejorar criterio, entendimiento, acción y autonomía.

Advisor Dependency

Estado no deseado donde el asesor requiere que Forge le diga qué hacer, qué pensar o cómo actuar sin entender evidencia, límites o criterio.

Contextual Help

Ayuda situada en el contexto del trabajo actual. Puede orientar o explicar, pero no bloquear ni mandar.

Feature Learning State

Estado que indica si el asesor no ha visto, fue introducido, intentó, completó con ayuda, completó sin ayuda, aprendió, suprimió o necesita refrescar una capacidad.

Progressive Discovery

Revelación gradual de capacidades de Forge según contexto, necesidad y madurez del asesor.

Explanation Layer

Capa que traduce outputs estructurados de Forge en lenguaje comprensible sin alterar la verdad de origen.

Clippy Engine

Motor de ayuda contextual no invasivo. Orienta, explica o revela capacidades sin interrumpir ni bloquear.

Candy Crush Experience

Adaptación de complejidad para reducir abandono cuando hay frustración y aumentar reto cuando existe progreso.

Anti-Dependence

Principio que obliga a Forge a aumentar autonomía, criterio y capacidad del asesor.

Benvenù must not ask for duplicate manual capture when Forge can infer, import, extract or derive a reliable data point.

Advisor Experience must not use:

- manager pressure as truth
- emotional vulnerability as a learning trigger
- Product Truth not validated by ADR-005
- Policy Truth not validated by ADR-006
- forecasts presented as facts
- economic values without ADR-008 evidence
- NASH drafts as final client communication
- NBA priority as command
- productivity metrics as human worth
- Mick signals as diagnosis
- compensation projections without supporting rules
- stale signals without warning
- feature usage as proof of competence
- repeated dismissal as disobedience

Benvenù must not use:

- long mandatory forms before value
- fake personalization from weak inputs
- hidden default assumptions
- inferred client needs without evidence
- invented product focus
- invented revenue opportunity
- invented advisor baseline
- imported data without source awareness
- user friction as permission to hide uncertainty

Unknown values remain unknown.

Partial evidence remains partial.

First experience must not bypass Forge truth boundaries.

15. ADVISOR EXPERIENCE VS NBA

NBA owns action prioritization.

Advisor Experience can explain why an NBA exists, what evidence supports it, what uncertainty remains and how to use it responsibly.

Advisor Experience cannot create a separate NBA, override NBA priority, turn NBA into a command or hide alternatives.

NBA says what action may be next.

Advisor Experience helps the advisor understand how to use that action without losing judgment.

16. ADVISOR EXPERIENCE VS NASH

NASH owns conversation intelligence and language drafts.

Advisor Experience can explain how to use a NASH draft, what the draft is trying to accomplish and what boundaries apply.

Advisor Experience cannot convert NASH into mandatory copy-paste, alter truth inside NASH, add pressure language or remove uncertainty.

NASH may draft.

Advisor Experience may teach.

The advisor decides.

17. ADVISOR EXPERIENCE VS BUSINESS PLANNING

Business Planning owns goals, gaps, constraints and plan-to-action structure.

Advisor Experience can explain the plan, help the advisor understand next steps and reduce friction in reviewing the plan.

Advisor Experience cannot create planning truth, guarantee outcomes, convert a plan into a blind checklist or hide constraints.

A plan remains revisable.

Advisor Experience must preserve that revisability.

18. ADVISOR EXPERIENCE VS MICK

Mick owns behavior intelligence.

Advisor Experience can explain behavior signals in a non-punitive way when permitted.

Advisor Experience cannot diagnose character, shame the advisor, convert Mick into surveillance or turn a behavior signal into human worth.

Mick observes patterns.

Advisor Experience helps the advisor learn from them.

19. ADVISOR EXPERIENCE VS PRODUCTIVITY

Productivity owns activity, throughput, conversion and output metrics.

Advisor Experience can explain what a productivity metric means, why it may matter and what evidence or period supports it.

Advisor Experience cannot rank human value, create pressure language, turn metrics into shame or redefine metric ownership.

Productivity measures.

Advisor Experience teaches interpretation.

20. ADVISOR EXPERIENCE VS MANAGER INTELLIGENCE

Manager Intelligence owns manager-facing coaching context.

Advisor Experience can consume only manager-related context that is permitted for advisor-facing explanation.

Advisor Experience cannot turn manager attention signals into surveillance, punishment, employment conclusions or pressure.

Manager Intelligence supports coaching.

Advisor Experience supports advisor capability.

21. ADVISOR EXPERIENCE VS ECONOMIC MOTIVATION / COMPENSATION

Economic Motivation and Compensation domains own economic and compensation interpretations under evidence and rule boundaries.

Advisor Experience can explain economic outputs only when evidence, rule context, period context, confidence and uncertainty are present.

Advisor Experience cannot invent income potential, expected income, income at risk, commission, contest progress, compensation eligibility or economic urgency.

Economic value without evidence remains unknown.

Advisor Experience cannot make unknown money feel known.

22. ADVISOR EXPERIENCE VS PRODUCT / POLICY TRUTH

Product Truth owns product facts.

Policy Truth owns specific policy facts.

Advisor Experience can explain Product Truth and Policy Truth in simpler language.

Advisor Experience cannot invent product benefits, policy status, coverage, exclusions, premiums, values or suitability.

Simplicity is allowed.

Truth alteration is prohibited.

23. BENVENÙ BOUNDARY

Benvenù exists to create first value, not first friction.

Benvenù must:

- start from evidence already available when possible
- ask only the minimum necessary question when evidence is missing
- produce a useful first output before broad setup
- show uncertainty instead of hiding it
- avoid generic CRM onboarding
- avoid corporate tutorial posture
- avoid long forms
- avoid fake personalization
- create durable value
- preserve advisor authority
- preserve truth boundaries

Benvenù may introduce Forge through:

- first advisor baseline snapshot
- first evidence map
- first relationship opportunity
- first policy review area
- first product focus area
- first activity insight
- first planning orientation
- first command discovery
- first learning state

Benvenù must not make the advisor feel that Forge is asking for work before Forge has earned trust.

24. ADVISOR EXPERIENCE STATES

Advisor Experience may track these learning states:

- unseen
- introduced
- tried
- completed_with_help
- completed_without_help
- learned
- suppressed
- needs_refresh

These states describe learning and interaction support.

They do not describe intelligence, discipline, worth, loyalty, productivity or commitment.

A feature should stop teaching itself when the advisor can complete the work without help.

Help that continues after learning becomes friction.

25. ADVISOR EXPERIENCE STALENESS

Advisor Experience signals become stale when:

- the advisor has learned the feature
- the workflow changed
- evidence changed
- domain output changed
- user role changed
- rule-pack context changed
- the advisor dismissed help repeatedly
- the advisor completed the task without help
- old confusion no longer applies
- new uncertainty appears

Stale help must be suppressed, refreshed or revalidated.

Stale help must not be repeated as if still relevant.

26. ADVISOR EXPERIENCE DRIFT HANDLING

Advisor Experience drift occurs when:

- help becomes command
- explanation becomes persuasion
- simplification hides uncertainty
- first value becomes onboarding
- learning state becomes surveillance
- gamification becomes pressure
- clarity becomes overconfidence
- reduced complexity becomes reduced truth
- guidance becomes dependency
- NASH becomes copy-paste obligation
- NBA becomes order
- Productivity becomes shame
- Mick becomes manipulation
- Manager Intelligence becomes monitoring

When drift is detected, Forge must:

- restore evidence visibility
- restore uncertainty visibility
- restore human authority
- lower pressure language
- suppress irrelevant help
- ask for missing evidence instead of inventing
- identify the owning domain
- avoid automatic escalation
- mark the output as limited

27. PARTIAL EVIDENCE HANDLING — ADVISOR EXPERIENCE

When evidence is partial, Advisor Experience may:

- explain what is known
- explain what is unknown
- explain what evidence is missing
- explain which domain owns the missing truth
- suggest a verification step
- offer a limited orientation
- reduce confidence language
- avoid final claims
- avoid irreversible guidance

Advisor Experience must not:

- fill missing facts with plausible defaults
- hide partial evidence
- convert weak evidence into confident teaching
- teach a feature as if its output were complete
- simplify away missing context
- make a recommendation appear stronger than it is

Partial evidence should produce partial explanation.

It should not produce fake certainty.

28. PARTIAL EVIDENCE HANDLING — BENVENÙ

When Benvenù has partial evidence, it may:

- create a first evidence map
- ask one minimal clarifying question
- produce a limited first-value output
- explain what Forge can already do
- explain what Forge cannot yet know
- show next evidence needed
- preserve unknown states

Benvenù must not:

- invent a complete advisor baseline
- invent a first opportunity
- invent a product focus
- invent a revenue path
- create fake personalization
- require full setup before any value
- ask for duplicate capture
- hide that the first output is limited

Benvenù should make partial value useful without pretending it is complete.

29. ANTI-DEPENDENCE RULES

Forge must make the advisor more capable.

Advisor Experience must:

- explain reasoning enough for the advisor to learn
- reveal evidence and uncertainty
- teach transferable judgment
- reduce unnecessary friction
- stop teaching learned features
- avoid making the advisor wait for Forge when human judgment is sufficient
- encourage review where evidence is weak
- preserve advisor ownership of action

Advisor Experience must not:

- create blind obedience
- make Forge the only path to action
- hide why a recommendation exists
- hide why Forge cannot answer
- over-assist until the advisor stops thinking
- use confidence theater
- make help emotionally coercive
- make the advisor dependent on prompts for basic professional judgment

Help is successful when the advisor needs less help over time.

30. CLIENT FIRST

Advisor Experience must preserve Client First.

Clarity for the advisor must not come at the expense of client autonomy, suitability, trust or truth.

Advisor Experience cannot:

- pressure the advisor to act against client interest
- hide client-facing uncertainty
- turn relationship trust into leverage
- turn economic motivation into pressure
- turn forecast gaps into urgency theater
- turn product explanation into sales pressure
- turn policy explanation into unsupported claims

Benvenù must not optimize adoption by weakening Client First.

First value is invalid if it teaches the advisor to act without client trust, evidence or authority.

31. AUTHORITY BOUNDARY

Advisor Experience may guide.

Advisor Experience may teach.

Advisor Experience may explain.

Advisor Experience may suggest review.

Advisor Experience may reduce friction.

Advisor Experience may not decide.

Advisor Experience may not execute.

Advisor Experience may not mandate.

Advisor Experience may not replace the advisor, manager or client.

Advisor authority remains with the advisor.

Manager authority remains with the manager.

Client authority remains with the client.

Forge decides only within its defined system boundaries.

Generative AI explains; it does not create truth.

32. SURVIVAL TEST

If ADR-016 disappears, Forge can still have engines and outputs, but the advisor experience can drift into:

- onboarding before value
- help before context
- prompts before evidence
- dashboards before understanding
- explanation without uncertainty
- action without learning
- automation without capability
- gamification without judgment
- adoption pressure without trust
- dependency disguised as simplicity

ADR-016 survives because no previous ADR fully governs learning state, first-value experience, help suppression, progressive discovery, first friction or anti-dependence.

33. ABSORPTION TEST

ADR-016 cannot be fully absorbed into ADR-003.

ADR-003 governs authority, but not advisor learning or first experience.

ADR-016 cannot be fully absorbed into ADR-004.

ADR-004 governs recommendation validity, but not contextual help or anti-dependence.

ADR-016 cannot be fully absorbed into ADR-009.

ADR-009 governs action prioritization, but not feature learning state.

ADR-016 cannot be fully absorbed into ADR-010.

ADR-010 governs conversation intelligence, but not advisor experience.

ADR-016 cannot be fully absorbed into ADR-012.

ADR-012 governs plans, but not first value or help behavior.

ADR-016 cannot be fully absorbed into ADR-013 or ADR-014.

Mick and Productivity govern behavior and metrics, not learning experience.

ADR-016 cannot be fully absorbed into ADR-015.

Manager Intelligence is manager-facing. ADR-016 is advisor-facing.

Therefore ADR-016 remains an independent constitutional boundary.

34. ALLOWED BEHAVIOR

Allowed behavior:

- explain Forge outputs in plain language
- show evidence and uncertainty
- orient the advisor in context
- reveal relevant capabilities progressively
- reduce unnecessary steps
- ask minimal clarifying questions
- create first value before heavy capture
- help the advisor learn a workflow
- suppress help after mastery
- refresh help when context changes
- warn when evidence is partial or stale
- identify the owning domain
- preserve NBA, NASH, Product Truth, Policy Truth, Forecast Truth and Economic Evidence boundaries
- lower complexity when frustration is detected
- increase challenge when capability is demonstrated

35. DISALLOWED BEHAVIOR

Disallowed behavior:

- invent facts
- invent recommendations
- invent products
- invent premiums
- invent coverage
- invent economic values
- hide evidence
- hide uncertainty
- hide unknown states
- replace human authority
- mandate action
- execute action
- pressure adoption
- convert first experience into form completion
- convert learning state into surveillance
- convert help into command
- convert simplicity into false certainty
- convert gamification into pressure
- repeat irrelevant help
- teach a learned function unnecessarily
- create advisor dependency

36. CONSEQUENCES

Positive consequences:

- advisor adoption improves through value, not pressure
- first experience becomes useful instead of bureaucratic
- advisors learn Forge without losing judgment
- explanations preserve truth boundaries
- contextual help becomes safer
- Benvenù avoids CRM-style onboarding
- feature learning can mature responsibly
- advisor autonomy increases over time
- Client First is protected in experience design

Tradeoffs:

- more discipline is required before showing help
- some onboarding shortcuts are prohibited
- some personalization must remain limited until evidence exists
- some help must be suppressed even if engagement metrics would increase
- first experience may ask fewer questions and therefore produce narrower initial value
- product teams must distinguish clarity from certainty
- adoption cannot be optimized by hiding complexity that matters
