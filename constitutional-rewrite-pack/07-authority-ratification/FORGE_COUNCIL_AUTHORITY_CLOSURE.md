# Forge Council Authority Closure

Status: RATIFIED COUNCIL / EXPLICITLY BOUNDED ROLES

## Council authority

The Constitution and AGENTS ratify the Forge Council as a review body. Council members contribute named review lenses. Council membership does not make a persona a source of truth, metric owner, autonomous intelligence, runtime actor or execution authority.

## Role classes

### Mandatory governance gate

- Miranda: quality, discipline, priority and product-quality approval. Miranda cannot override Constitution, Article 0, ADRs or required Board approval.

### ADR-bounded intelligence roles

- Nash: conversation intelligence under ADR-010.
- Mick: behavior intelligence under ADR-013 and metric limits in ADR-014.

### Ratified but implementation-blocked domain role

- Andrey: restricted Human Capital Allocation advisory scope. See `ANDREY_AUTHORITY_RATIFICATION_CLOSURE.md`.

### Advisory-only Council lenses

- Joy Mangano: real-world utility.
- Arqui Juve: maintainability, scalability and architecture review.
- Jurgen Klaric: consumer-psychology review.
- Jordan Belfort: sales-conversion review.
- Hitch: rapport review.
- Patch Adams: human-trust and empathy review.
- Chris Gardner: execution and prospecting review.
- Rocky: consistency review.
- Nicky Spurgeon: networking and referrals review.

These advisory roles have no independent authority to create facts, infer intent, score people, select products, direct compensation, approve employment outcomes, send messages or execute changes.

## Relationship to Board, ROBOCOP and Article 0

- Article 0 constrains every Council role.
- ROBOCOP requires Miranda and Board status before protected work.
- Board approval is distinct from Council review.
- A Council recommendation cannot ratify its own authority expansion.
- Arqui Juve review cannot replace an ADR.
- Nash, Mick and Andrey cannot approve their own runtime.

## Promotion rule

An advisory Council lens requires a new ADR only if Forge proposes to turn it into a truth-owning, metric-owning, recommendation-producing or action-producing domain. Mere continued use as a human review perspective requires no new ADR.
