## 1. THE CHALLENGE
Under the **Miranda Test** and the **"No Invented Data"** principle, Forge must avoid speculative language. The current term used in Seat #13's domain, **"Future Capacity Projection,"** contains a semantic risk of "Projection" without sufficient deterministic evidence.

## 2. TERMINOLOGY EVALUATION

| Term | Status | Miranda Assessment |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Projection** | **REJECTED** | Implies a leap into the future that may not be backed by current facts. It feels "speculative." |
| **Prediction** | **REJECTED** | Violates the deterministic nature of Forge. Forge doesn't predict; Forge explains. |
| **Forecast** | **CAUTION** | Permissible only if labeled as a suggestion (v1.1 Principle). |
| **Assessment** | **APPROVED** | Measures the *current* state of potential based on observable signals. It is an evaluation of facts. |

## 3. RECOMMENDED CHANGE

**Current Domain Title:**  
Talent Intelligence & Future Capacity Projection

**Proposed Domain Title (Hardened):**  
**Talent Intelligence & Future Capacity Assessment**

### Rationale:
- **Assessment** implies that Forge is looking at the **Vital Factors** (observable facts) and the **Relationship Capital** (evidence) to determine what the individual is *capable* of doing right now. 
- It removes the promise of a "guaranteed future" and focuses on the **Human Materia Prima Quality** as it exists today.

## 4. IMPACT ON EVIDENCE MODEL

Metrics previously labeled as "Projected Velocity" should be renamed to **"Observed Trajectory Potential"** or **"Capacity Rating."**

**Final Terminology Veredict:**  
The domain of Andrey shall be referred to as **"Talent Intelligence & Future Capacity Assessment."**
