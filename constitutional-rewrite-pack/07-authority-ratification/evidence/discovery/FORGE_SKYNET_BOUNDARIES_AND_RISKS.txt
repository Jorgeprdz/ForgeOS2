FORGE SKYNET BOUNDARIES AND RISKS

Status:
ARCHITECTURE DISCOVERY / NOT RATIFIED

Scope:
No implementation. No code. No engines. No schemas. No Build Tree changes.

BOUNDARY 1: SKYNET IS NOT AI

Risk:
The codename may cause people to imagine an autonomous intelligence layer.

Boundary:
Skynet is constitutional governance, not AI.

BOUNDARY 2: SKYNET IS NOT AN ENGINE

Risk:
Teams may attempt to implement Skynet as a runtime module before governance is
ratified.

Boundary:
Skynet begins as constitutional law. Implementation, if ever approved, is a
separate PAQ.

BOUNDARY 3: SKYNET IS NOT A RECOMMENDATION SYSTEM

Risk:
Skynet could become another intelligence domain.

Boundary:
Skynet does not recommend. It constrains recommendations.

BOUNDARY 4: SKYNET LAWS ARE NOT ORDINARY PRODUCT PRINCIPLES

Risk:
Too many preferences become laws.

Boundary:
Only cross-domain, high-risk, durable constraints qualify.

BOUNDARY 5: CONTEXTUAL TRUTHS ARE NOT AUTOMATIC SKYNET LAWS

Risk:
SMNYL or financial advisory context could be hardcoded as universal law.

Boundary:
Contextual truths become contextual policies unless universalized.

BOUNDARY 6: SKYNET MUST GOVERN INTERPRETATION

Risk:
Teams may constrain final recommendations but allow unsafe interpretation.

Boundary:
Unsafe interpretation contaminates downstream recommendations. Skynet must
govern both.

BOUNDARY 7: SKYNET MUST NOT BECOME BUREAUCRACY

Risk:
Every design choice becomes constitutional review.

Boundary:
Skynet governs high-risk intelligence behavior, not ordinary product work.

BOUNDARY 8: SKYNET MUST NOT FREEZE ARCHITECTURE

Risk:
Laws may prevent domain evolution.

Boundary:
Laws constrain harm. They should not prescribe implementation or domain design.

BOUNDARY 9: RATIFICATION MUST BE HUMAN

Risk:
AI or engines self-authorize governance.

Boundary:
Only explicit human constitutional review can ratify Skynet Laws.

BOUNDARY 10: LAW VIOLATION MUST HAVE CONSEQUENCE

Risk:
Laws become decorative.

Boundary:
If an intelligence conflicts with a law, the output must be blocked, degraded,
reviewed or invalidated.

RISKS

Risk 1:
Codename confusion.

Mitigation:
Always state Skynet is not AI, engine, assistant or intelligence domain.

Risk 2:
Bureaucratic sprawl.

Mitigation:
Keep active laws between 3 and 7 initially.

Risk 3:
Premature implementation.

Mitigation:
No engine, schema or runtime governance without separate approval.

Risk 4:
Context hardcoded as universal law.

Mitigation:
Separate universal laws from contextual policies.

Risk 5:
Laws become slogans.

Mitigation:
Each law must define conflict behavior.

Risk 6:
Domain teams bypass laws.

Mitigation:
Skynet Laws sit above all intelligence domains.

Risk 7:
Skynet blocks useful innovation.

Mitigation:
Write laws as boundaries, not methods.

Risk 8:
False safety.

Mitigation:
Skynet does not replace evidence contracts, ownership or human review.

RISK ANALYSIS FOR INITIAL LAW CANDIDATES

Evidence Before Judgment:

Risk:
Could slow outputs if evidence requirements are vague.

Mitigation:
Define evidence contracts and missing-evidence behavior.

Human Dignity Must Be Preserved:

Risk:
Could be too abstract.

Mitigation:
Tie to concrete bans: no moral labels, no single-worth score, no shame outputs.

Purpose Is Context, Not Pressure:

Risk:
Purpose access and manager visibility may be unclear.

Mitigation:
Define access, use and visibility boundaries.

Production Is Evidence, Not Proof:

Risk:
Commercial teams may want production-first shortcuts.

Mitigation:
Allow production as evidence, but require stage/reason/context before judgment.

No Single Score Represents Human Growth:

Risk:
Systems may still need summaries.

Mitigation:
Allow multi-factor summaries with caveats; prohibit totalizing human-growth
scores.

DO NOT DO

Do not:

- ratify laws in discovery
- create Skynet engine
- create Skynet schema
- modify Build Tree
- treat law candidates as active law
- allow contextual truth to become universal law by accident
- let Skynet review ordinary low-risk implementation details

FINAL RISK VERDICT

Skynet is architecturally valuable if it remains compact, universal,
human-ratified and focused on non-negotiable intelligence boundaries.

Skynet becomes harmful if it turns into AI, bureaucracy, implementation
micromanagement or contextual hardcoding.

Verdict:
CANDIDATE FOR LOCK AS GOVERNANCE LAYER.
