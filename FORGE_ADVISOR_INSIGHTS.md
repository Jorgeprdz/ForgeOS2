# Forge Advisor Insights

## What an Experienced Advisor Would Notice Immediately

### 1. The Quote and Policy Do Not Match by Insured

The quote is for Lariza Saenz, age 23.

The policy is for Franco Villanueva Najera, age 0.

This is the first thing to resolve. Before discussing product details, the
advisor should confirm whether the correct documents were paired.

### 2. The Issued Deductible Is Higher

Quote:

- $25,000 deductible.

Policy:

- $40,000 deductible.

Advisor implication:

The client has more first-risk responsibility in the issued policy than in the
quote.

### 3. The Issued Sum Insured Is Lower

Quote:

- $170,000,000.

Policy:

- $160,000,000.

Advisor implication:

The difference may be acceptable, but it should be acknowledged.

### 4. The Coinsurance Cap Is Lower

Quote:

- $97,000 cap.

Policy:

- $85,000 cap.

Advisor implication:

The issued policy has a lower cap, which may be favorable, but it must be
explained in context.

### 5. Protection Patrimonial Is Not Covered

The quote presentation lists Protection Patrimonial as a benefit. The issued
caratula shows Protection Patrimonial as not covered.

Advisor implication:

This is a possible expectation gap.

### 6. Foreign Protection Is Not Evident

The issued caratula shows national territoriality. No foreign optional coverage
appears active in the reviewed caratula.

Advisor implication:

The client should not believe this policy works globally.

## Risks

- Wrong quote attached to policy review.
- Client thinks the quote and caratula are the same.
- Client expects benefits described in the quote but not issued.
- Client does not understand the higher deductible.
- Client assumes accident deductible elimination means no out-of-pocket cost.
- Client assumes national policy covers foreign care.
- Client does not understand that the caratula is the contract snapshot.

## Opportunities

- Build trust by proactively explaining differences.
- Use the caratula to create a simple "what you own" review.
- Clarify what optional coverages were intentionally included or declined.
- Confirm family protection goals for a newborn/child insured.
- Offer a clean policy review before the 30-day correction window matters.
- Create a habit of quote-to-policy review for every issued GMM case.

## Follow-Up Questions a Great Advisor Would Ask

Document alignment:

- "Is this the correct quote for this issued policy?"
- "Do we have the matching quote for Franco?"
- "Was the Lariza quote included as an example or by mistake?"

Client expectation:

- "Did you expect a $40,000 deductible?"
- "Did you expect Protection Patrimonial to be active?"
- "Did you expect any international coverage?"
- "Did you choose monthly payment intentionally?"

Risk clarity:

- "Do you understand when accident deductible elimination applies?"
- "Do you know what documents to keep if there is a claim?"
- "Do you know which hospital level you selected?"

Next action:

- "Would you like a one-page summary of what the policy actually covers and
  what it does not?"
