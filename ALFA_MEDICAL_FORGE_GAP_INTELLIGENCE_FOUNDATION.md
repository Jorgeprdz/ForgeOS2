# Alfa Medical Forge Gap Intelligence Foundation

Status: DISCOVERY ONLY

Purpose: identify coverage gaps Forge should eventually recognize when
explaining Alfa Medical. No implementation is approved.

## Gap Definition

A coverage gap exists when the client expects protection but the available
evidence suggests the event, expense or context may fall outside full coverage.

Gap Intelligence should not create fear or pressure. It should create decision
clarity.

## Gap Categories

### 1. Waiting Period Gap

Pattern:

The event is medical and could be covered later, but the coverage has not yet
been active long enough.

Examples:

- Third-year waiting period conditions.
- Default 30-day illness waiting period.
- Maternity continuity requirement.
- Fetal surgery continuity requirement.
- Bariatric surgery sixth-year requirement.

Forge should say:

"This may depend on whether the required continuous coverage period has already
passed."

### 2. Preexistence Gap

Pattern:

The condition existed, was diagnosed, documented, treated or generated expenses
before policy start.

Examples:

- Prior diagnosis.
- Medical record before policy.
- Diagnostic test before policy.
- Prior expenses for same condition.
- Declared preexisting condition that is in an excluded category.

Forge should say:

"This needs preexistence review before assuming coverage."

### 3. Financial Participation Gap

Pattern:

The event may be covered, but the client may still pay a meaningful amount.

Examples:

- Deductible not exceeded.
- Coinsurance still applies.
- Coinsurance cap not reached.
- Hospital-level adjustment.
- Medication bought outside listed pharmacies.
- Reimbursement under tabulator below provider charge.

Forge should say:

"Coverage does not mean zero cost for the client."

### 4. Territory Gap

Pattern:

The event occurs outside the territory stipulated in the caratula or outside
the conditions of an optional foreign benefit.

Examples:

- Foreign treatment without Extension Abroad.
- Non-programmed foreign treatment.
- Catastrophic foreign condition that is not in the covered list.
- Continuous stay abroad beyond benefit duration.
- Student/temporary-worker status not previously evidenced.

Forge should say:

"Foreign care requires optional-benefit and residence/stay review."

### 5. Documentation Gap

Pattern:

The event might be covered, but necessary documents are missing.

Examples:

- No fiscal receipts.
- No prescription attached to medication expense.
- Missing clinical file.
- Missing diagnostic studies.
- Missing prior authorization.
- Missing notice for assisted reproduction.
- Missing proof of residence or stay abroad.

Forge should say:

"The medical fact may be valid, but the claim still needs evidence."

### 6. Authorization Gap

Pattern:

The benefit requires prior authorization, programming, second valuation or use
of assistance channel.

Examples:

- Fetal surgery.
- Genomic studies.
- ECMO.
- Gene therapies and orphan drugs.
- Knee, shoulder or spine surgery.
- Foreign programmed treatment.
- Ambulance assistance.
- Dental providers in agreement.

Forge should say:

"This benefit should not be treated as automatic. Authorization evidence is
needed."

### 7. Exclusion Gap

Pattern:

The event is medical but excluded by general or particular exclusion.

Examples:

- Cosmetic treatment.
- Preventive checkup.
- Psychiatric/psychological treatment.
- Infertility treatment outside the defined exception.
- Self-inflicted injury.
- Alcohol/drug responsibility.
- Racing or professional high-risk activity.
- Experimental treatment.

Forge should say:

"This may be a medical expense, but the policy appears to exclude this type of
event or treatment."

### 8. Optional Coverage Gap

Pattern:

The client assumes a benefit exists, but it only applies if optional coverage
was contracted and appears in the caratula.

Examples:

- HIV/AIDS coverage.
- Increased honorarium catalog.
- Extension Abroad.
- Catastrophic Diseases Abroad.
- Protection by Death.
- Students/Temporary Workers Abroad.
- Elimination of Deductible by Accident.
- Alfa Medical Cash by Diagnosis.
- Assistance Abroad.
- Dental Premium.

Forge should say:

"This is not confirmed by product name alone. The caratula must show whether
the optional coverage was contracted."

## Gap Output Principles

Forge should produce:

- Gap type.
- Why it matters.
- Required evidence.
- Safe advisor explanation.
- Human review flag where needed.

Forge must not produce:

- Pressure language.
- Fear-based persuasion.
- Absolute denial without complete evidence.
- Numeric estimate without caratula and catalogs.

## Practical Advisor Use

The best use of Gap Intelligence is before a client has a claim:

- Explain what the product solves.
- Explain what it does not solve.
- Show where optional coverage may matter.
- Prevent false expectations.

The worst use is after a claim:

- Using gaps to blame the client.
- Pretending uncertainty is certainty.
- Using exclusions as a sales scare tactic.
