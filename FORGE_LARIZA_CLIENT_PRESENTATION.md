# Forge Lariza Client Presentation

## Opening

Lariza, this is not about buying a policy because something bad will happen.

It is about deciding what you want in place if life surprises you before you
have time to prepare.

At 23, most people feel healthy. That is exactly why this conversation matters.
The best moment to protect your health financially is usually before you need
to prove that you need it.

## The Problem This Solves

A serious medical event can create two problems at the same time:

1. The health problem itself.
2. The financial pressure around getting treated.

The goal of this proposal is to keep those two problems from becoming one
bigger crisis.

If there is an accident, surgery, hospitalization or serious illness, this plan
is designed to help absorb the large medical cost so the decision is not only:

"Can I afford to get treated?"

## What This Gives You

This proposal gives you a national medical protection plan.

In simple terms:

- You have access to a large protection amount for covered major medical
  events.
- You have a defined first amount you would pay before the plan begins to help.
- After that, you share a percentage of covered expenses, with a limit shown in
  the quote.
- If the event is a covered accident, you added protection that can eliminate
  that first amount under the policy rules.

The purpose is not to remove every possible cost.

The purpose is to prevent a major medical event from becoming financially
unmanageable.

## Why This Matters at 23

At your age, the biggest advantage is not that you expect to use it tomorrow.

The advantage is that you can make the decision while you are healthy, before a
diagnosis or accident changes the conversation.

You are buying options for your future self:

- The option to choose care with less financial panic.
- The option to protect your savings.
- The option to not depend completely on family or debt.
- The option to keep a health event from interrupting your independence.

## What You Would Still Pay

This is not a zero-cost medical card.

If something covered happens, you may still participate in the cost.

Your quote shows:

- A first amount of $25,000.
- Then a 10% participation in covered expenses.
- A limit to that participation of $97,000.

Said simply:

You keep part of the responsibility, but you are not facing the whole medical
bill alone.

## The Accident Upgrade

You added accident deductible elimination.

Plain language:

If you have a covered accident and the accident meets the policy conditions,
that first $25,000 may be removed.

This matters because accidents are often the kind of event nobody schedules,
nobody budgets and nobody sees coming.

## What This Does Not Mean

This does not mean:

- Everything medical is paid.
- Every hospital or provider cost works the same.
- Foreign care is automatically included.
- Optional coverages listed in the brochure are all active.
- The quote is already the final contract.

Before buying, we should confirm what you want active and what you are
intentionally leaving out.

## The Real Decision

The real decision is not whether insurance is interesting.

The real decision is:

"If a serious medical event happened, do I want to carry that financial risk
myself, or do I want to transfer most of it while I am young and healthy?"

That is the decision this proposal puts in front of you.

## Next Conversation

Before deciding, we should talk through:

- Where you would want to be treated.
- Whether national coverage fits your life.
- Whether $25,000 feels comfortable as your first responsibility.
- Whether you travel enough to consider foreign coverage.
- Whether maternity benefits matter to your future plans.
- Whether you prefer a lower-cost plan or stronger comfort in a claim.
