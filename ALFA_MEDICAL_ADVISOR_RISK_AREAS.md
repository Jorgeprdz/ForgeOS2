# Alfa Medical Advisor Risk Areas

Status: DISCOVERY ONLY

This document identifies areas where advisors are likely to overstate,
misunderstand or under-explain Alfa Medical.

## 1. Confusing Alfa Medical with Alfa Medical Flex

Risk:

Using Flex language such as franquicia, copago or participacion for Alfa
Medical, or using Alfa Medical deductible/coinsurance language for Flex.

Correct framing:

Alfa Medical uses deducible, coaseguro and tope de coaseguro. Flex is a sibling
product with different mechanics.

## 2. Explaining Deductible Too Simply

Risk:

Saying "the deductible is what you pay first" without explaining siniestro,
unico, anual, reinstalable, basico and exceso behavior.

Why it matters:

The client's responsibility can change for complements, renewals, age rules and
deductible type.

## 3. Explaining Coinsurance Without Cap Exceptions

Risk:

Saying "coinsurance is capped" as if every scenario respects the same cap.

Why it matters:

Hospital-level adjustment, medication purchase path and foreign coverage can
change cap behavior.

## 4. Hospital Level Overconfidence

Risk:

Telling clients they can go to any hospital without explaining hospital-level
financial consequences.

Why it matters:

Alfa Medical has Practico, Integro and Pleno levels, and hospital-level
selection can add or reduce coinsurance. Some cases can remove the cap.

## 5. Waiting Period Blind Spots

Risk:

Not flagging third-year waiting period conditions, 30-day default illness
waiting period, 10-month maternity continuity, 24-month fetal surgery
continuity or sixth-year bariatric surgery conditions.

Why it matters:

The client may believe the policy is active, but the specific illness or
benefit may not yet be eligible.

## 6. Preexistence Overstatement

Risk:

Either saying preexisting conditions are always excluded or implying they become
covered after time.

Why it matters:

Declared preexistence can be covered only under strict requirements and excludes
several serious categories.

## 7. Maternity Simplification

Risk:

Presenting maternity as a simple benefit instead of a set of age, continuity,
year, territory, deductible and documentation rules.

Why it matters:

Maternity years 1 to 3 behave differently from year 4 onward. Reproduction
assisted maternity is much more restrictive.

## 8. Accident Benefit Overclaim

Risk:

Assuming accidents always eliminate or reduce deductible.

Why it matters:

Basic coverage includes reduction of deductible by accident under conditions.
Elimination of deductible by accident is optional. Both have requirements and
exclusions.

## 9. Foreign Coverage Ambiguity

Risk:

Saying "it covers abroad" without distinguishing optional Extension Abroad,
Catastrophic Diseases Abroad, Students/Temporary Workers Abroad and Assistance
Abroad.

Why it matters:

Foreign coverage can have different deductibles, coinsurance, caps, daily
limits, programming requirements and maximum stay conditions.

## 10. High-Specialty Treatment Risk

Risk:

Not requiring prior authorization or second valuation for treatments such as
fetal surgery, gene therapies, orphan drugs, ECMO, knee/shoulder/spine surgery,
genomic studies and certain specialized equipment.

Why it matters:

These benefits can be denied or limited if authorization and documentation are
missing.

## 11. Medication Path Risk

Risk:

Not telling the client that pharmacy selection can change coinsurance.

Why it matters:

Listed pharmacies and non-listed national pharmacies can produce different
client responsibility.

## 12. Product Accuracy vs Sales Simplicity

Risk:

Trying to make the product feel simple by removing the conditions that actually
protect the advisor and client from misunderstanding.

Better advisor behavior:

Explain the product in human terms, then name the coverage gates clearly:
event, waiting period, exclusion, territory, documentation, deductible,
coinsurance, hospital level and policy caratula.

## Advisor Safe Explanation Pattern

Use:

"This is designed to protect you from major covered medical expenses. The exact
result depends on the event, when it happens, where it happens, the hospital,
your deductible, coinsurance, cap and the exclusions. My job is to help you see
both the protection and the conditions."

Avoid:

"Everything major is covered."
