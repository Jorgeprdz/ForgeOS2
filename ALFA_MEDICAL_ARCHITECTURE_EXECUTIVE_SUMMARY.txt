1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The completed Alfa Medical Rule Inventory can support a Coverage Evaluation
Architecture, but it is not ready for engine implementation.

Forge must first learn how to think:

- confirm exact product
- confirm policy/caratula context
- identify insured
- classify event family
- check minimum evidence
- evaluate coverage candidate
- screen exclusions
- screen waiting periods
- screen preexistence
- check territory and optional coverage
- check authorization and documentation
- trigger human review when needed
- separate coverage eligibility from financial participation
- choose cautious assessment language

The key architecture finding:

Forge should not answer "covered or not covered" first.

Forge should first answer:

"Do we have enough evidence to evaluate the correct rule path?"

3. MINIMUM UNIVERSAL DATASET

Before case-specific assessment, Forge needs:

- exact product
- policy/caratula availability
- policy active status
- insured identity
- event family
- diagnosis or medically clear event description
- key date sequence
- territory/location
- optional coverage relevance
- preexistence known/unknown

If these are missing, Forge should provide educational explanation or ask the
next smallest question.

4. EVENT FAMILY MODEL

Forge should classify Alfa Medical situations into event families:

- Accident
- Illness
- Maternity
- Pregnancy Complication
- Newborn
- Assisted Reproduction Maternity
- Foreign Care
- Catastrophic Disease Abroad
- Dental
- Medication
- High-Specialty Treatment
- Hospitalization / Surgery
- Assistance Service

5. HUMAN REVIEW GATES

Mandatory review gates include:

- product uncertainty
- missing caratula for case-specific result
- preexistence ambiguity
- conflicting symptom/diagnosis/expense dates
- foreign coverage without optional proof
- programmed vs non-programmed foreign care ambiguity
- high-specialty treatment
- alcohol/drug/intentional conduct facts
- professional sport/racing/dangerous activity ambiguity
- hospital/provider classification unknown
- current catalogs unavailable
- maternity dates or assisted reproduction status unclear
- missing claim documents
- source conflicts

6. CONFIDENCE BOUNDARIES

Forge may say:

- Educational Explanation Only
- Insufficient Information
- Human Review Required
- Conditional Assessment
- Likely Covered
- Likely Not Covered

Forge must not say:

- Approved
- Denied
- Guaranteed
- The insurer will pay
- The insurer will not pay

7. EXAMPLE RESULT

Question:

"My wife has had Alfa Medical for 4 years. She is pregnant. She is 33 years
old. Would childbirth be covered?"

Forge reasoning:

- Product routes to Alfa Medical.
- Event family is Maternity.
- Age 33 fits basic maternity age range.
- Four years suggests year 4+ maternity path if continuous coverage is
  confirmed.
- Maternity requires at least 10 months continuous coverage at birth.
- Still missing: caratula, active policy status, expected birth date, territory,
  maternity benefit, deductible type, assisted reproduction status and
  documentation.

Proper answer:

Conditional assessment only. It may be eligible under year 4+ maternity rules
if continuity, caratula, territory, deductible type, assisted reproduction status
and documentation check out. Forge must not say simply "yes, covered."
