# Alfa Medical Real-Life Event Library

Status: DISCOVERY ONLY

This library evaluates common real-life situations. It is not a claims ruling.
Each scenario must be resolved against the actual policy caratula,
documentation, waiting periods, exclusions and medical evidence.

## Scenario Reading Key

- Likely covered: the event appears inside a covered class, subject to normal
  conditions.
- Conditional: coverage depends on specific facts.
- Likely not covered: the event appears excluded or outside stated coverage.
- Unknown: source reviewed is insufficient without caratula or facts.

## 1. Appendicitis

Discovery status: Likely covered from day one if acute and not preexisting.

Why:

The Condiciones Generales list acute appendicitis among events covered from the
first day of vigency, provided it is not preexisting.

Client responsibility:

- Deductible and coinsurance unless a special rule says otherwise.
- Tabulator/usual/reasonable limits.
- Documentation and medical necessity.

Forge must know:

- Start date of symptoms.
- Policy start date.
- Whether condition is preexisting.
- Hospital and territory.

## 2. Broken Leg

Discovery status: Conditional, generally accident pathway if external, sudden,
fortuitous and violent.

Why:

Accidents are covered from day one if they meet the accident definition and are
not excluded. If first medical expense happens more than 30 days after the
accident, the event may not be considered accident and may be treated as
illness.

Client responsibility:

- Deductible and coinsurance.
- Possible accident deductible reduction if all conditions apply.
- Exclusions if the activity was excluded or professional/high-risk context.

Forge must know:

- Accident date.
- First medical expense date.
- Cause and activity.
- Whether the activity was professional or excluded.

## 3. Cancer Diagnosis

Discovery status: Conditional.

Why:

Cancer treatment can be covered when the underlying illness is covered and
waiting periods apply. Diseases in mammary glands, including breast cancer, are
listed with a third-year waiting period. Catastrophic foreign cancer coverage is
available under optional foreign benefit conditions. Certain cancer types are
excluded from catastrophic foreign classification.

Client responsibility:

- Deductible and coinsurance.
- Waiting-period risk.
- Tabulator/usual/reasonable limits.
- Optional coverage requirements for foreign care.

Forge must know:

- Cancer type.
- Diagnosis date and first symptoms.
- Policy start/continuity.
- Territory.
- Optional coverages.

## 4. Pregnancy / Maternity

Discovery status: Conditional.

Why:

Maternity applies only to women who become pregnant within the stated age range
and have at least 10 months of continuous coverage at the time of birth. The
benefit differs for years 1 to 3 and year 4 onward. Maternity does not accept
period-waiting reduction/elimination for that benefit.

Client responsibility:

- Depends on policy year and benefit selected.
- In early years the maternity benefit may apply without deductible and
  coinsurance for the stated benefit.
- From year 4 onward deductible and coinsurance can apply.
- Deducible en Exceso may exclude the maternity benefit.

Forge must know:

- Mother age.
- Pregnancy and birth dates.
- Coverage continuity.
- Maternity benefit sum insured.
- Deductible type.

## 5. Heart Attack

Discovery status: Conditional.

Why:

Acute myocardial infarction without coronary disease is listed among conditions
covered from day one if not preexisting. Other cardiac scenarios require fact
review and may depend on preexistence, waiting period or covered illness
classification.

Client responsibility:

- Deductible.
- Coinsurance.
- Hospital level and tabulator rules.

Forge must know:

- Diagnosis.
- Whether coronary disease existed.
- First symptoms.
- Preexisting evidence.

## 6. Motorcycle Accident

Discovery status: Conditional.

Why:

The accident may be covered if it meets the accident definition and is not
excluded. Racing, speed, safety/resistance contests and certain professional or
dangerous activity contexts may be excluded or condition-heavy.

Client responsibility:

- Deductible and coinsurance.
- Possible accident deductible reduction if first attention and Mexico
  conditions are met.
- No coverage if excluded racing/contest context applies.

Forge must know:

- Was it ordinary transit or racing/contest?
- Alcohol/drug involvement?
- First attention timing?
- Territory?

## 7. Skiing Accident

Discovery status: Conditional.

Why:

Sports or dangerous activities can be covered when not practiced professionally.
Territory and foreign optional coverages matter if the accident occurs abroad.

Client responsibility:

- Deductible and coinsurance.
- Foreign-care deductibles/coinsurance/caps if abroad and optional coverage
  exists.
- Possible exclusion if professional or excluded event context.

Forge must know:

- Country/territory.
- Amateur vs professional practice.
- Optional foreign coverage.
- Accident timing and first expense.

## 8. Chronic Disease

Discovery status: Conditional / high risk.

Why:

Chronic conditions may be covered only if not preexisting and not excluded, but
declared preexisting coverage excludes several chronic-degenerative categories.

Client responsibility:

- Deductible, coinsurance and ongoing complement rules.
- Annual/reinstalable deductible implications.
- Potential denial if preexisting or excluded.

Forge must know:

- Diagnosis history.
- First symptoms/gastos.
- Declaration at application.
- Whether it belongs to excluded preexisting categories.

## 9. Emergency Surgery

Discovery status: Conditional.

Why:

Emergency surgery can be covered if caused by a covered accident or illness and
not excluded. Urgency can affect hospital-level coinsurance adjustment.

Client responsibility:

- Deductible and coinsurance.
- Tabulator/usual/reasonable limits.
- Documentation.

Forge must know:

- Cause of surgery.
- Urgency evidence.
- Hospital.
- Whether condition has waiting period.

## 10. Hospitalization Abroad

Discovery status: Conditional / optional.

Why:

Foreign coverage depends on optional coverages such as Extension Abroad,
Catastrophic Diseases Abroad, Students/Temporary Workers Abroad or Assistance
Abroad. Programmed vs non-programmed care changes financial terms.

Client responsibility:

- Foreign deductible.
- Foreign coinsurance.
- Foreign cap or no-cap depending on benefit.
- Reimbursement constraints.
- Room/ICU daily limits.
- Potential loss of benefit after continuous stay limits.

Forge must know:

- Which optional foreign benefit is contracted.
- Whether event is urgency, catastrophic or ordinary covered event.
- Whether treatment was programmed.
- Continuous time abroad.
- Residence evidence.
