The foundation is conceptually sufficient to support the next intelligence branches without changing the foundation, provided one constitutional boundary is treated as locked:

Forge Core is universal.
Rule Packs interpret commercial events.
SMNYL Agency 2026 is the first validated Rule Pack.
SMNYL Agency 2026 is not Forge Core.

3. Operational documents may mix rule, metric, process and coaching.

### Risk
Agency files such as Planeación Trimestral Partners, One Thing, P200 and Precont can contain rules, activities, goals, process guidance, coaching language and recognition criteria in the same artifact.

### Assessment
Important. Evidence & Provenance Foundation and Rule Snapshot Hardening are sufficient to handle this, but Rule Pack construction must classify each extracted item correctly.


4. Recruitment and productivity language may be carrier/channel-specific.

### Risk
Recruitment, P200, RTW, PM, Precont and planning materials may define activity expectations or productivity KPIs that should not become universal Core rules.

### Assessment
Important. The foundation handles this through Rule Packs, Metrics Ownership and Commercial Events. No foundation change required.


5. Multi-period interpretation risk.

### Risk
Quarterly planning, monthly productivity, campaign activity, recruitment cycles, precontract windows, contest periods and compensation payment periods can overlap.

### Assessment
Important. Periods & Operational Clocks Hardening is sufficient. No foundation change required.


6. Historical explanation depends on Rule Pack preservation.

### Risk
In 2032, Forge must explain why an advisor received or did not receive a 2026 SMNYL Agency bonus.

### Assessment
Important. RuleSnapshot Hardening and Evidence & Provenance Foundation are sufficient if Rule Pack snapshots preserve source documents and interpreted rule content.


7. Advisor Experience may surface Rule Pack-specific metrics.

### Risk
Benvenù, Clippy or Command OS could show carrier-specific metrics as if they are universal.

### Assessment
Important but controlled. Advisor Experience constraints already state that it consumes official owner snapshots and must not own business metrics.


8. Economic Motivation risk.

### Risk
Economic Motivation can overstate money if it consumes forecasts or unofficial Rule Pack interpretations.

### Assessment
Important. Metrics Ownership and Economic Motivation Guardrails are sufficient if enforced.


9. Event naming risk.

### Risk
Rule Pack-specific events may be confused with Core universal events.

### Assessment
Important. Commercial Events Taxonomy resolves the conceptual issue. Core events remain universal. Rule Pack outputs must be interpreted events or decision events scoped to the active Rule Pack.


10. Manager and Partner ambiguity.

### Risk
Partner Intelligence and Manager Intelligence may need different meanings across carriers and channels.

### Assessment
Important. Identity & Attribution Hardening is sufficient because it separates role, assignment, attribution and servicing.

### Consistency Review

Commercial Events vs Metrics

Pass.

Events record what happened or what was interpreted.
Metrics are values owned by domains or Rule Packs.
A metric must not pretend to be an event.
An event must not pretend to be a metric.


Commercial Events vs Rule Packs

Pass.

Production Events are universal.
Rule Packs interpret Production Events.
Interpretation Events can be scoped to a Rule Pack.


Metrics vs Rule Packs

Pass.

Core owns metric contracts and ownership discipline.
Rule Packs define carrier/channel-specific metrics.

### Example

Core may support ruled contest metrics.
SMNYL Agency 2026 may define Prima Meta.


Periods vs Rule Packs

Pass.

Core supports universal clocks and PeriodSnapshots.
Rule Packs define which periods matter for specific interpretation.

### Example

Core supports Evaluation Time.
SMNYL Agency 2026 may define semester evaluation for a specific program.


Evidence vs Rule Packs

Pass.

Rule Packs can be built from official company documents. Evidence and Provenance preserve document source, extraction path, confidence and correction history.


RuleSnapshot vs Rule Packs

Pass.

RuleSnapshot must include carrier, distribution channel, rule pack, rule pack version, rule family, source document and effective dates.


Foundation vs operational agency artifacts

Pass.

The operational agency artifacts do not break the foundation because they are not Core.

They are source material for Rule Pack construction, evidence, operational events, planning contexts and interpretation rules.
