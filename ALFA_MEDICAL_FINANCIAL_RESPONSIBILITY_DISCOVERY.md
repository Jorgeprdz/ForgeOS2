# Alfa Medical Financial Responsibility Discovery

Status: DISCOVERY ONLY

This document identifies what expenses may remain with the client under Alfa
Medical. It does not authorize a calculation engine.

## Core Client Responsibility Model

The client may remain responsible for:

- Deductible.
- Coinsurance.
- Coinsurance above or outside the cap when special conditions apply.
- Hospital-level adjustment penalties.
- Medication coinsurance.
- Expenses below deductible.
- Expenses not procedent.
- Expenses above policy limits.
- Expenses above tabulator or usual/reasonable values.
- Excluded treatments.
- Expenses without required documentation.
- Non-authorized services where authorization is required.
- Foreign-care penalties or limits.
- Optional benefits not contracted.
- Provider or service costs outside covered rules.

## Deductible

Alfa Medical defines deductible as the initial amount the insured must pay for
each covered illness or accident, identified in the caratula.

Practical meaning:

If the covered medical expense does not exceed the deductible, the client may
pay the whole event. The policy begins to participate only once procedent
expenses exceed the applicable deductible.

Important variants:

- Deducible Unico: applies one time at the start of each covered accident or
  illness.
- Deducible Anual: applies annually for complements of a covered claim after
  renewal, under stated conditions.
- Deducible Reinstalable: reinstates at each renewal for each covered accident
  or illness and its complements.
- Deducible Basico / Deducible en Exceso: creates different treatment for
  certain clauses.

## Coinsurance

Coaseguro is the percentage identified in the caratula that the insured pays
from covered medical expenses after deductible.

Practical meaning:

Even after the deductible is met, the client does not automatically stop paying.
The client participates in the covered claim through coinsurance until the
applicable cap is reached, unless a special rule changes the behavior.

## Coinsurance Cap

Tope de Coaseguro is the maximum participation by the insured for coinsurance
for each covered claim.

Practical meaning:

The cap may limit the client's coinsurance exposure, but it is not universal.
Some special cases can remove, modify or create separate caps, including
hospital-level adjustments, medication purchase outside listed pharmacies and
foreign care.

## Hospital-Level Adjustment

Alfa Medical has hospital levels Practico, Integro and Pleno.

When the insured chooses a hospital level different from the contracted level,
the coinsurance can be adjusted. Some upward-level hospital choices add
percentage points and may remove the coinsurance cap. Lower-level choices may
reduce the coinsurance under specific rules.

Practical meaning:

Hospital choice can change what the client pays. "I can go anywhere" is not the
same as "the financial participation is the same everywhere."

## Medication Responsibility

Medications are covered when prescribed and tied to a covered claim, but
pharmacy choice matters.

If medications are bought at listed pharmacies, the policy applies the stated
medication coinsurance and cap behavior. If bought at other national
pharmacies, coinsurance may be higher and without cap.

Practical meaning:

The client may pay more simply because the medication was purchased through a
different pharmacy path.

## Tabulator and Usual/Reasonable Limits

Medical fees and non-fee medical expenses are not paid by "whatever the
provider charged" as a simple rule.

Alfa Medical references:

- Catalogo de Honorarios Medicos y Quirurgicos.
- Tabulador.
- Gasto Usual, Razonable y Acostumbrado.
- Plan and zone contracted.

Practical meaning:

If the provider charges above the applicable catalog/usual/reasonable value,
the difference may remain with the client.

## Practical Examples

These examples are conceptual. They intentionally do not calculate amounts.

### Example 1: Hospital bill below deductible

Event: covered illness with procedent medical expenses below the deductible.

Likely client responsibility:

- Client pays the procedent expenses because the deductible has not been
  exceeded.
- Insurer payment may not begin.

### Example 2: Covered surgery above deductible

Event: covered surgery after waiting periods.

Likely client responsibility:

- Deductible.
- Coinsurance after deductible.
- Any amount above tabulator/usual/reasonable values.
- Any non-procedent or excluded charge.
- Potential hospital-level adjustment if hospital choice differs from plan.

### Example 3: Accident in Mexico with first attention in 30 days

Event: covered accident, first attention within the required period, expenses
exceed the policy threshold for accident deductible reduction.

Likely client responsibility:

- Reduced deductible if all conditions apply and no optional elimination
  conflict exists.
- Coinsurance on excess expenses.
- Non-covered or non-procedent expenses.

### Example 4: Medication bought outside listed pharmacy

Event: covered illness, prescribed medication purchased outside listed national
pharmacy.

Likely client responsibility:

- Higher medication coinsurance.
- Potential no-cap exposure on medication coinsurance.

### Example 5: Foreign treatment not programmed

Event: covered illness abroad under optional foreign extension but not
programmed before the insurer.

Likely client responsibility:

- Modified deductible/coinsurance rules.
- Reimbursement pathway.
- Foreign cap or no-cap rules depending on benefit.
- Possible honorarium and room/ICU daily limits.

## Forge Financial Responsibility Boundary

Forge must not estimate client out-of-pocket unless it has:

- Product exact: Alfa Medical.
- Policy version.
- Insured identity and eligibility.
- Event type and event date.
- First symptom/first expense date.
- Territory.
- Plan, zone, hospital level and hospital selected.
- Sum insured.
- Deductible model and amount.
- Coinsurance model and percentage.
- Coinsurance cap.
- Medication path if applicable.
- Optional coverages contracted.
- Tabulator/catalog values.
- Documentation and authorization status.

If any value is missing, Forge should say what is missing instead of inventing a
number.
