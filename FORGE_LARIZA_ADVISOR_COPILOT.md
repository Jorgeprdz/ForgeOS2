# Forge Lariza Advisor Copilot

## If Pedro Camarena Reviewed This Case

Pedro would not start with the deductible.

He would start with risk.

He would likely ask:

"Lariza, which medical risk could interrupt your life the most: an accident,
a serious illness, a hospitalization or needing treatment when you least expect
it?"

Then he would guide the conversation from risk recognition to risk transfer.

## What Pedro Would Notice

### 1. She Is Young and Normal Risk

At 23, the emotional barrier may be:

"I am healthy. Why now?"

Pedro would reframe:

"That is exactly why now is the best moment to decide. Insurance is easier to
buy before life gives you a reason to need it."

### 2. The Proposal Has Strong Catastrophic Protection

The quote shows a large protection amount.

Pedro would not lead with the number. He would translate it:

"This is not for a small consultation. This is for the kind of bill that could
change your finances."

### 3. Accident Deductible Elimination Is Emotionally Relevant

At Lariza's age, accident risk may feel more real than illness risk.

Pedro would use that bridge:

"Maybe you do not imagine a serious illness today, but can you imagine an
accident changing your week in one second?"

### 4. National Coverage Needs Lifestyle Review

Pedro would ask:

"Do you mostly live, work and travel in Mexico, or do you spend meaningful time
outside the country?"

If she travels often, the quote's national territoriality becomes an important
conversation.

## What Pedro Would Emphasize

- You cannot eliminate medical risk.
- You can reduce some risks with habits.
- But the financial consequence of a major medical event is usually something
  you transfer.
- The younger and healthier you are, the cleaner the decision usually is.
- The decision is not about fear. It is about responsibility.

## Questions Pedro Would Ask Lariza

Risk recognition:

- "What would worry you more: the illness itself or not knowing how to pay for
  the treatment?"
- "If tomorrow you needed surgery, who would be financially affected besides
  you?"
- "Do you have savings you would be comfortable using for a hospital bill?"

Lifestyle:

- "Do you travel outside Mexico?"
- "What hospitals would make you feel comfortable?"
- "Do you depend financially on yourself, your parents or both?"

Future:

- "Do you see maternity as something relevant in the next few years?"
- "Do you want this protection only for emergencies, or as part of a long-term
  health plan?"

Decision:

- "Would you rather pay less and carry more of the first risk, or pay more and
  feel more protected if something happens?"

## Emotional Drivers That Might Matter

- Independence.
- Protecting parents from emergency expenses.
- Peace of mind.
- Staying in control.
- Future family planning.
- Not delaying treatment because of money.
- Avoiding debt.
- Taking advantage of being young and healthy.

## Advisor Warning

Do not make Lariza feel she is being sold a product.

Make her feel she is being helped to decide what risk she wants to keep and
what risk she wants to transfer.
