## Task 4: Decision Clarity Review

### What protection was truly purchased?

The issued policy purchased a national Alfa Medical Integro medical-expense
protection for Franco Villanueva Najera.

In plain terms:

The family bought help for large covered medical bills in Mexico, with a large
coverage limit, a $40,000 first part paid by the client, and then a 10% share of
covered expenses up to the policy cap, subject to the policy rules.

They also bought extra protection for accidents: if a covered accident meets
the rules, the accident deductible can be eliminated.

### What situations would create out-of-pocket costs?

The family may still pay when:

- The event is below the deductible.
- The event is covered but coinsurance applies.
- A hospital or provider creates costs above what the policy recognizes.
- The event is excluded.
- The event happens outside national territory without the right optional
  coverage.
- The claim lacks documents.
- A benefit has a waiting period or special condition.
- The selected hospital/provider changes cost participation.

### What situations deserve special attention?

Special attention:

- Any accident: because CEDA may help, but only if the accident qualifies.
- Any treatment abroad: this policy is national.
- Any expensive hospital event: hospital level and tabulator matter.
- Any newborn/child condition: dates and policy conditions matter.
- Any condition suspected to have started before the policy.
- Any optional benefit the client thinks they bought but the caratula does not
  show.

### What misconceptions might the client have?

Possible misconceptions:

- "The quote is the policy."
- "Everything in the quote brochure is included."
- "Protection Patrimonial is active because it appears in the quote."
- "National coverage works abroad."
- "Accident deductible elimination means I pay nothing in any accident."
- "The insurer pays from peso one."
- "The $160,000,000 sum insured means every provider charge is fully paid."

## Forge Case Verdict

This is a strong case for advisor follow-up because the quote and policy do not
appear to be the same insured. The caratula should be treated as the source of
truth for the issued policy, and the advisor should confirm whether the quote
was included only as a sample, an earlier proposal, or the wrong file.
