# PAQ-12.x.y First Wow Moment Discovery

Status: ARCHITECTURE DISCOVERY / NOT IMPLEMENTED

Scope:

- Documentation only.
- No implementation.
- No code.
- No UI.
- No schemas.
- No engines.
- No build tree changes.

## 1. Objective

Determine what the first "wow" experience of Forge should be after Benvenù Act
1: Leopard Experience and before The Future You Conversation.

The First Wow Moment must prove that Forge is not another CRM without asking the
advisor to work for the system.

## 2. Experience Position

```text
Benvenù Experience
├── Act 1: Leopard Experience
├── Act 2: First Wow Moment
├── Act 3: First Value Opportunity
├── Act 4: The Future You Conversation
├── Act 5: Purpose Snapshot
├── Act 6: Alfred / Universal Command Bar Introduction
└── Act 7: Progressive Discovery
```

Leopard Experience sets the emotional frame.

First Wow Moment proves the frame with a small, concrete, explainable signal.

The Future You Conversation then asks the advisor what they want to build.

## 3. What Makes an Advisor Think "This Is Not Another CRM"?

The advisor thinks Forge is not another CRM when Forge does not begin by asking
for data and instead shows useful interpretation from context.

The difference is:

- CRM: "Capture your clients."
- Forge: "I found something worth your attention."

- CRM: "Complete your profile."
- Forge: "Here is one relationship, opportunity or signal we can act on."

- CRM: "Organize your database."
- Forge: "This is the next useful move."

The First Wow Moment should show that Forge understands work as decisions, not
records.

## 4. Smallest Amount of Information Required

The smallest acceptable information set is:

- Advisor identity or session context.
- One trusted source of existing context.
- One interpretable signal.
- Confidence level or evidence label.

The source may be:

- Existing portfolio/policy data.
- Imported prospect/client context.
- Recent activity.
- Calendar context.
- Existing relationship memory.
- Manager-provided context.
- Demo seed context for first-run prototype only, clearly labeled.

If no trusted context exists, First Wow should not fake intelligence. It should
show a zero-data version that explains what Forge can do without pretending it
already knows.

## 5. What Forge Can Infer Instead of Asking

Forge should infer from reliable context:

- Whether there is an existing relationship worth reviewing.
- Whether a follow-up appears overdue.
- Whether a policy or renewal may deserve attention.
- Whether a prospect has a next-action gap.
- Whether recent activity suggests momentum or friction.
- Whether an imported document or record contains a useful signal.
- Whether the advisor is starting with no context and needs a lighter path.

Forge should not ask for:

- Full profile setup.
- Personal purpose.
- Sales goals.
- Revenue targets.
- Motivation drivers.
- Activity commitments.
- Compensation expectations.
- Career plan details.

Those may belong later, and only with consent and context.

## 6. Immediate Value in Less Than 30 Seconds

The First Wow Moment should deliver one of these outcomes in less than 30
seconds:

- One next action worth doing now.
- One relationship worth reviewing.
- One hidden opportunity from existing context.
- One overdue follow-up with reason.
- One policy or renewal signal with evidence.
- One "Forge already found this" moment.

It should not require navigation, setup or field completion.

The ideal moment is one sentence plus one explainable signal:

"Forge found one relationship that may deserve attention today."

Then:

"Reason: recent activity exists, but no clear next action is recorded."

## 7. What Happens Between Leopard and Future You

Leopard says:

"Forge is different."

First Wow proves:

"Forge can notice something useful without making you fill a form."

Future You then asks:

"Now that you have seen Forge work, what do you want to build?"

The bridge should feel like:

1. Emotional opening.
2. Small proof.
3. Human conversation.

It should not feel like:

1. Welcome video.
2. Product tour.
3. Setup form.

## 8. Experiences to Reject

Reject any candidate that:

- Starts with profile setup.
- Starts with required fields.
- Starts with a dashboard.
- Starts with a tutorial.
- Starts with a checklist.
- Starts with a sales target.
- Starts with a motivational question.
- Shows too many metrics.
- Makes claims without evidence.
- Pretends to know what it cannot know.
- Uses emotional pressure.
- Looks like SaaS marketing.
- Feels like CRM onboarding.

## 9. Magical but Explainable

The First Wow should feel magical because it is immediate, not because it is
opaque.

Magical:

"Forge found a client you may want to contact today."

Explainable:

"Because there is a recent policy event and no follow-up recorded."

Magical:

"Forge found one relationship with referral potential."

Explainable:

"Because the relationship has strong engagement and a recent life-context
signal."

Magical:

"Forge can start with what already exists."

Explainable:

"This comes from your imported records. No manual setup required."

## 10. Trust Instead of Hype

Trust comes from restraint.

Forge should:

- Show one signal, not ten.
- Explain why the signal appears.
- Label confidence.
- Admit missing context.
- Avoid income promises.
- Avoid generic inspiration.
- Avoid saying it can transform the advisor's career.
- Let the advisor continue or skip.

Forge should sound like a calm operating system, not a motivational speaker.

## 11. What Forge Must Never Do

Forge must never:

- Invent client facts.
- Invent financial values.
- Invent goals.
- Invent motivation.
- Show a forecast before context.
- Show compensation potential before evidence and rules.
- Score productivity in the first moment.
- Diagnose behavior in the first moment.
- Ask for a full profile.
- Ask "what is your why?" as the first interaction.
- Pressure the advisor emotionally.
- Use Purpose Snapshot before it exists.
- Share the moment with a manager as performance evidence.

## 12. Candidate Experiences to Consider

Recommended candidates:

1. One Relationship Worth Reviewing
2. One Next Action Found
3. One Overdue Follow-Up Signal
4. One Policy/Renewal Signal
5. One Opportunity From Existing Context
6. Zero-Data Honest First Wow
7. Imported Context Comes Alive
8. Tomorrow's Agenda Preview

Each candidate should be evaluated by:

- Does it require no new data?
- Can it be explained?
- Does it create value in less than 30 seconds?
- Does it avoid CRM behavior?
- Does it build trust?
- Can it transition naturally into Future You Conversation?

## 13. Recommended Direction

Primary recommendation:

One Relationship Worth Reviewing.

Reason:

It is human, advisor-first and action-oriented. It proves that Forge exists to
help the advisor decide who to contact, why and what to do next.

Fallback recommendation:

Zero-Data Honest First Wow.

Reason:

If Forge has no reliable context, honesty builds more trust than fake
personalization.

## 14. Open Architecture Questions

- What trusted context is guaranteed available before Benvenù?
- Is there an existing demo seed state for new users, and how is it labeled?
- Which domain owns the first explainable signal?
- What confidence label vocabulary should Advisor Experience use?
- Can First Wow consume Relationship Intelligence without duplicating it?
- Can First Wow consume Policy & Sales Operations without becoming operations?
- How should skip/continue be recorded without creating behavioral judgment?

## 15. Final Verdict

First Wow Moment should be a small, explainable, context-aware proof that Forge
works for the advisor before asking the advisor to work for Forge.

It should not be a product tour.

It should not be a dashboard.

It should not be a form.

Implementation remains not approved.
