1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

What we discovered:

Recruitment Intelligence does not begin at the name.

The official training material shows that before a name appears there is a deeper commercial layer:

Relationships.
Communities.
Groups.
Market Natural.
Centers of Influence.
Referrals.
Trust.
Social access.
Professional access.
People who know people.

The root discovery is this:

Recruitment consumes relationship access.
Sales prospecting consumes relationship access.
Referral generation consumes relationship access.
Productivity depends partly on the advisor's ability to activate relationship access.
Partner growth depends partly on the Partner's ability to create advisors who can activate relationship access.

The problem this PAQ is solving:

PAQ-02 made Recruitment Intelligence an ARCHITECTURE CANDIDATE, but the ES training documents showed that the model still began too late.

If Forge models recruitment as:

Name
Prospect
Candidate

then Forge misses the real upstream asset:

Relationship Capital.

Why this PAQ exists:

Forge needs to prevent Recruitment Intelligence from incorrectly owning everything upstream.

Recruitment should not own all relationships, all communities, all Market Natural, all centers of influence, all referrals, all trust, or all social graph.

Recruitment should consume, activate and interpret part of that capital for recruiting purposes.

The deeper layer must be clarified before Recruitment Intelligence becomes ARCHITECTURE LOCK.

Core conclusion:

Relationship Capital is real.
Career Capital is real.
Recruitment consumes Relationship Capital.
Sales Prospecting consumes Relationship Capital.
Relationship Capital is shared between advisors, managers and Partners.
P200 is evidence of relationship capital.
PM is a network-expansion pattern.
CI is a trust and access amplifier.
The root cause underneath recruiting is the capacity to create, maintain and expand relationships.
The advisor-as-franchise metaphor is useful as a product and manager mental model, but not yet a constitutional principle.

Final decision:

Relationship Capital:
APPROVED as a transversal concept and shared asset model.
Not approved as an independent domain yet.

Career Capital:
DISCOVERED, strong candidate concept.
Not approved as a domain yet.

Trust Transfer Principle:
APPROVED as architecture principle candidate.
Not constitutional yet.

Franchise Principle:
DISCOVERED as useful model mental.
Not approved as formal principle.


2. RELATIONSHIP CAPITAL REVIEW

What Relationship Capital is:

Relationship Capital is the durable commercial value created by a person's access to other people, groups, communities, trust paths and referral networks.

It is the usable relationship surface from which opportunities can emerge.

It exists before a specific sales opportunity, recruitment candidate or policy.

It can be activated for:

Recruitment.
Sales prospecting.
Referrals.
Centers of Influence.
Community development.
Partner development.
Advisor productivity.

What Relationship Capital is not:

It is not a contact list.
It is not a CRM record.
It is not only P200.
It is not only referrals.
It is not only client relationships.
It is not a compensation metric.
It is not proof of production.
It is not proof of sales skill.
It is not owned by Recruitment Intelligence alone.
It is not Forge Core as a domain.

What Relationship Capital contains:

Family.
Friends.
Colleagues.
Clients.
Ex-clients.
Communities.
Clubs.
Associations.
Schools.
Professional groups.
Social groups.
Religious or civic groups.
Centers of Influence.
Natural market contacts.
Referral paths.
Indirect access.
People who recognize the advisor.
People who trust the advisor.
People who trust someone who trusts the advisor.
Objects or situations that reveal people.

Is it an asset?

Yes.

Relationship Capital is best understood as a commercial asset.

It can increase, decay, be activated, be neglected, be transferred partially through trust, and produce opportunities.

Is it an entity?

Not as a single entity.

It is an aggregate concept composed from CommercialPerson, CommercialRelationship, CommercialAccount, CommercialAttribution, EvidenceRecord and snapshots.

Is it a capability?

Partly.

The existence of a network is an asset.
The ability to activate it is a capability.

These must be separated:

Relationship Capital = what relationship access exists.
Relationship Activation Capability = how well the advisor/Partner uses it.

Is it a network?

Yes, but not only a network.

The graph matters, but quality, trust, familiarity, permission, influence and actionability matter too.

Is it a domain?

Not yet.

It is approved as a transversal concept.
It may later justify a formal domain or subdomain, but this PAQ should not create one.

Is it Forge Core?

No.

Forge Core should provide the primitives needed to represent it:

CommercialPerson.
CommercialRelationship.
CommercialAccount.
CommercialAttribution.
CommercialAssignment.
EvidenceRecord.
ProvenanceRecord.
PeriodSnapshot.

Is it Shared Commercial Model?

Partly yes.

The primitives live in Shared Commercial Model.
The interpretation "Relationship Capital" should not be hardcoded as a Core primitive until its ownership is ratified.

Is it a transversal concept?

Yes.

It is consumed by Recruitment Intelligence, Sales Prospecting, Relationship Intelligence, Revenue Intelligence, Partner Intelligence, Productivity Intelligence and Business Planning.


3. CAREER CAPITAL REVIEW

What Career Capital is:

Career Capital is the set of assets, capabilities and credibility that make a person commercially viable as an advisor, Partner or commercial leader.

It includes relationship capital, but is broader.

Relationship Capital answers:

Who can this person reach, through what trust paths, and with what relationship access?

Career Capital answers:

What commercial assets and capabilities does this person bring into the career, and how likely are they to convert those assets into sustained productivity?

Is Career Capital different from Relationship Capital?

Yes.

Relationship Capital is one component of Career Capital.

Career Capital may include:

Market Natural.
P200 / Proyecto de Marketing.
Access to groups.
Ability to prospect.
Confidence to contact.
Trustworthiness.
Credibility.
Professional history.
Community membership.
Centers of Influence.
Referral access.
Sales maturity.
Discipline.
Coachability.
Communication capability.
Observed execution behavior.
Market familiarity.
Need-segmentation awareness.

What belongs to Career Capital:

Market Natural.
Community access.
Professional graph.
Social graph.
Relationship Capital.
Referral habits.
Centers of Influence.
P200 completion quality.
Ability to ask for referrals.
Ability to activate names.
Prospecting comfort.
Credibility in a market.
Commercial confidence.
Relevant career history.
Execution consistency when measured by Mick.

What does not belong to Career Capital:

Paid commissions.
Official compensation.
TA qualification.
Contest results.
Policy production as a raw fact.
Career stage as formal status.
Rule Pack compensation formulas.
Manager compensation.
Product knowledge by itself unless it affects commercial viability.

Is Career Capital an asset?

Yes.

It is an aggregate commercial asset.

Is it a score?

Not fundamentally.

It may produce scores or snapshots later, but reducing Career Capital to one score would be dangerous.

Is it a snapshot?

It can produce snapshots:

Initial Career Capital Snapshot.
Market Natural Snapshot.
Relationship Activation Snapshot.
Prospecting Capacity Snapshot.

But Career Capital itself is broader than a snapshot.

Is it a capability?

Partly.

It includes both assets and capabilities.

Examples:

Asset:
Existing social/professional network.

Capability:
Ability to activate that network.

Is it an interpretation?

Yes.

Career Capital is an interpretation over relationships, evidence, behavior, history and market access.

Is it a domain?

Not yet.

Decision:
Career Capital remains DISCOVERED.

It should not become a domain until Forge determines whether it belongs under Relationship Intelligence, Recruitment Intelligence, Advisor Experience, Career Intelligence or Shared Commercial Model as a cross-domain concept.

5. P200 ANALYSIS

P200 is:

A list:
Yes, operationally.

An activity:
Completing or updating P200 is an activity.

Evidence:
Yes. It is evidence of Market Natural and relationship inventory.

A snapshot:
Yes. At a point in time, it shows the state of a person's relationship inventory.

An asset:
Not directly. The relationships represented are the asset. The P200 is an artifact that evidences the asset.

A score:
No. It may contribute to a score or quality interpretation, but is not a score.

What P200 measures:

Relationship inventory.
Known people.
Market Natural breadth.
Initial prospecting surface.
Ability or willingness to list contacts.
Possible social/professional reach.

What P200 predicts:

Potential prospecting capacity.
Potential referral surface.
Risk of running out of people to contact.
Potential early activity base.
Potential friction in recruitment or sales.

What P200 does not predict:

It does not guarantee production.
It does not guarantee conversion.
It does not guarantee advisor quality.
It does not guarantee willingness to call.
It does not guarantee trust.
It does not guarantee sales skill.

Who should own P200?

Recommended:
Shared Commercial Model owns the underlying people/relationship primitives and evidence.

Career Capital, if later approved, should own interpretation of P200 as market/career asset.

Recruitment Intelligence consumes P200 for recruitment capacity and candidate evaluation.

Sales Prospecting consumes P200 for client prospecting capacity.

Mick consumes P200 activation behavior.

Decision:
P200 should not be owned exclusively by Recruitment.

Best classification:
P200 is a Rule Pack / training artifact that captures Market Natural evidence.
It should produce snapshots and be consumed by Recruitment, Sales Prospecting, Business Planning and Productivity/Partner interpretations.


6. MAGIC QUESTION ANALYSIS

Recruitment Magic Question:

"A quien invitarias a una entrevista como esta?"

Sales equivalent:

"A quien le serviria lo mismo que vimos hoy?"

What they have in common:

Both convert an interaction into network expansion.
Both use trust and context to generate new names.
Both depend on the current person's relationship graph.
Both work better when the current person trusts the advisor/Partner.
Both create indirect access.
Both can produce compounding growth.

Is PM a source?

Yes, in recruitment reporting.

But "PM" as source is only one local label.

Is PM a pattern?

Yes.

It is a reusable referral-generation pattern.

Is PM a technique?

Yes.

It is a conversation/prospecting technique.

Is PM a motor of expansion?

Yes.

It is a network expansion mechanism.

Does PM belong to Recruitment?

Recruitment owns PM use for recruiting candidates.

Does PM belong to Sales?

Sales Prospecting owns analogous referral requests for client prospects.

Does PM belong to a higher layer?

Yes, conceptually.

The higher layer is Relationship Capital activation or Prospecting pattern.

Decision:
Do not model Magic Question only as RecruitmentSource.

Model it as:

Local source label in Recruitment.
Referral-generation event.
Network-expansion pattern.
Relationship activation technique.


7. CENTER OF INFLUENCE REVIEW

Is CI a source?

Yes, operationally.

CI can be a recruitment source or sales prospecting source.

Is CI a person?

Usually yes.

A CI is generally a CommercialPerson with influence over a group or network.

Is CI a node?

Yes.

CI is a high-leverage node in a relationship graph.

Is CI a capacity?

Partly.

Having a CI relationship is an asset.
Cultivating and activating a CI is a capability.

Is CI Relationship Capital?

Yes.

CI is part of Relationship Capital because it represents trusted access to other people.

Is CI Career Capital?

Yes, when attached to an advisor or candidate as part of their career-building commercial asset.

What decisions CI enables:

Which influence relationships to cultivate.
Which group to access through trusted introduction.
Which CI is producing quality referrals.
Which CI relationship is stale.
Which Partner/advisor has strong network leverage.
Which CI can help rebuild a weak funnel.

Architectural decision:

CI should be modeled as a relationship/influence role over CommercialPerson and CommercialRelationship, not as a simple source string.


8. TRUST TRANSFER PRINCIPLE REVIEW

Hypothesis:
Higher quality opportunities arise through transfer of trust.

Evidence from training material:

Referrals matter because the referred person gives attention due to association.
The value of the advisor's service is pre-established by the referring person.
The referred person has initial trust because the referrer trusts the advisor.
Centers of Influence create access through respect and recommendation.
Market Natural begins with people who recognize the advisor.

RDA:

High trust transfer when the referring advisor is credible.
Risk: advisor may refer weak-fit names.

PM:

Medium to high trust transfer depending on the originating candidate and relationship depth.
Risk: may generate volume without quality.

CI:

High trust transfer when CI is respected and active.
Risk: CI can decay or stop referring.

Client referrals:

High trust transfer when client is satisfied and relationship is strong.
Risk: client may not know who is a good fit.

Market Natural:

Variable trust transfer.
Recognition and familiarity help, but do not guarantee need or willingness.

FF:

Low trust transfer.
May still work through skill, timing, need or volume, but starts with lower inherited confidence.

Should Trust Transfer Principle become constitutional?

Not yet.

It has enough evidence to become an architecture principle candidate, but not constitutional doctrine.

Reason:

Trust transfer is powerful but not absolute.
High trust can still produce low quality.
Cold sources can still produce success.
Relationship access can create opportunity but not guarantee action.

Recommended status:
APPROVED as architecture principle candidate.
Not constitutional.

Principle statement candidate:

Forge should identify and preserve the trust path behind opportunities because trust transfer affects attention, conversion probability, relationship quality and recommended next action.


9. FRANCHISE PRINCIPLE REVIEW

Statement:
An advisor is a franchise.
The Partner's job is to open franchises.

Is it a useful metaphor?

Yes.

It explains that each advisor is not merely an employee or producer.
Each advisor can be viewed as an economic unit with its own market, activity, relationships, productivity and growth potential.

Does it explain productivity?

Partly.

It helps explain that productivity depends on the advisor's ability to activate their own market, relationships, habits and sales process.

Does it explain development?

Yes.

If an advisor is a franchise-like unit, development is not generic training.
Development is the process of making that unit commercially viable.

Does it explain promotion?

Partly.

Promotion may be understood as a shift from operating one unit to developing multiple units.

Does it explain Partner Intelligence?

Yes, as a model mental.

Partner Intelligence can ask:

Is this Partner opening viable advisor units?
Are those units developing?
Are they productive?
Are they expanding their own relationship capital?
Are they self-sustaining?

Should it become a principle?

Not yet.

It is too metaphorical for constitutional or domain lock.

It should remain a model mental until validated against:

Compensation rules.
Development outcomes.
Productivity patterns.
Advisor retention.
Promotion paths.
Manager/Partner economics.

Recommended status:
DISCOVERED.

Best use:
Product and management reasoning, not Core architecture.

11. RED TEAM REVIEW

Scenario 1:
Person with large network and zero production.

Failure:
Relationship Capital alone does not equal productivity.

Limit:
Relationship Capital must be separated from activation capability, sales skill and behavior.

Guardrail:
Do not treat network size as success.


Scenario 2:
Person with small network and high production.

Failure:
Relationship Capital model may under-value skill, niche, discipline or high-quality small networks.

Limit:
Relationship quality and activation matter more than raw size.

Guardrail:
Measure conversion and activity, not only graph size.


Scenario 3:
PM generates volume but not quality.

Failure:
Magic Question as source count becomes vanity metric.

Limit:
PM must be evaluated by downstream conversion and referral-chain quality.

Guardrail:
PM count is not PM value.


Scenario 4:
CI stops referring.

Failure:
CI as static asset overstates current value.

Limit:
Relationship Capital decays.

Guardrail:
Track relationship freshness, activity and recent referral output.


Scenario 5:
P200 complete but never activated.

Failure:
P200 as asset or score becomes misleading.

Limit:
P200 is evidence, not execution.

Guardrail:
Separate P200 completion from P200 activation.


Scenario 6:
Advisor successful without Market Natural.

Failure:
Career Capital model may overemphasize Market Natural.

Limit:
Some advisors can build through cold prospecting, digital channels, niche expertise, institutional access or strong sales skill.

Guardrail:
Market Natural is a leading indicator, not a universal requirement.


Scenario 7:
Recruitment successful without Relationship Capital.

Failure:
Relationship Capital may be too broad or under-detected.

Limit:
Some recruitment can come from cold campaigns, brand, job boards or institutional pipelines.

Guardrail:
Do not reject FF or non-relationship sources. Treat them as lower trust transfer, not invalid.


Scenario 8:
High production with few relationships.

Failure:
Relationship count may not explain quality of relationship, account size, niche, or high-value market access.

Limit:
CommercialAccount quality and opportunity value matter.

Guardrail:
Measure relationship quality and economic conversion, not only number of relationships.


Red Team conclusion:

Relationship Capital is real but dangerous if reduced to size.
Career Capital is real but dangerous if reduced to one score.
Trust Transfer is real but not deterministic.
Franchise Principle is useful but too metaphorical for lock.


12. ARCHITECTURE DECISION

Relationship Capital:
APPROVED

Decision meaning:
Approved as a transversal architecture concept and shared commercial asset model.

Not approved as:
Independent domain.
Implementation.
Schema.
Score.

Why:
Evidence is strong across ES training material and Recruitment PAQs.
It explains the upstream layer before names, prospects and candidates.
It is required by Recruitment, Sales Prospecting, Relationship Intelligence and Partner Intelligence.


Career Capital:
DISCOVERED

Decision meaning:
Recognized as real and important, but not yet approved as a formal domain or locked model.

Why:
It is broader than Relationship Capital and likely includes behavior, credibility, market access, prospecting ability and commercial skill.
Ownership is still unclear.
It may overlap with Career Intelligence, Mick, Advisor Experience, Recruitment and Partner Intelligence.


Trust Transfer Principle:
APPROVED

Decision meaning:
Approved as architecture principle candidate for future domain design.

Not approved as:
Constitutional principle.
Rule.
Formula.
Guarantee.

Why:
Training material gives strong evidence that referrals and CI improve attention, perceived value and initial trust.
However, trust transfer does not guarantee conversion or quality.


Franchise Principle:
DISCOVERED

Decision meaning:
Useful model mental for Partner Intelligence and development conversations.

Not approved as:
Constitutional principle.
Domain.
Core model.

Why:
It explains advisor economic units and Partner leverage, but remains metaphorical and needs validation against productivity, development, retention and compensation evidence.

14. FINAL VERDICT

Final finding:

The true root layer is not Recruitment.

The true root layer is the capacity to create, maintain and expand relationships.

Recruitment is one way of activating that capacity.

Sales prospecting is another.

Partner growth depends on creating advisors who can activate that capacity repeatedly.

Official corrected causal root:

Relationship Capital
Career Capital
Prospecting Activation
Names

Recruitment Intelligence remains valid.

But Recruitment Intelligence must not own the relationship layer.

It consumes it.
It activates it.
It expands it.
It interprets it for recruiting decisions.

Relationship Capital should be accepted as an approved transversal architecture concept.

Career Capital should remain discovered until ownership is clarified.

Trust Transfer should be accepted as an approved architecture principle candidate.

Franchise Principle should remain discovered as a useful model mental.

Recommended project direction:

Proceed to Recruitment Hardening before opening Partner Intelligence, Productivity Intelligence, Forecast Intelligence or Business Planning Intelligence.

Reason:

The Intelligence Phase should start with a hardened recruitment root because future Partner growth depends on whether Forge can correctly explain how relationship capital becomes advisor capacity.
