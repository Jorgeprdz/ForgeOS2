1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Recruitment Intelligence is the Forge domain responsible for explaining whether a Partner is creating the future advisor base required for growth.

Its job is not to recreate Reclutamiento.xlsx.

Its job is to convert the tacit agency recruiting system into decision intelligence:

Where do candidates come from?
Which sources create quality?
Which names become interviews?
Which interviews convert?
Where does the funnel break?
Which candidates are stuck?
Which candidates generate networks?
Which recruiting behaviors predict future advisor capacity?
Which gaps threaten Partner productivity and compensation?
What should the Partner do next?

The operating phrase:

"Si reclutas, nada mas importa.
Si no reclutas, nada mas importa."

is not a slogan inside Forge.

It becomes a measurable architecture:

Source quality.
Name flow.
Stage conversion.
Precontract transition.
Signed advisor creation.
Development capacity.
Future productivity.
Future Partner economics.

Recruitment Intelligence owns the recruitment funnel and recruiting source quality.

It does not own Precontract execution, advisor development, productivity, compensation, career promotion, contest qualification, Rule Pack targets or official income.

Recruitment Intelligence consumes Forge Core primitives:

CommercialPerson.
CommercialRelationship.
CommercialAssignment.
CommercialAttribution.
EvidenceRecord.
ProvenanceRecord.
RuleSnapshot.
PeriodSnapshot.

Recruitment Intelligence produces:

Recruitment funnel state.
Source quality interpretation.
Candidate stage history.
Recruitment risks.
Recruitment network visibility.
Recruitment snapshots.
Recruitment decisions.
Recruitment forecasts clearly labeled as forecasts.

Final domain status:

Recruitment Intelligence is ready for ARCHITECTURE CANDIDATE.

It is not ready for ARCHITECTURE LOCK because POP remains undefined, PCV Partner 2026 source rules were not text-extractable in this pass, and SMNYL Agency 2026 Rule Pack thresholds must be formalized before lock.


2. DOMAIN PURPOSE

Mission:

Recruitment Intelligence helps Partners and managers build the future advisor base by converting recruiting activity into clear decisions, risks, source quality insights, funnel movement and causal explanation.

Problem it solves:

Manual recruitment tracking is spread across names, P200, PM, monthly sheets, One Thing, RTW, Precont and planning workbooks.

This creates duplicate capture, unclear ownership, weak historical traceability and low decision clarity.

Recruitment Intelligence solves this by giving Forge a canonical domain that can answer:

Do we have enough names?
Are those names coming from quality sources?
Are we relying too much on cold sources?
Are we asking Magic Question consistently?
Which candidates are moving?
Which candidates are stuck?
Where does the funnel break?
Which source produces connected candidates?
Which source produces signed advisors?
Which candidates generate valuable networks?
Which Partner is creating future advisor capacity?
Which recruiting gap threatens future productivity?

Decisions enabled:

Who should be contacted next.
Which candidates require follow-up.
Which source should be activated.
Which warm-source channel is weak.
Which candidate is at risk.
Which interview stage is bottlenecked.
Which Partner needs coaching.
Whether recruiting volume is enough for the current goal.
Whether the Partner is building future productivity.
Whether the downstream development load is likely to increase.

Questions Recruitment Intelligence answers:

Do I have enough names?
Do I have enough warm sources?
Which source produces quality?
Where is my funnel breaking?
Which candidate should move next?
Which candidate is abandoned?
Which candidate generated valuable referrals?
Am I creating future advisors or only collecting names?
Is my recruiting activity sufficient for my Partner goal?
What is the next recruiting action?

Recruitment Intelligence is responsible for:

Recruitment sources.

P200 interpretation as market evidence.
Recruitment risk identification.

Recruitment decisions and next actions.
Recruitment causal chain explanation.

Recruitment Intelligence is not responsible for:

Precontract lifecycle execution.
Key activation rules.
Key expiration rules.
EFC execution.
Policy folio operations.
Commission calculation.
TA qualification.
Advisor productivity calculation.
Partner compensation calculation.
Career stage after contract.
Contest qualification.
Manager compensation.
Conservation metrics.
Official income.
UI onboarding.
Generic CRM contact storage.

Separation from Precontract:

Recruitment ends when a candidate becomes connected and starts precontract.
Precontract owns key activation, precontract window, EFC, operational requirements and readiness to sign.
Recruitment may consume precontract outcome to measure conversion quality.

Separation from Development:

Recruitment can identify future development load.
Development owns advisor development actions after signing or formal entry into advisor path.
Recruitment does not own advisor development plans.

Separation from Productivity:

Recruitment predicts future productivity risk through source quality, P200 and early behavior.
Productivity Intelligence owns productivity metrics and interpretations.

Separation from Compensation:

Recruitment creates upstream facts and signals.
Compensation Intelligence owns paid, accrued, calculated and rule-based compensation events.
Recruitment must not calculate Partner income.

Separation from Forecast:

Recruitment owns recruiting facts and recruitment-owned metrics.
Forecast Intelligence may forecast likely outcomes using recruitment data.
Recruitment may produce recruitment-specific forecast snapshots, but all forecasts must be labeled as forecasts.

Separation from Business Planning:

Recruitment identifies gaps.
Business Planning converts goals into forecast, gap and actions across domains.
Recruitment supplies the recruiting layer of that plan.

Ownership summary:

Recruitment owns:
Candidate recruitment state, source quality, recruitment funnel metrics, PM network metrics, recruitment risk.

Precontract owns:
Connected-to-contract operational lifecycle.

Development owns:
Advisor development after recruiting handoff.

Productivity owns:
Productivity metrics and Rule Pack productivity interpretation.

Compensation owns:
Financial events, bonuses and official income.

Forecast owns:
Cross-domain future scenario modeling.

Business Planning owns:
Goal to forecast to gap to actions plan orchestration.

Rule Pack owns:
SMNYL-specific targets, stage expectations, POP meaning, warm-source quotas, activity standards and downstream bonus interpretation.

Forge Core owns:
Universal primitives only.

6. SOURCE MODEL

Recruitment Source Quality Model should exist.

It should belong to Recruitment Intelligence as an interpretation model over source events, source relationships and downstream conversion.

It must not be hardcoded into Forge Core.


6.1 NOM

Meaning:
Nominados.

Classification:
Warm or semi-warm depending on nominator relationship.

Strengths:
Can provide trust transfer.
Can represent intentional recommendation.
May reveal hidden networks.

Weaknesses:
Quality depends heavily on nominator judgment.
May produce social names without fit.

Transferred confidence:
Medium to high.

Loop capacity:
Medium.

Recommended metrics:
NOM names.
NOM to EI.
NOM to ES.
NOM to connected.
NOM to signed.
Average stage time.
Drop-off reason.
Nominator quality.


6.2 CI

Meaning:
Centro de Influencia.

Classification:
Warm.

Strengths:
High leverage.
Repeated supply possible.
Trust transfer can be strong.

Weaknesses:
May depend on relationship maintenance.
Can become stale if not cultivated.

Transferred confidence:
High when CI is credible.

Loop capacity:
High.

Recommended metrics:
CI names.
CI conversion.
CI repeat contribution.
CI downstream quality.
CI relationship freshness.


6.3 RDA

Meaning:
Referido de Asesor.

Classification:
Warm.

Strengths:
Advisor trust transfer.
Can indicate advisor engagement.
Can identify advisors who recruit well.

Weaknesses:
Quality depends on advisor judgment and incentive alignment.
May create social referrals with low seriousness.

Transferred confidence:
High if advisor is trusted and understands fit.

Loop capacity:
High.

Recommended metrics:
RDA names by advisor.
RDA to EI.
RDA to connected.
RDA to signed.
RDA quality by referring advisor.
RDA repeatability.


6.4 OP

Meaning:
Observacion Personal.

Classification:
Warm or semi-warm.

Strengths:
Reflects Partner judgment.
Can identify high-potential people outside formal referral channels.

Weaknesses:
Can be subjective.
May lack trust transfer.

Transferred confidence:
Medium.

Loop capacity:
Medium.

Recommended metrics:
OP names.
OP conversion.
OP judgment accuracy.
OP stage drop-off.


6.5 PM

Meaning:
Pregunta Magica.

Classification:
Warm network-origin source.

Strengths:
Creates growth loop.
Can multiply candidate generation.
Can reveal network quality.

Weaknesses:
May generate many names with weak fit.
Relationship to candidate may be shallow.
Can inflate source volume without quality.

Transferred confidence:
Medium, variable by candidate and relationship.

Loop capacity:
Very high.

Recommended metrics:
PM names per candidate.
PM names to EI.
PM names to connected.
PM names to signed.
PM referral depth.
PM referral chain value.
Originating candidate network quality.


6.6 FF

Meaning:
Fuente Fria.

Classification:
Cold.

Strengths:
Can create volume.
Useful when warm channels are insufficient.
Can expand beyond existing network.

Weaknesses:
Low trust transfer.
Often lower conversion.
Can mask weak warm-source discipline.

Transferred confidence:
Low.

Loop capacity:
Low to medium.

Recommended metrics:
FF volume.
FF to EI.
FF to connected.
FF conversion cost.
FF drop-off reason.
FF dependency ratio.

7. P200 SEMANTICS

Hypothesis reviewed:
P200 is not recruitment.
P200 represents Mercado Natural.

Verdict:
The hypothesis is correct with nuance.

P200 is not only recruitment.
P200 is market capital evidence.

What P200 really is:

An inventory of natural market names.
A proxy for relationship reach.
A signal of candidate/advisor market access.
A discipline artifact.
A potential recruitment source.
A future productivity indicator.

What it measures:

Market depth.
Relationship inventory.
Willingness to list names.
Potential prospecting capacity.
Possible future activity base.
Completeness of natural market thinking.

What it predicts:

Potential ability to prospect.
Potential early advisor activity.
Potential referral capacity.
Risk of "no tiene contactos".
Risk of low future productivity.

What it does not prove:

It does not prove sales skill.
It does not prove advisor quality.
It does not prove willingness to contact.
It does not prove conversion.
It does not prove future income.

Should it belong to Recruitment?

Recruitment should consume and interpret P200 for candidate sourcing and recruiting risk.
Recruitment should not be the sole owner of P200 as a universal concept.

Should it belong to Career Capital?

Conceptually yes, if Forge later formalizes Career Capital.
But no new domain should be created here.

Should it belong to Shared Commercial Model?

The underlying primitive should belong to Shared Commercial Model as relationship/market evidence.

Recommended conceptual placement:

P200 as a Rule Pack-specific artifact over a universal MarketInventory or CommercialNetworkEvidence concept.

Is it an entity?

The P200 artifact can be represented as a conceptual entity or evidence bundle.
Each P200 name should resolve to or create a CommercialPerson candidate only when it enters an active workflow.

Is it evidence?

Yes.
P200 is evidence of market inventory.

Is it a snapshot?

Yes.
P200 completion at a point in time should produce a snapshot.

Is it an activity?

Completing or updating P200 is an activity.
The list itself is not merely an activity.

Best answer:

P200 should be modeled as market evidence consumed by Recruitment Intelligence, not as a recruitment-only list.

11. RECRUITMENT NETWORK

Recruitment Network should exist formally.

Value:

It captures the fact that recruitment is not only a linear funnel.

Candidates, advisors, centers of influence and partners generate networks.

Example:

Juan refers Pedro.
Pedro refers Ana.
Ana becomes Partner.

This matters because Juan may have created large network value even if Juan never signed.

Should it persist?

Yes, but as relationship and attribution history, not as a separate duplicate identity graph.

What decisions it enables:

Which candidates generate valuable networks.
Which advisors are strong recruiting nodes.
Which centers of influence deserve attention.
Which Partner is building referral loops.
Which source has hidden downstream value.
Which candidate deserves follow-up because of network quality.

Risks:

Privacy.
Consent.
Incorrect attribution.
Overvaluing network size over quality.
Duplicated identities.
Treating social connection as commercial permission.
Compensation disputes if network attribution is later monetized.

Guardrails:

Use CommercialRelationship for relationships.
Use CommercialAttribution for credit when applicable.
Use EvidenceRecord for source.
Use RuleSnapshot for any monetized interpretation.
Separate network value from compensation.

14. FOUNDATION COMPATIBILITY

14.1 CommercialPerson

Compatible.

Conflict:
If RecruitmentCandidate becomes durable identity, foundation is violated.


14.2 CommercialAccount

Compatible.

Recruitment may not always need CommercialAccount, but business, family or center-of-influence recruitment paths may use it.

Conflict:
None immediate.


14.3 CommercialRelationship

Compatible and required.

Used for referrals, nominations, PM, CI and network edges.

Conflict:
Relationship must not imply attribution or compensation automatically.


14.4 CommercialAssignment

Compatible and required.

Used for Partner/manager responsibility over candidate pipeline by period.

Conflict:
Do not use assignment as attribution.


14.5 CommercialAttribution

Compatible and required.

Used for referral origin, PM origin, connection credit and possible Partner credit.

Conflict:
Attribution must not imply payment without Rule Pack.


14.6 EvidenceRecord

Compatible and required.

Conflict:
Manual spreadsheet data may have low confidence unless provenance is preserved.

Conflict:
None.

Compatible and required.

Stage expectations.
Recruitment activity standards.
Downstream Partner compensation interpretations.

Conflict:

15. FINAL VERDICT

Status:
ARCHITECTURE CANDIDATE

Why not FOUNDATION DRAFT:
The domain is more mature than a draft because PAQ-01 reviewed real operational artifacts and this model defines boundaries, entities, relationships, events, metrics and foundation compatibility.

Why not ARCHITECTURE LOCK:
Three items remain unresolved:

1. POP definition is not confirmed.
2. PCV Partner 2026 rules were not text-extractable in this pass.
3. SMNYL Agency 2026 Rule Pack needs formal recruitment rules for targets, source warmth, stage expectations and downstream Partner interpretation.

What is approved:

Recruitment Intelligence as a formal Intelligence Phase domain.
RecruitmentCandidate as a recruitment context over CommercialPerson.
Recruitment Source Quality Model.
Magic Question Network Model.
Recruitment Network as a formal conceptual model.
Recruitment funnel ownership.
Recruitment event taxonomy.
Recruitment metric ownership.
P200 as market evidence consumed by Recruitment, not recruitment-only list.

What is not approved:

Implementation.
Engines.
Schemas.
UI.
Hardcoded SMNYL thresholds in Core.
Compensation calculations.
POP interpretation without source rule.

Final architectural statement:

Recruitment Intelligence is the domain that tells Forge whether a Partner is creating future advisor capacity.

It must protect the difference between:

Names and quality.
Volume and conversion.
Referral count and network value.
Connected and signed.
Recruitment and precontract.
Signing and development.
Forecast and fact.
Rule Pack interpretation and Core primitive.

This model is sufficient to proceed to a formal Recruitment Intelligence Architecture document or Rule Pack recruitment hardening pass.

It is not sufficient for implementation lock.
