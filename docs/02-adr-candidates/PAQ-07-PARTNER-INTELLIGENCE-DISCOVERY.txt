1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Partner Intelligence is the Forge domain responsible for explaining whether a Partner is building a sustainable commercial unit.

It does not measure only recruitment.
It does not measure only productivity.
It does not measure only compensation.
It does not measure only promotion.

Partner Intelligence exists because a Partner's real job is not to personally sell more.
The Partner's real job is to create, develop, retain and scale productive commercial capacity through other people.

The core question is:

Is this Partner building a future-producing unit or only creating temporary activity?

Recruitment Intelligence answers:

Is candidate flow being created?

Partner Intelligence must answer:

Is candidate flow becoming productive, retained, scalable advisor capacity?

The phrase from Andrey Garcia:

"Un asesor es una franquicia.
La labor del Partner es abrir franquicias."

is architecturally useful, but not literally complete.

It should not be treated as a corporate definition.
It should not become a rigid constitutional principle yet.
It should be treated as a strong operating model:

A Partner builds economic units.
Each advisor can become an independent production unit.
The Partner's value is the creation and development of those units.

The phrase is strongest when it explains:

Recruitment.
Development.
Productivity creation.
Unit scaling.
Leadership pipeline.

The phrase is weakest when it implies:

Every signed advisor is already a franchise.
Every advisor has equal economic potential.
Opening many advisors is enough.
Partner success can be measured only by headcount.

Final discovery:

The true economic unit Partner Intelligence must study is not merely "advisor".

The true economic unit is:

Productive Advisor Capacity

This includes:

Signed advisor.
Activated behavior.
Development progress.
Production capability.
Retention probability.
Future leadership potential.
Economic contribution.

Therefore:

Recruitment creates future advisor supply.
Development converts signed people into productive advisors.
Productivity proves economic output.
Retention preserves the unit.
Leadership scales the unit.
Compensation reflects some outcomes, often late.

Partner Intelligence integrates those signals into one domain-level interpretation:

Is the Partner building sustainable commercial capacity?


2. PARTNER DEFINITION

A Partner is not only a manager.

Manager is an incomplete definition because it emphasizes supervision.

A Partner is not only a coach.

Coach is incomplete because it emphasizes development but not recruitment, capacity creation or economic accountability.

A Partner is not only a recruiter.

Recruiter is incomplete because recruitment without development creates headcount without productive capacity.

A Partner is not only a builder.

Builder is useful but vague unless Forge defines what is being built.

A Partner is not only a talent developer.

Talent Developer is useful but incomplete because it starts after candidate or advisor identification.

A Partner is not only a business unit creator.

Business Unit Creator is closer because it captures economic responsibility, but it may ignore the human development dimension.

A Partner is not literally a franchise builder.

Franchise Builder is a powerful model mental, but the advisor is not always a true franchise from day one.

Best operational definition:

A Partner is a commercial capacity builder.

The Partner identifies, attracts, converts, develops, activates and retains people who can become productive advisor units.

The Partner's job is to convert human potential into durable commercial capacity.

Compatible roles:

Recruiter.
Coach.
Developer.
Capacity builder.
Commercial leader.
Pipeline owner.
Risk manager.
Talent multiplier.

Incomplete roles:

Supervisor.
Reporter.
Motivator.
Administrative manager.
Compensation recipient.

Partner Intelligence definition:

The domain that evaluates whether a Partner is building sustainable commercial capacity through recruitment, development, productivity, retention and future leadership creation.


3. ECONOMIC UNIT ANALYSIS

Critical question:

What economic unit is a Partner trying to create?

Hypothesis:

The advisor.

This is partially correct.

An advisor can be:

Vendedor:
Yes, but this is incomplete.
The advisor sells, but the Partner is not merely collecting salespeople.

Productor:
Yes, when the advisor generates production.
However, a signed advisor with no production is not yet a productive economic unit.

Empleado:
No as the main architecture model.
Forge should not model advisor value as employment status.

Unidad economica:
Yes, when the advisor produces, retains, renews and grows.
This is the strongest architectural model.

Microempresa:
Partially.
Useful because advisors manage relationships, pipeline, activity, clients and revenue.
Dangerous if it ignores dependency on manager, product, carrier, office, rule pack and development system.

Franquicia:
Useful as metaphor and operating model.
Not literally universal.
It helps explain duplication of a commercial model through people.
It fails if Forge assumes every signed advisor is already scalable, autonomous or economically durable.

Otra cosa:
Productive Advisor Capacity.

This is the best architectural unit.

Productive Advisor Capacity is not just a person.
It is a person plus activated behavior plus commercial capability plus development progress plus retention probability plus production output.

Partner Intelligence must distinguish:

Potential advisor.
Candidate.
Connected candidate.
Signed advisor.
Active advisor.
Productive advisor.
Retained advisor.
Leader candidate.
Manager successor.

Destroying the advisor-as-franchise hypothesis:

Counterexample 1:
A Partner signs 20 advisors and none produces.
If each advisor were a franchise, the Partner built 20 units.
Reality: the Partner created 20 weak or inactive units.

Counterexample 2:
A Partner has 3 advisors and one produces most of the unit.
The unit has production, but the Partner has concentration risk.

Counterexample 3:
A Partner has many productive advisors but no future leaders.
The unit may be productive now but not scalable.

Counterexample 4:
A Partner has high compensation because of one temporary production spike.
The unit may not be healthy.

Counterexample 5:
A Partner recruits constantly but does not retain.
The unit leaks capacity.

Conclusion:

An advisor is not automatically a franchise.
An advisor can become an economic unit.
Partner Intelligence must measure the conversion from signed person to productive advisor capacity.

Recommended model:

Candidate Flow
Signed Advisor
Active Advisor
Productive Advisor
Retained Advisor
Scalable Advisor
Leadership Candidate

The Partner's unit economics are built on the quantity, quality, productivity, durability and scalability of those advisor units.

9. PARTNER RISK MODEL

Partner Risk is owned by Partner Intelligence.

It integrates risks from Recruitment, Development, Productivity, Retention, Behavior and Forecast domains.


Recruitment Collapse

Definition:
Future candidate flow is insufficient.

Signals:
Low candidate pipeline.
Low warm sources.
Few interviews.
Few connected.
Pipeline aging.

Impact:
Future advisor capacity shortage.


Development Collapse

Definition:
Signed advisors are not becoming active or productive.

Signals:
Many signed with low activity.
Poor early development completion.
Weak coaching cadence.
Low first production.

Impact:
Recruitment does not convert into economic capacity.


Productivity Concentration

Definition:
Unit production depends on too few advisors.

Signals:
High production share from one advisor.
Low median productivity.
Large gap between top producer and rest.

Impact:
Unit is fragile even if current production is high.


Single Advisor Dependency

Definition:
Partner outcome is materially dependent on one advisor.

Signals:
One advisor drives qualification, compensation or promotion readiness.

Impact:
Loss or slowdown of one advisor damages unit health.


Retention Risk

Definition:
Advisor capacity may not persist.

Signals:
Early inactivity.
Low development engagement.
Declining production.
Low relationship activation.
Low persistence indicators.

Impact:
Unit loses accumulated capacity.


Leadership Gap

Definition:
No emerging advisors can become multipliers.

Signals:
No productive advisors with influence.
No advisors developing others.
No future Partner candidates.

Impact:
Unit cannot scale beyond current Partner.


Future Capacity Gap

Definition:
Current recruitment and development are insufficient for future goals.

Signals:
Forecasted advisor supply below goal.
Connected count below needed.
Signed count below needed.
Development backlog.

Impact:
Business plan becomes unrealistic.


Forecast Gap

Definition:
Partner expected outcomes differ materially from current trajectory.

Signals:
Goal higher than capacity.
Pipeline below forecast need.
Productivity below assumption.
Retention lower than plan.

Impact:
Partner needs action before lagging metrics fail.


Pipeline Aging

Definition:
Candidates or advisors are stuck too long without movement.

Signals:
Old candidates.
Old connected candidates.
Signed advisors with no early activity.

Impact:
Future capacity decays.


Source Fragility

Recruitment depends on weak or narrow source channels.

Signals:
High FF.
Low CI.
Low PM.

Impact:
Candidate supply becomes fragile.


Development Bandwidth Risk

Definition:
Partner signs more people than they can develop.

Signals:
High signed count with low coaching cadence.
Delayed follow-up.
Inconsistent development progress.

Impact:
Advisor conversion quality declines.


Compensation Mirage

Partner compensation looks strong while underlying unit health is weak.

12. PARTNER VS COMPENSATION

Partner Intelligence and Compensation Intelligence must remain separate.

Compensation Intelligence explains:

What was calculated.
What was earned.
What was paid.
What rules applied.
What period governed the payment.
Why the payout occurred.

Partner Intelligence explains:

Whether the Partner's commercial unit is healthy, durable and scalable.


Why a unit can appear successful in compensation but not be healthy:

Compensation is often lagging.
One advisor may create most of the payout.
A temporary production spike can inflate payout.
Rule Pack incentives may reward short-term behavior.
Recruitment may be collapsing.
Retention may be weak.
Development may be failing.
Future capacity may be empty.

Therefore:

High compensation is evidence.
It is not proof of unit health.


Why a unit can appear healthy and not yet reflect in compensation:

Recruitment pipeline may be strong but not yet signed.
Signed advisors may be developing but not yet producing.
Activity may be improving before production appears.
Retention may be improving before compensation reflects it.
Leadership capacity may be emerging before payout rules recognize it.

Therefore:

Low current compensation is not always failure.
It may be an early-stage capacity build.


Boundary:

Compensation owns payouts.
Partner owns unit health interpretation.

Partner Intelligence may consume compensation.
It must not calculate compensation.

Compensation may consume Partner assignments and Rule Pack context.
It must not define Partner health.

14. RED TEAM REVIEW

Scenario 1:
Many signed, 0 productivity.

What breaks:
If Partner Intelligence treats signed count as success, it fails.

Correct interpretation:
Recruitment conversion is strong, but development or productivity conversion is broken.

Guardrail:
Signed advisors must be separated from productive advisor capacity.


Scenario 2:
High production, 1 advisor.

What breaks:
If Partner Intelligence treats production total as unit health, it fails.

Correct interpretation:
Unit has productivity concentration risk and single advisor dependency.

Guardrail:
Measure distribution, not only total production.


Scenario 3:
Many advisors, low retention.

What breaks:
If Partner Intelligence treats headcount as capacity, it fails.

Correct interpretation:
Unit leaks capacity.
Recruitment may be replacing losses rather than compounding growth.

Guardrail:
Retention must be part of Partner health.


Scenario 4:
Much recruitment, no development.

What breaks:
If Partner Intelligence celebrates recruitment volume, it fails.

Correct interpretation:
Candidate supply is not becoming productive capacity.

Guardrail:
Recruitment output must be connected to signed, active and productive outcomes.


Scenario 5:
Much development, no recruitment.

What breaks:
If Partner Intelligence focuses only on current advisors, it misses future shortage.

Correct interpretation:
Current advisor base may improve, but future capacity pipeline is drying.

Guardrail:
Partner health must include future advisor capacity.


Scenario 6:
Star Partner, no successors.

What breaks:

Correct interpretation:

Guardrail:
