1. Executive Summary

Forecast Intelligence is the Forge OS domain that estimates what may happen next based on evidence, behavior, productivity, conservation, pipeline, historical context, period and assumptions.

Forecast Intelligence exists because Forge can already answer:

- Did the advisor execute? Mick.
- Did execution produce repeatable output? Productivity Intelligence.
- Was that output durable and healthy? Conservation Intelligence.
- What fact happened? Production Events.
- What money applies under rules? Compensation Intelligence.

Forecast Intelligence answers:

- What is likely to happen next?
- What outcome is at risk?
- What gap may appear?
- What confidence does Forge have?
- What assumptions drive the estimate?
- What evidence supports or weakens the forecast?

Forecast Intelligence is not fact.
Forecast Intelligence is not compensation.
Forecast Intelligence is not business planning.
Forecast Intelligence is not revenue pipeline.
Forecast Intelligence is not productivity.
Forecast Intelligence is not conservation.

Core architecture rule:

Forecasts are suggestions.
Facts are facts.

Forecast Intelligence must never present predictions as institutional truth, guaranteed income, confirmed compensation, confirmed production, confirmed conservation or official business result.

Why Forecast could not open before Productivity and Conservation:

- Without Productivity, Forge could confuse activity with future output.
- Without Conservation, Forge could project fragile production as durable output.
- Without PAQ-08.5, Forge could confuse local predictive truth with institutional historical truth.

Forecast Intelligence is now ready for discovery because Productivity and Conservation are locked as upstream interpretation domains.


2. Forecast Definition

Forecast:

- A structured estimate of what may happen in a future or not-yet-confirmed period.
- Must include evidence, assumptions, confidence, period and source state.
- Not a fact.

Projection:

- A forward-looking extension of current trend, pipeline, productivity, conservation or historical performance.
- Can be useful, but must not be presented as guaranteed.

Estimate:

- A bounded interpretation based on available evidence.
- May be local predictive, source-backed or rule-backed.

Scenario:

- A conditional view of what may happen if certain assumptions hold.
- Example: if pipeline moves, if productivity holds, if conservation risk does not materialize.
- No scenario is official truth.

Confidence:

- Qualitative indication of how reliable the forecast is based on evidence, source quality, completeness, history, rule support and lag.
- No numeric confidence thresholds are approved in this PAQ.

Uncertainty:

- Explicit recognition that evidence may be incomplete, lagged, corrected, contradicted or dependent on future behavior.

Local predictive forecast:

- Forecast based on local signals such as Mick behavior, pipeline, productivity trend, conservation risk and current activity.
- Useful for action.
- Not institutional truth.

Institutionally confirmed outcome:

- Source-backed historical outcome confirmed by Production Events, carrier reports, official snapshots, payment reports or other institutional evidence.

Clear separations:

Forecast is not Fact.

- Facts are source-backed events.
- Forecast is future or uncertain interpretation.

Forecast is not Compensation.

- Compensation interprets money under rules.
- Forecast may estimate possible direction only when evidence and rules support it.

Forecast is not Business Plan.

- Forecast asks what may happen.
- Business Planning asks what must be done.

Forecast is not Revenue Pipeline.

- Pipeline is opportunity state.
- Forecast estimates likely movement or materialization from pipeline and other signals.

Forecast is not Productivity.

- Productivity interprets activity-to-output conversion.
- Forecast estimates future output or risk using productivity signals.

Forecast is not Conservation.

- Conservation interprets durability and quality.
- Forecast consumes conservation risk and confirmed snapshots.


3. Domain Purpose

Forecast Intelligence enables better decisions before official outcomes arrive.

It helps answer:

- Is the advisor likely to meet expected output?
- Is the manager unit likely to miss a target?
- Is pipeline strong enough to support expected production?
- Is productivity trend supporting future output?
- Is conservation risk weakening future durability?
- Is business planning based on realistic assumptions?
- Is forecast confidence low due to missing evidence?

Consumers:

Advisor:

- Uses Forecast to understand future risk, gaps and next action.

Manager:

- Uses Forecast to prioritize coaching, intervention and resource allocation.

Partner:

- Uses Forecast to evaluate unit capacity, recruitment/development/productivity trajectory and future risk.

Business Planning:

- Consumes Forecast to convert Goal to Forecast to Gap to Actions.

Economic Motivation:

- Consumes Forecast only when economic translation is rule-backed and evidence-backed.

Compensation:

- May consume Forecast context for pending or estimated explanation, but confirmed compensation remains Compensation's domain.

Revenue Intelligence:

- Feeds pipeline and opportunity context into Forecast.

Advisor Experience:

- Explains Forecast without creating anxiety, false certainty or perceived punishment.

Operational improvement:

- Forecast should help advisors and managers act earlier.
- Forecast should reduce surprise.
- Forecast should expose uncertainty.
- Forecast should prevent planning based only on hope or raw pipeline.

7. Boundary with Productivity Intelligence

How Forecast consumes productivity:

- Uses productivity trend.
- Uses productivity bottleneck.
- Uses Productive Advisor Capacity signal.
- Uses output repeatability.
- Uses productivity risk.
- Uses productivity quality interpretation when available.

Avoiding confusion:

- Productive Advisor Capacity is current/observed interpretation.
- Forecast estimates future trajectory from that signal.

Productivity trend:

- Helps Forecast detect whether output trajectory is improving, flat or declining.

Productivity risk:

- Reduces confidence or triggers forecast risk.

Repeatable output vs fragile output:

- Repeatable output may support stronger forecast.
- Fragile output must be checked against Conservation before being treated as durable.

Rule:

Productivity says what is converting.
Forecast estimates what could occur next.


8. Boundary with Conservation Intelligence

How Forecast consumes conservation risk:

- Uses conservation risk to reduce confidence or adjust durability expectation.
- Uses policy quality signals to avoid over-projecting fragile output.
- Uses official conservation snapshots for confirmed historical quality.

How Forecast handles LIMRA/IGC lag:

- Separates local predictive conservation risk from institutional historical snapshots.
- Labels pending official reports.
- Updates forecast when late reports or corrections arrive.

Local vs official:

- Local conservation risk can influence action and forecast confidence.
- Official conservation snapshot can confirm or correct forecast assumptions.

Rule:

Forecast must not project fragile production as durable without Conservation context.


9. Boundary with Revenue Intelligence

How Forecast consumes pipeline:

- Uses active opportunities, pipeline movement, pipeline aging and opportunity status.

Avoiding confusion:

- Pipeline is not forecast.
- Pipeline is an input.

Stalled opportunities:

- Reduce forecast confidence.
- May trigger forecast risk or gap signal.

Pipeline aging:

- Indicates possible weakening of near-term production forecast.

Revenue opportunity vs production forecast:

- Revenue Intelligence says where potential money can come from.
- Forecast estimates what part may materialize under evidence, behavior, productivity, conservation and period context.

Rule:

Revenue says where money can come from.
Forecast estimates what may materialize.


10. Boundary with Compensation Intelligence

When Forecast can speak about probable income:

- Only when Compensation rules exist.
- Only when RuleSnapshot exists.
- Only when evidence supports the estimate.
- Only when confidence and uncertainty are explicit.

When Forecast must not speak about income:

- No compensation rules.
- No evidence.
- No RuleSnapshot.
- Missing production facts.
- Weak or local-only signals.
- Unclear period.
- Conservation dependency unresolved.

Avoiding income guarantee:

- Forecast must label possible, estimated, pending or uncertain outcomes.
- Forecast must never say income is guaranteed.

Compensation estimate vs confirmed compensation:

- Estimate is Forecast or Economic Motivation context.
- Confirmed compensation is Compensation Intelligence.

Economic Motivation connection:

- Economic Motivation may translate forecast gaps into money only with compensation rules, evidence and confidence.

Rule:

Forecast does not calculate compensation.
Compensation interprets money under rules.
Economic Motivation translates gaps with guardrails.

13. Forecast Period Model

Daily local signal:

- Purpose: detect immediate behavior or pipeline movement changes.
- Inputs: Mick signals, pipeline movement, daily activity.
- Consumers: Advisor, Manager, Advisor Experience.
- Risk: too noisy; must not be official forecast.

Weekly forecast review:

- Purpose: review short-cycle trajectory and coaching risks.
- Inputs: productivity trend, pipeline movement, activity patterns.
- Consumers: Manager, Advisor, Partner.
- Risk: incomplete production confirmation.

Monthly forecast:

- Purpose: evaluate near-term output trajectory.
- Inputs: production facts, productivity signals, pipeline, conservation risk.
- Consumers: Advisor, Manager, Business Planning.
- Risk: conservation and compensation lag.

Quarterly forecast:

- Purpose: support manager, Partner and business planning cycles.
- Inputs: monthly trends, productivity, conservation, pipeline, goals.
- Consumers: Manager, Partner, Business Planning.
- Risk: official reports may arrive after quarter.

Semester forecast:

- Purpose: support medium-cycle planning, career/contest/partner context where relevant.
- Inputs: productivity trend, production facts, conservation, career stage, pipeline.
- Consumers: Partner, Business Planning, Forecast review.
- Risk: Rule Pack boundaries may apply.

Annual forecast:

- Purpose: support annual planning and capacity outlook.
- Inputs: historical performance, current pipeline, productivity, conservation, business goals.
- Consumers: Business Planning, Partner, Manager.
- Risk: too many assumptions; confidence must be explicit.

Lagged forecast correction:

- Purpose: adjust prior forecast after official or late evidence arrives.
- Inputs: carrier reports, official indexes, late Production Events, corrections.
- Consumers: Forecast, Business Planning, Compensation context.
- Risk: silent mutation of history.

16. Confidence and Evidence Model

High confidence:

- Strong evidence, stable source, sufficient history, clear period, low lag and rule-backed where rules apply.
- No numeric threshold approved.

Medium confidence:

- Some evidence exists, but assumptions, lag, source quality or data completeness remain limited.

Low confidence:

- Weak evidence, incomplete source, unstable trend, unclear period or significant lag.

Insufficient evidence:

- Forecast should not present a directional conclusion beyond stating evidence is insufficient.

Pending institutional confirmation:

- Local signals exist but official report is pending.

Local predictive only:

- Based on local behavior, pipeline, productivity or conservation risk.
- Useful for action, not official truth.

Source-backed:

- Supported by identifiable SourceSystem and EvidenceRecord.

Rule-backed:

- Supported by RuleSnapshot.

Historical trend backed:

- Supported by prior observed performance, but still not guaranteed.

Rule:

- Forecast must always expose confidence and evidence state.

- Mick signals weaken future trajectory.

Productivity declines:

- Productivity trend weakens expected future output.

Conservation risk increases:

- Future durability decreases.

Official report contradicts local forecast:

- Institutional historical truth corrects local predictive view.

Forecast based on weak evidence:

- Confidence must drop or forecast should be withheld.

Forecast ignores lag:

- Forecast overstates certainty.

Forecast ignores product line:

- Product-specific differences may distort forecast.

Forecast ignores advisor stage:

- Career context may make assumptions invalid.

Forecast ignores attribution:

- Forecast may assign future outcome to wrong advisor/manager/partner.

Forecast used as fact:

- Critical governance failure.

Forecast used as compensation promise:

- Critical financial and trust risk.

21. Red Team Review

Scenario: High pipeline, low productivity.

- Failure mode: Forecast overestimates future production from pipeline.
- Impact: Advisor and manager believe output is likely when conversion is weak.
- Mitigation: Forecast consumes Productivity trend and bottleneck.

Scenario: High productivity, weak conservation.

- Failure mode: Forecast projects durable future output from fragile production.
- Impact: Future income and planning are overstated.
- Mitigation: Forecast consumes Conservation risk and quality signals.

Scenario: High activity, no output.

- Failure mode: Mick signals create false forecast optimism.
- Impact: Activity is mistaken for future production.
- Mitigation: Forecast requires Productivity conversion signals.

Scenario: Low activity, one big case.

- Failure mode: Forecast overprojects from isolated output.
- Impact: Productive trajectory is overstated.
- Mitigation: Require repeatability and evidence context.

Scenario: Forecast says on track, institutional report later contradicts.

- Failure mode: Local predictive forecast treated as confirmed truth.
- Impact: Loss of trust and bad planning.
- Mitigation: Correction event and local-vs-confirmed labeling.

Scenario: Manager treats forecast as fact.

- Failure mode: Manager overreacts or punishes based on uncertain estimate.
- Impact: Bad coaching and trust erosion.
- Mitigation: Alerts include confidence and evidence status.

Scenario: Advisor treats forecast as guaranteed income.

- Failure mode: Forecast becomes income promise.
- Impact: Financial expectation risk.
- Mitigation: No guaranteed income language.

Scenario: Forecast ignores product mix.

- Failure mode: Product differences distort projection.
- Impact: Invalid forecast.
- Mitigation: Consume product line context and RuleSnapshot where applicable.

Scenario: Forecast ignores policy cancellation.

- Failure mode: Cancelled output remains in future expectation.
- Impact: Overstated future result.
- Mitigation: Consume Production Events and Conservation corrections.

Scenario: Forecast ignores reporting lag.

- Failure mode: Pending reports appear as missing or confirmed result.
- Impact: Wrong confidence.
- Mitigation: Reporting lag model and pending institutional confirmation state.

Scenario: Forecast creates too many alerts.

- Failure mode: Advisor and manager ignore noise.
- Impact: Alert fatigue.
- Mitigation: Alert thresholds require future hardening; no numeric thresholds in this PAQ.

Scenario: Forecast loops with Mick/Manager action.

- Failure mode: Forecast alert triggers action, action changes Mick, Mick changes Forecast repeatedly.
- Impact: Overload and feedback loops.
- Mitigation: Period snapshots, throttled refresh, event/snapshot separation.
