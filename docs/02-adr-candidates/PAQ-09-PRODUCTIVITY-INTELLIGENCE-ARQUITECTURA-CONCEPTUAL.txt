1. Executive Summary

- Productivity Intelligence es el dominio de Forge OS que explica si la actividad y ejecucion del asesor se convierten en output comercial significativo, repetible y durable.

- Productivity Intelligence no es:
  - Mick.
  - Behavior Intelligence.
  - Production Events.
  - Advisor Development.
  - Compensation Intelligence.
  - Revenue Intelligence.
  - Manager Intelligence.
  - Career Intelligence.
  - Conservation Intelligence.

- Productivity Intelligence vive entre ejecucion y resultado comercial:
  - Mick mide si el asesor ejecuto conductas.
  - Productivity interpreta si esas conductas produjeron output.
  - Production Events registran hechos de produccion.
  - Compensation interpreta dinero bajo reglas.
  - Manager Intelligence consume productividad para coaching, riesgo y accion tactica.

- Cadena conceptual:
  - Mick.
  - Execution Behavior.
  - Activity Signals.
  - Productivity Intelligence.
  - Output Conversion.
  - Production Events.
  - Compensation / Contest / Conservation / Revenue Interpretations.

- Productivity Intelligence existe para evitar errores de arquitectura:
  - Actividad no es productividad.
  - Training no es desarrollo.
  - First Production no es productividad durable.
  - Compensation no es productividad.
  - Revenue forecast no es output confirmado.
  - Manager bonus no es salud real de productividad.

- Resultado de PAQ-09:
  - Productivity Intelligence es un dominio valido en estado ARCHITECTURE CANDIDATE.
  - Debe pasar por hardening antes de cualquier engine, UI o consumo operativo formal.


2. Domain Purpose

- Proposito central:
  - Explicar si el trabajo comercial del asesor se convierte en output productivo.

- Preguntas que responde:
  - El asesor es productivo?
  - El asesor se esta volviendo productivo?
  - La actividad esta convirtiendo?
  - La produccion se repite?
  - La productividad mejora, se estanca o cae?
  - Que bottleneck existe entre comportamiento y output?
  - La productividad esta concentrada en un producto, fuente, cluster o periodo?
  - La primera produccion se esta convirtiendo en productividad repetible?
  - El asesor tiene mucha actividad pero poco output?
  - El asesor produce pese a senales debiles de comportamiento?
  - Que debe hacer el asesor para mejorar productividad?
  - Que debe coachar el manager?

- Valor arquitectonico:
  - Protege a Forge de confundir acciones con resultados.
  - Permite feedback accionable para asesor y manager.
  - Entrega senales a Partner Intelligence, Advisor Development, Revenue, Career, Business Planning y Economic Motivation.
  - Evita que Compensation o Manager Intelligence calculen productividad por su cuenta.

- Principio central:
  - Productividad no es existencia de actividad.
  - Productividad es conversion de actividad en output comercial significativo.


3. Boundaries

3.1 Que es Productivity Intelligence

- Es:
  - Interpretacion de dominio sobre output comercial del asesor.
  - Puente entre comportamiento y produccion.
  - Medicion de efectividad comercial.
  - Consumidor de senales de Mick.
  - Consumidor de Production Events.
  - Consumidor de contexto de Advisor Development.
  - Productor de Productivity Risk.
  - Productor de Productivity Status.
  - Productor de senales para managers, partners y planning.

3.2 Que no es Productivity Intelligence

- No es:
  - Behavior tracking.
  - Task ownership.
  - Compensation calculation.
  - Contest qualification.
  - Revenue forecast.
  - Business plan ownership.
  - Advisor Experience.
  - Career stage ownership.
  - Conservation index ownership.
  - Manager bonus calculation.
  - Raw Production Events.

3.3 Boundary con Mick

- Mick posee:
  - Behavior execution.
  - Activity completion.
  - Consistency of actions.
  - Follow-up discipline.
  - Coaching responsiveness.
  - Prospecting cadence.
  - Execution gaps.

- Productivity posee:
  - Si el comportamiento produce output.
  - Activity-to-output conversion.
  - Productivity trend.
  - Productivity bottleneck.
  - Productive advisor interpretation.
  - Output consistency.

- Ejemplo:
  - Mick: el asesor completo follow-ups.
  - Productivity: esos follow-ups si o no se estan convirtiendo en citas, propuestas, polizas, prima o produccion.

3.4 Boundary con Production Events

- Production Events posee:
  - POLICY_ISSUED.
  - POLICY_PAID.
  - PAYMENT_POSTED.
  - POLICY_CANCELLED.
  - POLICY_REINSTATED.
  - COMMISSION_POSTED cuando la fuente lo reporta como hecho.

- Productivity posee:
  - Interpretacion de esos hechos como output performance.
  - Repeatability of production.
  - Conversion de actividad a produccion.
  - Productivity status.

- Ejemplo:
  - Production Event: POLICY_PAID.
  - Productivity: esa poliza pagada contribuye al output del asesor en el periodo correcto, bajo PeriodSnapshot y RuleSnapshot aplicables.

3.5 Boundary con Compensation

- Compensation posee:
  - Commission calculation.
  - Bonus calculation.
  - Accrual.
  - Payment.
  - Adjustment.
  - Reversal.
  - Compensation explanation.

- Productivity posee:
  - Si el output del asesor es productivo.
  - Si el output es repetible.
  - Si la actividad convierte en produccion.

- Guardrail:
  - Productivity no calcula ingresos.
  - Productivity no infiere bonos.
  - Productivity no usa comision pagada como score de productividad sin contexto de reglas.

3.6 Boundary con Advisor Development

- Advisor Development posee:
  - Transformacion de Signed Advisor a Productive Advisor Capacity.
  - Development progress.
  - First production readiness.
  - Coachability as transformation.
  - Development risk.

- Productivity posee:
  - Output cuando la actividad comercial ya puede evaluarse como performance.
  - Repeatable production.
  - Activity-to-output effectiveness.

- Senales compartidas:
  - First production.
  - First appointment.
  - Repeat activity.
  - Consistency.
  - Early production.

- Regla:
  - First Production es milestone de desarrollo.
  - Repeatable output es evidencia de productividad.

3.7 Boundary con Manager Intelligence

- Manager Intelligence consume:
  - Productivity status.
  - Productivity risk.
  - Productivity concentration.
  - Advisor output trends.
  - Team productivity distribution.

- Productivity posee:
  - Interpretacion de productividad a nivel advisor y cohort.

- Manager Intelligence posee:
  - Manager action.
  - Coaching priority.
  - Team risk.
  - Unit interpretation.
  - Advisor intervention priority.

8. Lag & Periodicity

8.1 Naturaleza multi-speed

- Productivity tiene varias velocidades:
  - Behavior puede actualizar daily o near real-time.
  - Pipeline puede actualizar daily.
  - Production Events actualizan cuando llegan source facts.
  - Compensation puede actualizar despues de close o payment.
  - Conservation puede actualizar con reporting lag.

8.2 Local Productivity View

- Proposito:
  - Ayudar al advisor y manager a actuar temprano.

- Inputs:
  - Mick behavior signals.
  - Pipeline movement.
  - Appointments.
  - Follow-ups.
  - Early production events.

- Naturaleza:
  - Local predictive or near-current view.

- Riesgo:
  - No debe presentarse como institutional historical truth.

8.3 Institutional Productivity Confirmation

- Proposito:
  - Confirmar production con hechos oficiales o source-backed.

- Inputs:
  - Policy paid.
  - Carrier report.
  - Payment posted.
  - Official production report.

- Naturaleza:
  - Institutional historical truth.

- Riesgo:
  - Puede llegar tarde.

8.4 Periodicity model

- Daily:
  - Activity execution.
  - Follow-ups.
  - Contact attempts.
  - Appointment movement.
  - Pipeline action.

- Weekly:
  - Activity-to-output trend.
  - Pipeline conversion.
  - Advisor productivity risk.
  - Manager coaching priorities.

- Monthly:
  - Production output.
  - Productivity trend.
  - Productive advisor status.
  - Career stage alignment.

- Quarterly:
  - Manager unit productivity.
  - Partner capacity.
  - Business plan progress.

- Lagged:
  - Conservation-informed quality.
  - LIMRA.
  - IGC.
  - Official reports.

8.5 Lag risks

- Riesgos:
  - Productivity appears low before official production posts.
  - Productivity appears high before cancellations or conservation issues emerge.
  - Manager acts too late if waiting for official reports.
  - Compensation appears to validate productivity even when output is concentrated or fragile.

- Guardrail:
  - Productivity debe mostrar current local view.
  - Productivity debe mostrar confirmed historical view.
  - Productivity debe mostrar evidence status.
  - Productivity debe mostrar confidence.
  - Productivity debe mostrar reporting lag.


9. Risks

9.1 Activity equals Productivity

- Riesgo:
  - Forge trata alta actividad como alta productividad.

- Correccion:
  - Activity pertenece a Mick.
  - Productivity requiere output conversion.

9.2 First Output equals Durable Productivity

- Riesgo:
  - Forge trata first production como productividad durable.

- Correccion:
  - First production es milestone.
  - Productivity requiere repeatability y output interpretation.

9.3 Compensation equals Productivity

- Riesgo:
  - Forge trata ingreso como productividad.

- Correccion:
  - Compensation es ruled economic interpretation.
  - Productivity es output effectiveness.

9.4 Production Events equal Productivity

- Riesgo:
  - Forge trata raw production facts como productividad sin periodo, contexto o repeatability.

- Correccion:
  - Production Events son hechos.
  - Productivity es interpretacion.

9.5 Manager Intelligence owns Productivity

- Riesgo:
  - Manager domain calcula productivity para team alerts.

- Correccion:
  - Manager consume productivity.
  - Productivity owns productivity interpretation.

9.6 Revenue Intelligence owns Productivity

- Riesgo:
  - Revenue pipeline se confunde con productivity.

- Correccion:
  - Revenue owns opportunities.
  - Productivity owns activity-to-output performance.

9.7 Conservation lag ignored

- Riesgo:
  - Produccion de baja calidad se cuenta como productividad durable.

- Correccion:
  - Conservation signals deben informar productivity quality con lag documentado.

9.8 Rule Pack leakage

- Riesgo:
  - Productivity thresholds se hardcodean en Core.

- Correccion:
  - Rule Packs definen thresholds.
  - Core define primitives y ownership.

9.9 Duplicate metrics

- Riesgo:
  - Activity conversion se calcula en Mick, Revenue, Manager y Productivity por separado.

- Correccion:
  - One metric, one owner.
  - Productivity owns productivity conversion.

9.10 False alerts

- Riesgo:
  - Alertas se disparan por evidencia incompleta, lag, vista local o ownership ambiguo.

- Correccion:
  - Alertas deben incluir evidence status, confidence, source y si el signal es local predictive o institutionally confirmed.


10. Alerts & Feedback

10.1 Advisor alerts

- Posibles alertas al advisor:
  - Activity is happening but not creating appointments.
  - Appointments are happening but not creating opportunities.
  - Opportunities are not reaching policy stage.
  - First production has not repeated.
  - Follow-ups are not converting.
  - Pipeline is aging.
  - Productivity is concentrated in one product or source.
  - Local productivity is improving but not yet confirmed by institutional reporting.
  - Production exists but quality may be at risk pending conservation signals.

- Cada alerta debe incluir:
  - What happened.
  - Why it matters.
  - What to do next.
  - Confidence or evidence status.

10.2 Manager alerts

- Posibles alertas al manager:
  - Advisor has high activity but low output conversion.
  - Advisor has low activity and declining productivity.
  - Advisor produced once but has no repeat activity.
  - Advisor pipeline is aging.
  - Advisor productivity dropped from prior period.
  - Team productivity concentrated in one advisor.
  - Cohort development is not becoming productivity.
  - Official reporting lag may hide current productivity risk.

10.3 Feedback style

- Feedback al advisor debe ser:
  - Actionable.
  - Specific.
  - Non-punitive.
  - Economically clear when rules support it.
  - Connected to next behavior.

- Feedback al manager debe ser:
  - Prioritized.
  - Coaching-oriented.
  - Tied to evidence.
  - Separated from compensation unless rules support it.

10.4 Advisor Experience integration

- Clippy puede explicar:
  - Why activity is not productivity.
  - Why first production is not enough.
  - Why pipeline aging matters.
  - Why productivity is not confirmed yet.
  - Why a manager is recommending a coaching action.

- Candy Crush Experience puede adaptar:
  - Simpler next actions for low-execution advisors.
  - Deeper productivity diagnostics for advanced advisors.
  - Reduced complexity when frustration or low learning is detected.

  - No proyecta bonus.

12.7 Career Intelligence

- Career posee:
  - Career stage.
  - Career Month.
  - Career classification.
  - Stage transitions.

- Productivity provee:
  - Productivity interpretation when career stage, promotion, classification or progression depends on productivity.

- Rule Pack debe definir:
  - Any career thresholds.
  - Any productivity requirements.
  - Any promotion rules.

12.8 Economic Motivation

- Economic Motivation consume productivity signals.

- Productivity puede identificar:
  - Activity-to-output gap.
  - Pipeline conversion gap.
  - Repeat production gap.
  - Productivity decline.
  - Productive capacity risk.

- Economic Motivation puede traducir a:
  - Potential income impact.
  - Income at risk.
  - Expected income range.
  - Economic urgency.

- Solo cuando existan:
  - Compensation rules.
  - RuleSnapshot.
  - Evidence.
  - Sufficient confidence.
  - Explicit forecast uncertainty.

- Guardrails:
  - No invented financial values.
  - No guaranteed income.
  - No bonus projection without Compensation rules.
  - No productivity-to-money translation without evidence.
