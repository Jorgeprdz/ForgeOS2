Status: CONSTITUTIONAL DISCOVERY COMPLETE / NO RATIFICATION / NO IMPLEMENTATION

## Executive decision

**KEEP IN DISCOVERY**

The evidence does not justify making Russell a constitutional domain, Council seat, runtime or authority. The claimed problem is plausible but not demonstrated. This decision is definitive for the current evidence state. It does not prohibit future discovery. It prohibits authority inflation from the existing Russell documents.

## 1. Constitutional problem

### Claimed problem

Russell discovery claims that strategically correct language can sound generic, corporate or unlike the advisor. The proposed concern is loss of authentic expression when Nash-generated guidance is rendered into language.

### Claimed architectural gap

The discovery proposes a gap between:

- conversation strategy and drafting owned by Nash; and
- user-specific expression described as personal voice or communication identity.

### Why existing authorities may not completely answer it

- Nash owns conversation intelligence and language drafts, so it already owns much of the surface where the problem would be solved.
- Hitch is a Council rapport lens, not a ratified truth-owning domain or runtime contract.
- Miranda evaluates quality and constitutional discipline, not individual linguistic preferences.
- Mick owns observable behavior boundaries, not message expression.
- Andrey's restricted Human Capital Allocation scope has no necessary relationship to authentic communication.
- Advisor Experience may teach use of a Nash draft but ADR-016 explicitly preserves Nash ownership of language drafts.

### Evidence quality

No objective evidence demonstrates that the proposed gap requires a new authority:

- no advisor study;
- no corpus with explicit consent;
- no comparison of Nash drafts against advisor-confirmed preferences;
- no demonstrated adoption failure caused by voice mismatch;
- no evidence that Nash cannot safely contain expression adaptation;
- no privacy impact assessment;
- no validated identity or drift metric.

The current evidence demonstrates a hypothesis, not a constitutional necessity.

## 2. Domain

### Candidate domain

The narrowest defensible candidate domain is **advisor-controlled communication-expression compatibility**.

It could evaluate whether a draft's observable expression aligns with advisor-confirmed communication preferences while preserving the source strategy, evidence and authority boundaries.

### It would not govern

- personality;
- identity as a person;
- values, character, worth or potential;
- client intent or consent;
- rapport truth;
- conversation strategy;
- next best action;
- product, policy, forecast or compensation truth;
- behavior coaching;
- hiring, development-resource allocation or manager judgment;
- message sending or external execution.

### Candidate boundary

Its possible scope would begin only after a governed Nash draft exists and end before human approval or delivery. This boundary remains hypothetical and does not grant authority.

### Responsibilities it could never assume

It could never infer a person's authentic self, silently imitate them, create strategy, manipulate emotional tone, fabricate relationship context, determine effectiveness, approve sending or override the advisor.

---

## 3. Constitutional purpose

The only constitutionally defensible purpose would be:

> Preserve the advisor's agency over personal expression when Forge explains or drafts language, without changing strategy, evidence, truth ownership or human authority.

This is an architectural purpose, not a feature list. It remains unratified.

## 4. Responsibilities

### Candidate exclusive responsibilities

None are proven exclusive today. The evidence does not establish a responsibility Nash cannot own.

### Candidate shared responsibilities

- With Nash: expression of a governed conversation draft.
- With Hitch: human reception and rapport considerations.
- With Advisor Experience: transparency and user control over suggested language.
- With privacy governance: lawful/minimized use of communication evidence.
- With Miranda: quality, non-manipulation and strategy-preservation review.

### Delegated responsibilities

If ever authorized, all truth, strategy, evidence, consent, approval and delivery responsibilities must remain delegated to their existing owners. Russell could not absorb them.

## 5. Authority

Under the current evidence:

- Takes decisions: **No**.
- Vetoes human action: **No**.
- Warns: **Not authorized**; a future capability could present a non-binding compatibility warning.
- Blocks: **No**. Only a governed validator may block an invalid system output, never a legitimate human decision.
- Modifies canonical information: **No**.
- Modifies strategy: **No**.
- Modifies a draft: **Not authorized**. A future contract could create an alternative expression candidate without overwriting the Nash source draft.
- Revocation: the advisor must be able to reject/correct any expression candidate; Miranda/ROBOCOP/Board can block the capability; source owners can invalidate inputs.

Russell currently has `AUTHORITY=NONE`.

## 6. Relationship with Nash

### Nash produces

ADR-010 and ADR-016 establish that Nash owns conversation intelligence and language drafts. Nash may produce conversation support, draft language, tone options and related explanations, all subject to evidence and human authority.

### Candidate Russell input

Only a governed Nash draft plus immutable strategy/intent constraints and advisor-authorized preference evidence could be considered.

### Candidate Russell output

At most, a clearly labeled alternative-expression candidate and compatibility explanation. The original Nash draft and strategy must remain unchanged and traceable.

### Strategy modification

Prohibited. Changing objective, recommendation, claim strength, product meaning, client interpretation, call to action or evidence would cross into Nash/other owners.

### Expression modification

This is the only plausible candidate scope, but it is not yet proven to require an independent owner.

### Authenticity versus effectiveness conflict

No automated winner is permitted. Nash cannot force “effective” language that violates human agency; a Russell-like capability cannot weaken truth or strategy in the name of authenticity. The advisor decides among transparent alternatives. If safety, evidence or non-manipulation fails, the system output is invalid regardless of style.

### Ownership finding

Because Nash already owns language drafts, the default architecture should test expression adaptation as a bounded Nash capability before proposing a separate domain.

## 9. Relationship with Hitch

## 11. Identity model

The discovery's “authentic self,” “communication DNA,” “professional mask” and stable-trait claims are not constitutionally supported.

The only safe candidate definition is:

> Communication expression preference is a user-correctable, context-bound description of observable choices in language. It is not the person's identity, personality or inner truth.

### Permanent elements

None may be declared permanent from the available evidence.

### Contextual elements

Potentially observable and user-correctable dimensions include channel, language/locale, formality, verbosity, cadence and directly stated tone preference.

### Elements never alterable by Forge

- personal values;
- identity labels;
- protected characteristics;
- moral character;
- consent;
- intent;
- advisor-authored source history;
- canonical Nash strategy or factual claims.

### Adaptable elements

Only surface expression explicitly authorized by the advisor, with the original preserved. No adaptation is canonical until the advisor accepts it.

## 12. Identity Drift

### Definition

Candidate-only: the difference between observable expression features in a draft and advisor-confirmed, context-relevant communication preferences.

It must not mean distance from a person's “true identity.”

### Metrics

No canonical drift metric exists. The RUSSELL-002 score is unvalidated.

### Levels and thresholds

The proposed Low `0.0–0.2`, Medium `0.3–0.6` and High `0.7–1.0` thresholds are discovery hypotheses. They are not adopted because no calibration data, owner, error analysis or human validation exists.

### Consequences

No automatic consequence is authorized. A future validated assessment could offer an explanation or alternative draft. It could not veto, block, send or alter strategy.

### Events

No canonical Russell event names or event ownership exist. Event definition is deferred until a domain/contract decision exists.

- context and language separation;
- no demographic proxies;

- provenance and purpose metadata;
- testing across advisors, channels and communication contexts;
- independent privacy and Miranda review.

## 17. Risks

### Risks of existence

- creates a false model of a person's “real identity”;
- normalizes surveillance of private communication;
- freezes users into past linguistic patterns;
- stereotypes by language, culture or channel;
- duplicates Nash ownership;
- turns style into opaque scoring;
- teaches advisors to depend on imitation;
- changes strategy while claiming to change only expression.

### Risks of absence

- some drafts may feel generic or corporate;
- advisors may spend more effort editing language;
- adoption may suffer if drafts repeatedly mismatch stated preferences.

These absence risks are plausible but not empirically demonstrated.

### Abuse risks

- impersonation;
- covert persuasion optimization;
- manager monitoring of personal communication;
- psychological/personality profiling;
- reuse of private client data;
- synthetic messages presented as advisor-authored.

### Constitutional risks

- Article 0 dependency and loss of human judgment;
- ADR-001 weak/unauthorized evidence;
- ADR-002 duplicate ownership;
- ADR-003 hidden transfer of authority;
- ADR-004 invented identity claims;
- ADR-010 collision with Nash;
- ADR-011 manipulation and consent;

- new runtime before public contracts;
- circular Nash–Russell rewriting;

## 20. Final recommendation

# KEEP IN DISCOVERY

### Justification

The problem is plausible but not objectively demonstrated. Russell has no exclusive constitutional invariant, no valid evidence model, no privacy authority, no calibrated metrics, no public contracts and no implementation dependency. Nash already owns conversation intelligence and language drafts. Hitch remains an advisory rapport lens. No evidence proves a new Council seat or runtime is necessary.

### Evidence

- Every Russell source labels itself discovery/no implementation.
- Build Tree marks Russell pending and prohibits implementation without ratification.
- ADR-016 says Nash owns conversation intelligence and language drafts.
- The discovery itself leaves governance, privacy, archetype and seat placement open.
- Candidate drift thresholds and identity dimensions have no validation evidence.
- The current repository already explored tone/channel/message adaptation without establishing Russell as an owner.

### Risks accepted by keeping discovery

Forge may continue to produce generic drafts while the hypothesis is tested. This is lower constitutional risk than creating identity surveillance, duplicate ownership or an unvalidated authority.

### Architectural impact

- No Russell domain.
- No Russell Council seat.
- No Russell runtime.
- No Russell persistence, interfaces, schemas, events or metrics.
- Nash ownership remains unchanged.
- A bounded Nash expression-adaptation experiment may be proposed only as privacy-reviewed discovery, not production implementation.

### Constitutional impact

No Constitution, ADR, Council or authority changes occur. Russell remains traceable historical discovery evidence.

### Rewrite Pack changes

- Retain Russell discovery evidence under `07-authority-ratification/evidence/discovery/`.
- Retain `RUSSELL_STATUS_DECISION.md` and this complete discovery.
- Keep `RUSSELL_AUTHORITY=NONE` in the authority matrix.

### ADR actions

- Create now: **None**.
- Modify: **None**.
- Close: **None**.
- If future evidence demonstrates a distinct domain: propose a new `Communication Expression, Identity and Privacy Boundary` ADR only after user research, privacy policy, Nash overlap test and metric validation.

## Reopening criteria

Russell may be reconsidered only when all are available:

1. Evidence of a recurring advisor problem attributable specifically to expression mismatch.
2. Comparative proof that a bounded Nash capability cannot adequately own the problem.
3. Explicit advisor consent and privacy/retention/deletion policy.
4. User-correctable communication-preference semantics that do not claim inner identity.
5. Calibrated measurement with false-positive/negative analysis.
6. Strategy-preservation and non-manipulation tests.
7. Clear ownership and interface boundaries with Nash and Hitch.
8. Miranda and Board approval for constitutional review.
