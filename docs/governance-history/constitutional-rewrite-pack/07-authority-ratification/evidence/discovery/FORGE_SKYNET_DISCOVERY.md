# FORGE Skynet Discovery

Status: ARCHITECTURE DISCOVERY

Locked codename: SKYNET

Scope:

- No implementation.
- No code.
- No UI.
- No engines.
- No schemas.
- No Build Tree modifications.
- No ratification.
- No automatic approval.

## 1. Definition Boundary

Skynet is not:

- an AI
- an engine
- an assistant
- an intelligence domain
- a recommendation system

Skynet is the constitutional governance layer of Forge.

It is a collection of constitutional laws, boundaries and non-negotiable
principles that all current and future intelligence systems must obey.

## 2. Objective

Determine whether Forge requires a constitutional layer above all intelligence
systems.

Forge already contains and will continue to contain many intelligence systems:

- Career Intelligence
- Manager Intelligence
- Partner Intelligence
- Relationship Intelligence
- Forecast Intelligence
- Future Intelligence Domains

The architecture question is no longer only:

"What should an intelligence do?"

The governance question becomes:

"What must no intelligence ever be allowed to do?"

## 3. Executive Finding

Forge does require a constitutional governance layer above all intelligence
systems.

This layer should exist because intelligence systems can evolve faster than
constitutional doctrine. If future domains interpret people, careers, risk,
purpose, production, readiness, relationships or money, they need limits before
they generate recommendations.

Skynet should govern interpretation and recommendation.

It should prevent future intelligence systems from:

- judging without evidence
- reducing human growth to a score
- using purpose as pressure
- treating production as proof
- violating dignity
- turning manager visibility into surveillance
- hardcoding contextual truth into Forge Core

## 4. What Qualifies a Principle as a Skynet Law?

A principle qualifies as a Skynet Law candidate when it meets all or most of
these conditions:

- It applies across multiple intelligence domains.
- It prevents serious harm or trust collapse.
- It constrains both present and future systems.
- It protects human interpretation, dignity or evidence integrity.
- It remains valid even when engines and domains evolve.
- It is operationally testable as a boundary.
- It is not merely a product preference.
- It is not tied to one company, rule pack or local process.
- Violating it should block or invalidate an output.

Skynet Laws should be few, durable and hard to bypass.

## 5. Constitutional Truth vs Skynet Law

Constitutional Truth:

Describes what Forge is, believes or prioritizes.

Example:

"Forge is not a CRM."

Skynet Law:

Constrains what every intelligence is allowed to do.

Example:

"No intelligence may produce judgment without evidence."

Difference:

Constitutional Truths can be descriptive, directional or identity-forming.

Skynet Laws are governing constraints.

A Constitutional Truth may become a Skynet Law only if it functions as a
non-negotiable cross-domain constraint.

## 6. Can a Skynet Law Be Contextual?

Default answer:

No, not as a top-level Skynet Law.

Skynet Laws should be universal wherever possible.

Contextual principles can become:

- Skynet Policy
- Rule Pack Boundary
- Organization Profile Constraint
- Domain-Specific Governance Rule

Example:

"Latent demand must not justify manipulation" may be a contextual Skynet Policy
for financial advisory or SMNYL contexts.

It should not become a universal top-level Skynet Law unless validated across
Forge's broader scope.

## 7. Must All Skynet Laws Be Universal?

Top-level Skynet Laws should be universal.

Skynet may also support contextual governance below the law layer:

```text
Skynet Law
└── Contextual Policy
    ├── Rule Pack Constraint
    ├── Organization Profile Constraint
    └── Domain Boundary
```

This preserves Forge Core while allowing contextual enforcement.

## 8. Can Skynet Laws Be Modified?

Yes, but only through constitutional amendment.

Modification should require:

- explicit human review
- documented reason
- impact analysis across domains
- compatibility check with existing laws
- migration notes for affected intelligence systems
- ratification record

Skynet Laws should not be casually edited during implementation.

## 9. Who Ratifies a Skynet Law?

Ratification should not be automatic.

Candidate ratification authority:

- human constitutional review
- explicit PAQ approval
- repository-level documentation update
- future governance process if established

An engine, AI layer or intelligence domain must never self-ratify a Skynet Law.

## 10. What Happens When an Intelligence Conflicts With a Skynet Law?

The Skynet Law wins.

Expected behavior conceptually:

- block the output
- degrade to safer output
- mark the result as invalid
- request missing evidence
- require human review
- refuse the recommendation
- preserve an audit reason

No intelligence should override Skynet by claiming higher confidence.

## 11. Recommendations or Data Interpretation?

Skynet should govern both:

- data interpretation
- recommendations

Reason:

Recommendations are downstream of interpretation.

If an intelligence interprets production as proof, purpose as motivation score,
or title as career stage, the recommendation is already contaminated.

Skynet must constrain the interpretation layer before the recommendation layer.

## 12. How Many Skynet Laws Should Exist?

Few.

Recommended initial target:

3 to 7 top-level laws.

Too many laws create bureaucracy.

Too few laws leave dangerous gaps.

Skynet should be compact enough that every intelligence can be evaluated against
it.

## 13. What Prevents Skynet From Becoming Bureaucracy?

Skynet avoids bureaucracy if laws are:

- few
- universal
- blocking only when necessary
- tied to serious harm
- written as constraints, not procedures
- paired with simple conflict outcomes
- separated from implementation details
- not used to micromanage domain design

Skynet should not approve every feature.

Skynet should invalidate unsafe intelligence behavior.

## 14. Initial Candidate Laws

Initial candidates remain candidates only:

1. Evidence Before Judgment
2. Human Dignity Must Be Preserved
3. Purpose Is Context, Not Pressure
4. Production Is Evidence, Not Proof
5. No Single Score Represents Human Growth

These are strong because they are cross-domain, protective and repeated across
the constitutional consolidation.

This document does not ratify them.

## 15. Final Question

If Forge eventually accumulates decades of collective professional intelligence,
what constitutional protections must exist before Forge is trusted to interpret
that intelligence?

Answer:

Forge must have a law layer that prevents intelligence from becoming authority
without evidence, pressure without consent, scoring without dignity, or
interpretation without boundaries.

Before Forge is trusted with decades of professional intelligence, every
intelligence must obey:

- evidence requirements
- dignity protections
- purpose boundaries
- anti-reduction rules
- context boundaries
- human review paths for ambiguous or sensitive outputs

## 16. Discovery Verdict

CANDIDATE FOR LOCK AS GOVERNANCE LAYER.

Reason:

Skynet is not a new intelligence system. It is the constitutional constraint
layer required because Forge will contain many intelligence systems.

The concept is strong enough to proceed to future governance lock review, but no
law should be ratified until a separate Skynet Law ratification PAQ defines law
wording, amendment process, ownership and enforcement expectations.
