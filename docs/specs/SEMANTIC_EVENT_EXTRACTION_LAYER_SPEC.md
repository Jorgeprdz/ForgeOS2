# Semantic Event Extraction Layer Specification (Forge Alpha v0.2)

Status: DRAFT
Date: 2026-06-17
Version: 0.3

## 1. Purpose
Design a secondary extraction layer to supplement the deterministic engine, allowing Forge to interpret natural advisor language while strictly adhering to constitutional guardrails.

## 2. Constitutional Invariant
> "Semantic interpretation may expand what Forge notices. It must never expand what Forge claims to know."

## 3. Core Principles
- **Unknown Preference:** When interpretation is ambiguous, "Unknown" is preferred over speculative inference.
- **Semantic Ceiling:** Semantic extraction may increase *recall*, but must never exceed the maximum claimable knowledge supported by explicit evidence. It is permanently prohibited from inferring: emotions, personality, hidden intent, urgency based on vulnerability, psychological state, manipulation strategy, purchase probability, conversion likelihood, political/religious beliefs, or health status.
- **Duality of Truth:** Semantic extraction improves *recall* (noticing more). Truth Resolution ensures *precision* (knowing what is true).

## 4. Extraction Architecture
1. **Deterministic Extractor (Primary):** Rule-backed, auditable, and reproducible extraction. Accepted directly by the Ledger due to clear provenance and deterministic definition; confidence scores are metadata, not authority.
2. **Semantic Extractor (Fallback):** Interpretive extraction. Produces *candidate evidence* only. It must **NEVER** write directly to the Event Ledger.

## 5. Candidate Evidence & Adjudication
A semantic candidate may only become canonical truth if at least one of the following is true:
- **Deterministic Corroboration:** Confirmation by the primary deterministic engine.
- **Explicit Human Acceptance:** Advisor review and approval.
- **Ledger Corroboration:** Validation against existing, high-confidence ledger history.

## 6. Candidate Lifecycle & Expiration
- **Proposed:** Initially generated by the Semantic Extractor.
- **Accepted:** Promoted to the Ledger after corroboration or human acceptance.
- **Rejected:** Discarded by Human or Truth Resolution.
- **Expired:** Automatically invalidated if not processed within the review window (default: 72 hours).
  - Expired candidates never enter canonical truth.
  - Expired candidates remain auditable.
  - Expired candidates cannot be silently reactivated.

## 7. Human Acceptance Gate
- **Advisor Review Flow:** All semantic candidates are surfaced in the Advisor OS as "Proposed Events."
- **UI Examples:** Proposed events require an explicit "Accept" or "Reject" action, accompanied by an `evidence_span` preview.
- **Low-Confidence Handling:** Candidates with confidence below a defined threshold require mandatory advisor inspection.
- **Override Logging:** Any manual acceptance or rejection must be logged with the actor's ID and timestamp for auditability.

## 8. Evidence Span Integrity
Every semantic candidate **MUST** contain:
- Exact quoted `evidence_span`.
- Source offsets when available.
- Traceable provenance.
- **Invalidation:** If no `evidence_span` exists, the candidate is **INVALID**.

## 10. Implementation Recommendation
**Recommendation:** Semantic Extraction is approved for **Alpha v0.2 EXPERIMENTAL ONLY**. Full adoption into production or Beta is contingent upon rigorous, empirical validation of precision and recall metrics and a final constitutional review.
