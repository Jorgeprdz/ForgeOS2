# Forge Lariza Pedro Camarena Test

## Presentation Score

Score: 8.5 / 10

## Would This Help a Prospect Understand?

Yes.

The presentation starts with the human risk:

- A serious medical event can become a financial crisis.
- Lariza is young and healthy, which makes the timing relevant.
- The policy is about transferring large risk, not paying every small medical
  expense.

That helps her understand why the conversation matters before seeing numbers.

## Would This Help a Prospect Decide?

Yes, with one caveat.

It helps her decide because it frames the real decision:

"Do I want to carry this risk myself or transfer most of it?"

It also names the tradeoffs:

- Annual cost.
- First responsibility.
- National territory.
- Optional coverages.
- Accident protection.

Caveat:

The presentation would be stronger with one simple financial-impact example,
such as:

"If a hospital bill were very large, here is the part you would not want to
carry alone."

The quote alone does not include a concrete claim example, so Forge should not
invent one as fact.

## Would This Feel Like a Real Advisor Conversation?

Mostly yes.

It uses questions a real advisor would ask:

- What risk worries you?
- Who would be affected financially?
- Do you travel?
- Would $25,000 be manageable?
- What hospitals matter to you?

It avoids sounding like a brochure because it does not begin with:

- sum insured
- deductible
- coinsurance
- tabulator

It begins with why protection matters.

## Would This Feel Like an Insurance Brochure?

Not if delivered conversationally.

It could become brochure-like if the advisor reads it flatly or jumps too
quickly into product facts.

The advisor should slow down at the risk-transfer question:

"What part of this risk are you comfortable keeping yourself?"

That is the Pedro-style moment.

## What Is Impressive

- The conversation begins with Lariza's life, not the product.
- The quote is translated into decisions.
- Optional coverages are not treated as automatically included.
- Accident deductible elimination is explained as useful but conditional.
- The advisor receives questions, not just facts.

## What Is Still Missing

- Lariza's actual lifestyle.
- Her hospitals of preference.
- Her travel frequency.
- Whether she has family financial support.
- Her savings comfort level.
- Her attitude toward maternity planning.
- Whether annual payment is comfortable.
- Whether she wants to compare another deductible or plan level.

## Final Test Verdict

This would help Lariza understand and decide.

It would feel like a real advisor conversation if the advisor uses the quote as
a decision tool, not as a product brochure.

The next best improvement is a short, realistic consequence example built from
approved product rules and a clearly labeled scenario.
