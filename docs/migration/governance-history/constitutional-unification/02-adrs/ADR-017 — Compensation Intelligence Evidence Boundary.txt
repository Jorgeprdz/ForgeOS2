ADR-017 — COMPENSATION INTELLIGENCE EVIDENCE BOUNDARY

1. TITLE

ADR-017 — Compensation Intelligence Evidence Boundary

2. STATUS

Final

3. CONTEXT

Forge ya cuenta con ADR-001 a ADR-016.

ADR-017 define cómo Forge puede interpretar reglas de compensación, comisiones, bonos, concursos, anticipos, retroactivos, qualification status, periodos económicos de carrera y escenarios de ingreso sin inventar ingresos, sin confundir escenarios con pagos reales, sin usar compensación como presión comercial y sin redefinir Metric Truth, Economic Evidence, Forecast Truth o Authority Boundary.

Compensation Intelligence existe para responder:

¿Qué reglas, condiciones, snapshots, periodos, producción y eventos deben existir para afirmar algo sobre compensación, comisión, bono, anticipo, retroactivo o calificación económica?

Compensation Intelligence no responde:

- cuánto se pagó sin evidencia de pago
- cuánto se pagará con certeza
- qué ingreso está garantizado
- qué producto debe venderse por comisión
- qué cliente debe ser presionado por payout
- qué asesor vale más por ingreso
- qué manager puede castigar por brecha económica
- qué qualification status es final sin snapshot
- qué concurso aplica sin rule pack
- qué retroactivo existe sin reglas y evidencia suficiente

Sin Compensation Intelligence Evidence Boundary, Forge puede:

- inventar comisiones
- inventar bonos
- inventar ingresos
- inventar anticipos
- inventar retroactivos
- inventar qualification status
- inventar contest group
- confundir prima emitida con prima pagada
- confundir producción meta con producción real
- confundir comisión estimada con comisión pagada
- confundir bono posible con bono confirmado
- mezclar Life y GMM
- mezclar quarter, semester y month windows
- mezclar snapshots de periodos distintos
- tratar forecast como compensation truth
- usar compensation gap como presión comercial
- recomendar producto por payout contra Client First
- activar manager enforcement económico
- reducir advisor worth a ingreso

ADR-017 protege la frontera entre compensación evidenciada, compensación estimada, compensación proyectada, compensación devengada y compensación pagada.

4. DECISION

Compensation Intelligence es el owner conceptual de interpretación rule-based de compensación, comisiones, bonos, concursos, anticipos, retroactivos, eligibility económica y qualification status económico.

Compensation Intelligence puede interpretar rule packs oficiales, commission schedules, contest rules, production records, paid premium records, issued premium records, snapshots, periodos y evidence states para explicar estados de compensación.

Compensation Intelligence no puede inventar montos, porcentajes, reglas, bonos, concursos, calificaciones, grupos, pagos, anticipos o retroactivos.

Compensation Intelligence debe diferenciar:

- paid
- earned
- estimated
- projected
- provisional
- stale
- conflicting
- unknown

Compensation Intelligence puede calcular o explicar escenarios solo cuando existen reglas, periodos, snapshots, producción y evidencia económica suficiente.

Compensation scenario is not real payment.

Paid income requires payment evidence.

Earned compensation requires rule, production, period and eligibility evidence.

Projected compensation remains forecast-bound.

Unknown compensation remains unknown.

5. DEFINITIONS

Compensation Intelligence

Dominio responsable de interpretar reglas de compensación, eligibility, comisiones, bonos, concursos, anticipos, retroactivos, snapshots y estados económicos de carrera.

Compensation Evidence

Evidencia que permite sostener una afirmación sobre compensación. Debe conservar source, rule, period, snapshot, production basis, payment state, confidence y limits.

Commission Schedule

Documento o regla oficial que define condiciones de comisión para un producto, periodo, canal, carrera o rule pack.

Contest Rule Pack

Conjunto de reglas oficiales que define eligibility, grupos, periodos, producción reconocida, premios o bonos de concurso.

Compensation Rule Pack

Paquete de reglas aplicable a compensación, comisiones, bonos, anticipos, retroactivos o qualification económica.

RuleSnapshot

Versión preservada de una regla en un periodo específico. No debe recalcularse históricamente con reglas nuevas salvo análisis separado.

Compensation Period

Ventana temporal aplicable a cálculo o interpretación de compensación: month, quarter, semester, campaign, contest, career month u otro periodo explícito.

Production Event

Hecho comercial o económico registrado, como producción emitida, producción pagada, prima emitida, prima pagada, renovación, conservación, persistencia o evento de póliza.

Issued Premium

Prima emitida. No equivale automáticamente a prima pagada.

Paid Premium

Prima pagada con evidencia de pago.

Initial Premium

Prima asociada a emisión inicial o primer pago según regla aplicable.

Renewal Premium

Prima asociada a renovación según regla aplicable.

Commission Possible

Estado donde una comisión podría aplicar si reglas y evidencia se validan.

Estimated Commission

Comisión estimada bajo reglas y evidencia parcial o supuestos explícitos.

Earned Compensation

Compensación devengada bajo regla, producción, periodo y eligibility suficientes, pero no necesariamente pagada.

Paid Compensation

Compensación pagada con evidencia de pago.

Projected Compensation

Escenario futuro de compensación bajo supuestos. No es pago real ni devengado confirmado.

Bonus Possible

Estado donde un bono podría aplicar si se cumplen condiciones y evidence requirements.

Bonus Confirmed

Bono confirmado con rule pack, periodo, snapshot, eligibility y evidencia oficial suficiente.

Advance

Anticipo económico sujeto a reglas, condiciones, recuperación o ajuste, si aplica. No es ingreso final sin reglas.

Retroactive Difference

Diferencia potencial o confirmada por recalculation retroactivo con reglas y evidencia suficiente.

Qualification Status

Estado económico o de concurso/carrera calculado o confirmado bajo reglas, periodo y snapshot.

Contest Group

Grupo de concurso o eligibility. No debe inferirse sin rule pack y evidencia.

No Compensation Conclusion Possible

Estado donde falta regla, snapshot, producción, pago, periodo o evidencia suficiente para conclusión responsable.

8. INPUT BOUNDARIES

Compensation rule pack

Can define applicable rules.

Must not be treated as current if stale, superseded or period-mismatched.

Commission schedule

Can define commission conditions.

Must not become product recommendation or guaranteed payout.

Contest rule pack

Can define contest eligibility and groups.

Must not infer contest group without evidence.

Official production record

Can support compensation interpretation.

Must not be redefined by Compensation Intelligence.

Paid premium record

Can support paid-based compensation.

Must preserve payment evidence and period.

Issued premium record

Can support issued-production context.

Must not be treated as paid premium unless the rule explicitly allows it.

Policy Truth

Can provide policy states or events.

Must not be redefined by compensation desire.

Product Truth

Can provide product category, type or rules.

Must not become payout-first sales logic.

Economic Evidence

Can provide source-bound economic values.

Must not be duplicated or overwritten.

Metric Truth

Can provide official production, paid premium, issued premium or qualification metrics.

Must not be recalculated locally if an official metric owner exists.

Forecast scenarios

Can provide projected context.

Must remain projected and cannot become compensation truth.

Business Planning outputs

Can provide goals, gaps and constraints.

Must not become production evidence or payout mandate.

Productivity Metrics

Can provide activity or output context.

Must not prove compensation eligibility unless the rule explicitly depends on that metric.

Manager-confirmed context

Can clarify team, campaign or human context.

Must not become rule, payment or production truth.

Advisor-confirmed context

Can clarify advisor situation.

Must not become payment evidence or official qualification.

CRM records

Can provide candidate evidence.

Must preserve source and may require validation.

Conservation / persistence records

Can support rules that require conservation or persistence.

Must not be inferred from old policy status.

LIMRA / IGC records

Can be consumed when applicable.

Must not be treated as interchangeable unless a rule explicitly maps them.

Official qualification snapshot

Can support confirmed qualification status.

Must preserve period, version and source.

Campaign / semester / quarter / month context

Can define windows.

Must not be mixed.

13. COMPENSATION VS ECONOMIC EVIDENCE

Economic Evidence answers:

What economic values have source, currency, period, rule, state and limits?

Compensation Intelligence answers:

What rules, eligibility, snapshots, production, payments, bonuses, commissions and qualification states apply?

Economic Evidence owns source-bound economic value validity.

Compensation Intelligence consumes Economic Evidence when economic values matter.

Compensation Intelligence must not duplicate currency conversion truth.

Compensation Intelligence must not invent economic values.

Economic Evidence can say a paid value exists.

Compensation Intelligence can say how that value interacts with a compensation rule.

14. COMPENSATION VS FORECAST

Forecast answers:

What could happen?

Compensation Intelligence answers:

What compensation is confirmed, estimated, projected, earned, paid or not proven under rules and evidence?

Forecast can generate scenarios.

Compensation Intelligence can calculate compensation scenarios under explicit rules and assumptions.

Forecast does not create compensation truth.

Compensation scenario is not real payment.

Projected compensation remains forecast-bound until evidence changes its state.

15. COMPENSATION VS METRIC TRUTH

Metric Truth defines official metric owner.

Compensation Intelligence consumes official metrics.

Compensation Intelligence must not redefine:

- official production
- paid premium
- issued premium
- qualification metric
- conservation metric
- persistence metric
- activity metric

If official metric output is missing, Compensation Intelligence must mark evidence gap.

It must not create duplicate metric truth.

16. COMPENSATION VS POLICY / PRODUCT TRUTH

Policy Truth owns specific policy facts.

Product Truth owns product facts.

Compensation Intelligence may consume policy/product context where compensation rules depend on it.

Compensation Intelligence must not redefine:

- policy status
- premium status
- coverage
- product benefits
- product category
- product suitability
- policy events

Product payout difference is not product recommendation.

Policy event must be evidence, not compensation desire.

17. COMPENSATION VS BUSINESS PLANNING

Business Planning may use compensation context as constraint, scenario or review input.

Business Planning must not use income gap as pressure.

Business Planning must not invent payout-first routes.

Compensation Intelligence may explain economic implications under rules.

It must not create business plan mandates.

A compensation gap is planning context.

It is not a command.

18. COMPENSATION VS MANAGER INTELLIGENCE

Manager Intelligence may use compensation context for coaching.

Manager Intelligence must not convert compensation scenario into pressure, castigo, enforcement or labor judgment.

Compensation Intelligence may provide evidence gaps, stale warnings and context.

It must not produce manager enforcement.

Income difference is not advisor worth.

Economic gap is not punishment trigger.

19. COMPENSATION VS ADVISOR EXPERIENCE

Advisor Experience may explain compensation in clear language.

Advisor Experience must preserve evidence, uncertainty, rule context and limits.

Advisor Experience must not convert scenarios into promises.

Advisor Experience must not hide partial evidence.

Advisor Experience can teach what paid, earned, estimated and projected mean.

It cannot change compensation state.

20. COMPENSATION VS ECONOMIC MOTIVATION

Compensation Intelligence answers what rule, scenario or payment exists.

Economic Motivation answers how to present economic implications without violating Client First.

ADR-018 should govern motivation, anxiety, pressure and Client First.

ADR-017 governs compensation evidence.

Compensation Intelligence must not become motivation layer.

Compensation Intelligence must not use compensation to pressure advisor or client.

21. LIFE VS GMM / PERIOD BOUNDARY

Life and GMM compensation rules may differ.

Compensation Intelligence must preserve product line, rule pack, period and calendar.

Life and GMM must not be mixed unless a rule explicitly allows combined treatment.

Semester, quarter, month, campaign and contest windows must not be mixed.

Initial premium and renewal premium must not be mixed.

Issued premium and paid premium must not be mixed.

Prima meta, prima pago, commission and renewal must remain distinct.

LIMRA and IGC must not be treated as interchangeable unless an explicit rule maps them.

22. PAID VS EARNED VS ESTIMATED VS PROJECTED

Paid compensation requires payment evidence.

Earned compensation requires rule, eligibility, production, period and evidence that compensation has been earned but not necessarily paid.

Estimated compensation requires rule and evidence, but may include explicit assumptions or partial evidence.

Projected compensation is a future scenario and remains forecast-bound.

Possible compensation indicates potential eligibility, not confirmation.

Confirmed qualification requires official snapshot or sufficient official evidence.

Provisional qualification must be labeled provisional.

Unknown compensation remains unknown.

23. RULE PACK / SNAPSHOT HANDLING

Compensation Intelligence must preserve:

- rule pack

- RuleSnapshot
- compensation period

- contest period
- advisor career stage
- contract date
- career month

- channel context

- payment basis

Historical compensation periods must preserve historical RuleSnapshots.

Historical periods must not be recalculated with new rules except as separate analysis.

Do not mix 2025 commission schedules with 2026 contest rules.

Do not apply stale rule pack as current.

Do not infer rule version from desired outcome.

- convert unknown into zero unless explicit rule allows it
- hide uncertainty
- promise compensation
- pressure advisor or client

25. COMPENSATION STALENESS

Compensation evidence becomes stale when:

- rule pack changed
- period changed
- snapshot changed
- official production changed
- payment changed
- policy status changed
- conservation changed
- persistence changed
- advisor career stage changed
- campaign rule changed
- contest group changed
- paid premium changed
- issued premium changed
- new economic evidence appeared
- recalculation occurred
- retroactive adjustment occurred
- product line context changed
- channel context changed
- official qualification snapshot changed

Stale compensation evidence must be marked stale.

Stale compensation evidence must not support paid, confirmed or final language.

26. COMPENSATION DRIFT HANDLING

Compensation Drift occurs when:

- estimated commission is treated as paid
- possible bonus is treated as confirmed
- forecast is treated as compensation truth
- issued premium is treated as paid premium
- production goal is treated as real production
- old rule pack is treated as current
- snapshot is mixed with another period
- Life and GMM are mixed
- quarter and semester are mixed
- contest group is inferred without evidence
- manager pressure is treated as rule
- compensation is used to recommend product
- income gap is used to pressure client
- advisor worth is inferred from income
- economic motivation is treated as compensation truth

When drift exists, Forge must:

- mark compensation drift risk
- restore state labels
- restore rule context
- restore snapshot
- restore period
- restore evidence source
- preserve uncertainty
- block paid/confirmed language
- require human review where needed
- preserve Client First
- preserve Authority Boundary

27. CLIENT FIRST

Compensation Intelligence must preserve Client First.

Compensation cannot justify unsuitable recommendation.

Commission cannot justify product pressure.

Bonus cannot justify client manipulation.

Contest eligibility cannot justify violating client trust.

Income gap cannot justify pressuring a client.

Advisor compensation context must not override product suitability, policy truth, client need or client autonomy.

28. AUTHORITY BOUNDARY

Compensation Intelligence may inform.

Compensation Intelligence may explain.

Compensation Intelligence may estimate under rules.

Compensation Intelligence may mark evidence gaps.

Compensation Intelligence may recommend human review.

Compensation Intelligence may not decide for advisor.

Compensation Intelligence may not decide for manager.

Compensation Intelligence may not decide for client.

Compensation Intelligence may not guarantee payment.

Compensation Intelligence may not create enforcement.

Human authority remains governed by ADR-003.

29. SURVIVAL TEST

If ADR-017 disappears:

- Compensation Intelligence can invent income
- Economic Evidence can be overextended into rule interpretation
- Forecast can become compensation truth
- Metric Truth can be recalculated locally
- Business Planning can use fantasy income
- Manager Intelligence can enforce economic gaps
- Advisor Experience can explain scenarios as promises
- Economic Motivation can use false numbers
- Client First can be harmed by payout pressure
- Authority Boundary can be weakened by promised compensation
- paid, earned, estimated and projected can collapse into one unsafe state

ADR-017 survives because no previous ADR fully governs compensation rules, snapshots, qualification states, paid/earned/projected distinctions, retroactive differences or contest eligibility evidence.

30. ABSORPTION TEST

ADR-017 cannot be absorbed into ADR-008.

ADR-008 governs economic value evidence, but not compensation rule interpretation, eligibility, snapshots or paid/earned/projected state.

ADR-017 cannot be absorbed into ADR-007.

ADR-007 governs future scenarios, but not compensation truth or rule-based eligibility.

ADR-017 cannot be absorbed into ADR-002.

ADR-002 governs metric ownership, but not compensation interpretation.

ADR-017 cannot be absorbed into ADR-012.

ADR-012 governs planning, but not compensation evidence.

ADR-017 cannot be absorbed into ADR-015.

ADR-015 governs manager-facing coaching context, not compensation rule truth.

ADR-017 cannot be absorbed into future ADR-018.

ADR-018 should govern economic motivation and Client First presentation, not compensation evidence.

ADR-017 must survive as independent constitutional boundary.

37. FINAL VERDICT

ADR-017 establishes that Forge may interpret compensation rules, scenarios, eligibility and payment states only with evidence, rule context, period and snapshot.

Compensation Intelligence may explain paid, earned, estimated, projected, possible, provisional, stale, conflicting and unknown compensation states.

Compensation Intelligence may not invent income.

Compensation Intelligence may not promise payment.

Compensation Intelligence may not convert forecast into compensation truth.

Compensation Intelligence may not convert compensation into client pressure.

Paid compensation requires payment evidence.

Earned compensation requires rule, production, period and eligibility evidence.

Estimated compensation requires explicit assumptions and limits.

Projected compensation remains a scenario.

Unknown compensation remains unknown.

Compensation scenario is not real payment.

A compensation claim without rule, evidence, period, snapshot, production basis and uncertainty is not Compensation Intelligence.

It is invented income with a rule-shaped mask.
