## 12. Execution Roadmap

Every phase is sequential. A FAIL blocks later phases.

### Phase 1 — Constitutional Unification

**Objective:** produce and ratify one Unified Constitution with Article 0/Ley Zero as Article 0.

**Documents affected:** new Unified Constitution candidate, clause crosswalk, hierarchy reconciliation, ratification PAQ/certificate. Existing Constitution and Article 0 remain untouched.

**Prerequisites:** named human ratifier/quorum; AX audit accepted; complete V3/Article 0 clause inventory; Miranda review scheduled.

**Risks:** semantic loss, constitutional bloat, Article 0 dilution.

**PASS:** explicit human ratification, certificate, version/effective date, zero unmapped normative clauses, no competing active Constitution.

**FAIL:** missing ratifier, unresolved clause, altered Article 0 meaning, conflicting highest authority or absent Miranda review.

**Rollback:** before activation, withdraw candidate. After activation, never edit/revert history; suspend effective transition and ratify a corrective amendment or explicit predecessor reactivation record.

## 14. Rollback Strategy

Constitutional rollback is not `git revert` and never rewrites ratified history.

### Before ratification

- withdraw or revise candidate documents;
- retain review evidence;
- do not update active registries or Build Tree;
- restore no files because canonical sources were never changed.

### Ratified but not active

- issue a withdrawal/suspension certificate by the same authorized human governance;
- retain the ratification record and reason;
- do not activate dependent phases.

### Active

- declare constitutional HOLD;
- identify affected outputs/documents and effective versions;
- issue a new corrective amendment or explicit predecessor-reactivation certificate;
- update status registry atomically;
- preserve old/new texts, hashes, dates and decisions;
- re-run every downstream compatibility gate.

### Partial phase failure

- phase is `FAIL`, never “mostly passed”;
- later phases remain blocked;
- unratified candidate/navigation changes may be discarded;
- ratified artifacts require formal supersession, not deletion;
- no Build Tree or Rewrite Pack status may claim completion.
