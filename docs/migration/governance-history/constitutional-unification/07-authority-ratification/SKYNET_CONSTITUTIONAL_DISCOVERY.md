## 2. Constitutional Gap Analysis

### Problem

Forge has multiple present and future intelligences that interpret the same human and commercial reality. Locked ADRs govern named domains, while ROBOCOP governs changes to the repository. Neither supplies a finite, versioned, cross-domain catalog answering: “What may no intelligence output do, regardless of domain or implementation?”

The gap is therefore not absence of values. The values exist. The gap is an operationally addressable normative seam between constitutional doctrine and heterogeneous outputs.

Without that seam, a new intelligence can satisfy its local ADR yet still combine evidence, scoring, prediction, pressure and automation in a way that violates a cross-domain human boundary. Doctrine can evolve more slowly than implementations, and duplicated local interpretations can diverge.

### Preventable damage

- judgment presented without attributable evidence;
- unknowns converted into certainty;
- people reduced to scores, rankings or productivity proxies;
- purpose or economic context converted into pressure;
- surveillance represented as management visibility;
- automated outputs mistaken for human decisions;
- contextual carrier or organization truth hardcoded as universal truth;
- future intelligences escaping constraints because no named ADR covers them.

### Why the Constitution alone is not an operational restriction mechanism

The Constitution provides identity, principles and authority boundaries. It is intentionally broad and immutable. It does not enumerate a law version, evaluation point, violation result, evidence record or amendment lifecycle for every output class. Adding those mechanics directly to the Constitution would increase churn and blur foundational doctrine with application governance.

SKYNET is justified only if it translates already-ratified doctrine into a small set of universal negative constraints without becoming a second constitution or an implementation.

### Required distinctions

| Construct | Meaning | May authorize implementation? |
|---|---|---|
| Constitution | Foundational identity, purpose, authority and invariant architecture | Only through its governed decision process. |
| Constitutional principle | Foundational norm explaining what Forge protects | No, not by itself. |
| SKYNET Law | Explicitly ratified, universal and testable restriction on intelligence outputs/actions | Constrains all implementations; does not select one. |
| SKYNET Policy | Versioned application guidance beneath one law; contextual but cross-domain | No expansion or override of its parent law. |
| Domain governance rule | Rule owned by one constitutional domain | Only inside that domain and its ADR boundary. |
| Rule Pack | Carrier/channel/organization/period interpretation of facts | Never Forge Core truth and never constitutional law. |
| Acceptance criterion | Observable condition used to accept a specified change | Evidence of conformance, not normative authority. |
| Implementation validation | Test or audit showing a mechanism obeys authority | Cannot create, amend or ratify authority. |

Conclusion: the gap is real, but a SKYNET label is useful only as a governed catalog of restrictions. It is not justified as a new intelligence or autonomous authority.

## 3. Definition and Boundary

### Formal definition

SKYNET is a proposed constitutional governance layer consisting of:

1. a finite registry of explicitly ratified universal restrictions;
2. a normative lifecycle for those restrictions;
3. a conflict and human-review model;
4. auditable conformance requirements independent of implementation.

It is simultaneously a constitutional layer, a normative system and a catalog. Its “authority” is documentary: each ratified law binds outputs because human constitutional authority ratified it. SKYNET itself is not a person or autonomous decision maker.

### Explicit exclusions

SKYNET is not an intelligence, AI, agent, assistant, engine, runtime, business domain, recommendation source, source of truth, metric owner, Council seat or execution service. It has no personality, objectives, memory, self-modification, self-ratification or default autonomy.

### Material scope

Potentially governed outputs include interpretations, classifications, recommendations, summaries, predictions, explanations, rankings, scores, manager/advisor outputs, automated decision candidates and executable action proposals. Future intelligences enter scope by output type, not by name.

Outside scope are ordinary visual choices, file layout, innocuous implementation details, local calculations without constitutional effect and product preferences. Those remain governed by design, architecture, domain ADRs, specifications or Rule Packs.

### Universal/context classification test

A proposal qualifies as a SKYNET Law only if every answer is yes:

1. Does it prevent serious cross-domain human, truth or authority harm?
2. Does it remain valid across carriers, channels, organizations, products and periods?
3. Does it constrain an output or action rather than prescribe a feature?
4. Is the violation conceptually testable with observable evidence?
5. Is it absent or insufficiently operationalized in lower-level governance?
6. Can it remain implementation-neutral?
7. Is it durable enough to justify constitutional change control?

If the rule is cross-domain but contextual, it is a SKYNET Policy. If it belongs to one domain, it is a domain rule. If it depends on carrier/channel/organization/period, it belongs in a Rule Pack or Organization Profile. If it merely expresses a product preference or cannot be tested, it is rejected.

## 4. Authority Hierarchy

The historical proposed chain cannot be accepted unchanged because it omits ADR placement and conflicts with AX-001. The defensible target hierarchy is:

1. Article 0 as ratified human-purpose boundary, subject to formal reconciliation with AX-001;
2. Forge Constitution and ratified constitutional amendments;

4. SKYNET Policies and domain governance rules;
5. Rule Packs, Organization Profiles and contextual policies;
6. engines, intelligences, runtimes and outputs.

SKYNET Laws cannot silently supersede an ADR, and an ADR cannot silently amend a SKYNET Law. A conflict requires an explicit constitutional reconciliation record. Until C-001 is resolved, no final assertion that Article 0 is legally “above” the Constitution is safe; operationally, every interpretation must satisfy both.

ROBOCOP, Miranda and the Forge Council are not rungs in the content hierarchy:

- ROBOCOP is the documentary/change-compliance gate.
- Miranda is the mandatory quality and constitutional-discipline review gate within assigned scope.
- The Forge Council supplies bounded review perspectives.
- Explicit human constitutional authority ratifies; no reviewer self-ratifies.

## 5. Conflict Model

### Article 0

SKYNET must strengthen human judgment and never replace it. It may block, degrade or withhold an automated Forge output. It may prevent Forge from executing an unauthorized action. It may not declare a legitimate human decision invalid merely because the system disagrees.

Legal, consent, privacy, security and source-authority restrictions may prevent the system from participating in a requested action. That is a boundary on Forge, not a substitution of human judgment. Article 0 admits no exception that would let automation assume final professional, employment or human-worth authority.

When a law appears to conflict with human judgment:

1. stop or degrade the automated output;
2. disclose the governing law and evidence gap;
3. route to human review;
4. preserve the human's ability to decide outside Forge, subject to law and legitimate authority;
5. escalate normative conflict for constitutional reconciliation rather than improvising an exception.

### ROBOCOP

ROBOCOP validates changes; SKYNET restricts governed outputs. The Constitutional Gate should identify applicable ratified laws, law versions, evidence, violation behavior and human-review path. ROBOCOP must block a change when a ratified law is applicable and conformance evidence is absent, contradictory or bypassed. It does not itself interpret business truth.

### Miranda

Miranda reviews quality, scope discipline, constitutional compatibility, harmful ambiguity and adequacy of conformance evidence. Conceptual review results are `APPROVE`, `HOLD`, `REJECT` or `REQUIRE_EVIDENCE`; these are review outcomes, not lawmaking.

Miranda cannot ratify a law, waive a non-derogable protection or expand its own authority. A Miranda SKYNET decision can be overturned only by the explicit human constitutional authority through a recorded review; the override cannot contradict Article 0, Constitution or a ratified law without formal amendment.

### Possible output dispositions

- `ALLOW`: sufficient evidence and no violation.
- `DEGRADE`: retain only the supported portion and disclose uncertainty.
- `REQUEST_EVIDENCE`: no judgment until required evidence exists.
- `REQUIRE_HUMAN_REVIEW`: system cannot safely finalize.
- `INVALIDATE_OUTPUT`: recommendation or classification has no valid authority.
- `BLOCK_SYSTEM_ACTION`: Forge must not execute the action.
- `ABSTAIN`: Forge emits no substantive judgment.

Every non-allow disposition requires an auditable reason and law/version reference. SKYNET restricts; it does not create a preferred recommendation or authorize its own exceptions. Confidence never overrides a law.

## 6. Human Judgment Safeguards

- The system distinguishes blocking an output from blocking a person.
- Every adverse disposition states evidence, uncertainty, applicable law and review route.
- Human review is meaningful: reviewers receive source context and can correct facts.
- No law may convert a score into final judgment of worth, potential, employability or professional authority.
- No automated interpretation may silently become an executed action.
- Consent and privacy boundaries remain effective during review.
- An abstention is safer than invented certainty.
- Human overrides are attributable and cannot mutate source truth or erase prior evidence.
- Automated evaluators cannot author, ratify, amend or retire the laws they evaluate.

## 7. Governance Lifecycle

### Ownership and custody

Constitutional ownership remains with explicit human constitutional authority. The Board may perform that function only after its membership, quorum and authority are canonically defined. Governance maintainers are custodians, not owners. Any participant may propose; domain owners supply evidence; Miranda and relevant Council roles review; human constitutional authority ratifies; an independent governance audit verifies compliance.

No intelligence, engine, AI, runtime or implementation team may autoratify a law.

### Law states

| State | Minimum evidence and authority | Allowed transition | Required record | Existing implementation impact |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| `CANDIDATE` | Named harm and source evidence; proposer identified | DISCOVERY or REJECTED outside registry | Proposal record | None |
| `DISCOVERY` | Cross-domain analysis, alternatives and overlaps | REVIEW, CANDIDATE withdrawal | Discovery dossier | None |
| `REVIEW` | Exact text, universal/testable analysis, conflicts, Article 0 compatibility, PAQ | RATIFIED or DISCOVERY | Human and Miranda/Council review record | Readiness assessment only |
| `RATIFIED` | Explicit human ratification and immutable version | ACTIVE | Ratification certificate | Migration planning; not silently active |
| `ACTIVE` | Effective date, registry entry, applicability and enforcement contract | AMENDED, SUSPENDED, RETIRED, SUPERSEDED | Activation record | Mandatory conformance |
| `AMENDED` | New evidence, impact and explicit ratification | ACTIVE as new version | Amendment certificate and lineage | Versioned migration; old outputs preserved |
| `SUSPENDED` | Demonstrated harm, ambiguity or invalid operation; human authority | ACTIVE, AMENDED, RETIRED | Time-bound suspension and review owner | Stop applying prospectively; preserve records |
| `RETIRED` | Law no longer necessary; impact and archival review | None except new candidate | Retirement certificate | No prospective authority; history retained |
| `SUPERSEDED` | Replacement law ratified and mapping complete | None | Supersession map | New outputs use replacement; old lineage preserved |

`RATIFIED` and `ACTIVE` are distinct: ratification creates authority; activation declares the effective version and conformance date.

### Exact ratification procedure

1. File a versioned proposal with exact wording and proposer.
2. Record evidence origin and source authority.
3. Demonstrate cross-domain relevance and serious harm.
4. Pass the universal/context classification test.
5. Analyze overlap with Constitution, ADRs, policies, domain rules and Rule Packs.
6. Prove implementation neutrality and conceptual testability.
7. Resolve Article 0 and constitutional conflicts.
8. Obtain domain-owner and privacy/security review where implicated.
9. Obtain Miranda review when quality, human impact or constitutional discipline is implicated.
10. Record an explicit PAQ with measurable acceptance conditions.
11. Obtain explicit human constitutional ratification; silence and usage are invalid.
12. Assign immutable law ID, semantic version and effective date.
13. Issue a ratification certificate and registry entry.
14. Update Constitution Map, governance registry, applicable ADR references and Build Tree status without rewriting history.

### Amendment procedure

An amendment is any change to wording, scope, applicability, disposition, exception class or evidence requirement. It requires new evidence, affected-domain analysis, conflict review, migration plan, backward-compatibility decision, human approval, new version and certificate.

Outputs retain the law version and evidence snapshot under which they were generated. They are not silently recalculated or relabeled. A material safety defect may trigger explicit re-evaluation, while preserving both the original and revised records.

### Exceptions

Article 0, human dignity, source truth, consent, privacy and non-substitution boundaries are non-derogable. A universal law cannot receive a private permanent bypass.

A policy-level or explicitly derogable operational provision may receive a scoped exception requested by an accountable human owner, reviewed by affected domain/security/privacy owners and authorized by human constitutional authority. It must name scope, reason, risk, compensating controls, start, expiry (maximum one release or 90 days unless the ratified law sets a shorter limit), reviewer and audit record. Renewal is a new decision; expiration is automatic. No AI or intelligence may request and approve the same exception.

## 8. Conceptual Enforcement Contract

Ratifying SKYNET does not ratify a runtime.

### Evaluation points

Applicable laws are evaluated at governed seams: before a recommendation/classification becomes authoritative output, before presentation of materially human-impacting conclusions, and before an executable action is authorized. Evaluation may be performed by domain-local conformant mechanisms; it must not require one monolithic SKYNET engine.

### Conceptual input

- output type and accountable owner;
- source/evidence references and confidence/unknown state;
- subject and intended audience;
- domain, Rule Pack, Organization Profile and period context;
- proposed action authority and consent state;
- applicable law/policy versions.

### Conceptual result

- disposition (`ALLOW`, `DEGRADE`, `REQUEST_EVIDENCE`, `REQUIRE_HUMAN_REVIEW`, `INVALIDATE_OUTPUT`, `BLOCK_SYSTEM_ACTION`, `ABSTAIN`);
- violated or satisfied law IDs/versions;
- evidence gaps and supported subset;
- human-review route;
- accountable owner, timestamp and trace reference.

Normative authority is the ratified law. The evaluator is an implementation mechanism. Runtime is where that mechanism executes. Evidence supports the result. Audit verifies lineage and conformance. None may impersonate another.

### Number of laws

The historical “3 to 7” range is a useful ceiling, not a valid minimum. The recommended initial registry contains **two** laws: Evidence Before Judgment and Human Dignity Must Be Preserved. A third law is added only when it prevents a distinct universal harm not covered by existing wording.

Consolidate when laws share harm, evidence and disposition. Use child policies for contextual application. Reject proposals that restate an ADR, name a carrier/product, prescribe implementation, lack a testable violation or add review cost without distinct harm reduction. Seven should be a review trigger, not a quota.
