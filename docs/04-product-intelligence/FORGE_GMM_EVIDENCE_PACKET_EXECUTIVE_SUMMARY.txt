3. UNIVERSAL PACKET

Every GMM case requires:

- exact product or product uncertainty
- policy/caratula status
- policy active status or unknown
- insured identity
- event description
- event family or uncertainty
- approximate date context
- territory/location
- optional coverage relevance
- preexistence known/unknown

4. COMPLETENESS LEVELS

Level 0:
No Evidence

Level 1:
Routing Evidence

Level 2:
Conditional Assessment Evidence

Level 3:
Likely Covered / Likely Not Covered Evidence

Level 4:
Financial Responsibility Evidence

Level 4 is not stronger coverage certainty. It is separate financial context.

5. FINAL QUESTION EXAMPLE

User:

"My wife has had Alfa Medical for 4 years.
She is pregnant.
Would childbirth be covered?"

Level 0:
If only "would pregnancy be covered?" is known, Forge can only explain
maternity rules generally.

Level 1:
With "Alfa Medical, wife, pregnant, 4 years," Forge can route to Maternity but
cannot assess responsibly.

Level 2:
Add mother age, expected birth date, active policy/caratula context, territory,
coverage continuity statement and assisted reproduction status. Forge may give
conditional assessment.

Level 3:
Add authoritative caratula, active policy proof, confirmed continuity through
birth, deductible type, maternity benefit, documentation, no assisted
reproduction complications or assisted-reproduction documents if applicable, and
no human review gate. Forge may say likely covered or likely not covered,
subject to insurer review.

Level 4:
Add deductible, coinsurance, maternity benefit amount, plan/zone, territory,
hospital/provider and applicable catalogs. Forge may discuss financial
responsibility boundary but still must not calculate unless a future approved
engine exists.
