# Alfa Medical Exclusion Intelligence

Status: DISCOVERY ONLY

This document groups exclusions by practical meaning. It does not replace the
Condiciones Generales and must not be used as a claims ruling.

## Exclusion Principle

Alfa Medical does not cover every medical expense. It covers covered events and
covered expenses after applying:

- General exclusions.
- Particular exclusions for each benefit.
- Waiting periods.
- Deductible and coinsurance.
- Documentation requirements.
- Medical necessity.
- Territory and provider conditions.

An event can look medical and still be excluded.

## Exclusion Categories

### 1. Provider Conflict and Self-Dealing

Excluded:

- Fees, medical services, medications or supplies when the insured or direct
  family provides the service.
- Services from companies where the insured or direct family has social or
  shareholder participation.

Practical meaning:

The policy tries to avoid reimbursing medical expenses where the insured or
family could financially benefit from the service provider.

### 2. War, Military, Public Security and Intentional Violence Contexts

Excluded:

- Accidents or illnesses while serving in military, combat, security or
  anti-drug bodies when caused by that activity.
- Injuries from war, riot, rebellion or civil/military insurrection.
- Injuries from intentional crimes where the insured is responsible.

Practical meaning:

High-risk institutional or intentional-violence contexts may fall outside the
ordinary covered medical-risk model.

### 3. Self-Inflicted Harm and Substance-Related Events

Excluded:

- Self-inflicted injury or suicide attempt.
- Alcoholism or toxicomania treatment.
- Illnesses caused by alcohol ingestion or drug/psychotropic use, except when
  prescribed and followed as indicated.
- Accident injuries related to alcohol above the applicable local limit or drug
  use for which the insured is responsible, except prescribed use followed as
  indicated.

Practical meaning:

Medical harm tied to self-infliction or substance misuse can be denied even if
the medical cost is real.

### 4. Aesthetic, Preventive and Elective Improvement Treatments

Excluded:

- Cosmetic or appearance-improvement procedures.
- Calvicie correction.
- Hormone growth treatment.
- Checkups and general health reviews.
- Preventive or prophylactic treatment.
- Weight-loss treatments except the defined bariatric surgery benefit.
- Gender-change surgery and associated treatments/complications.

Practical meaning:

The product is centered on restoring health after covered events, not paying
for elective improvement, prevention or general wellness monitoring.

### 5. Fertility, Infertility and Reproductive Treatment

Excluded:

- Treatments to avoid or procure procreation, infertility or sterility
  treatment and their complications, except what is specifically allowed under
  reproduction-assisted maternity.
- Expenses for the newborns and mother when fertility/sterility treatment was
  used outside the covered conditions.

Practical meaning:

Reproductive coverage exists, but it is narrow and condition-heavy. The product
does not broadly cover fertility treatment.

### 6. Mental Health, Psychology and Sleep Disorders

Excluded:

- Psychiatric and psychological studies or treatments of any kind, regardless
  of cause.
- Sleep disorders, apnea, snoring disorders, autism, conduct, learning or
  language disorders, dementia, depression, neurosis, psychosis and related
  complications.
- Sleep-inducing medications.

Practical meaning:

Clients may assume a medical policy covers mental health or sleep-related
conditions. In Alfa Medical these are broadly excluded under the general
exclusions, apart from assistance-style orientation benefits.

### 7. Experimental or Unapproved Treatments

Excluded:

- Studies or treatments lacking required approval or evidence.
- Experimental or investigational treatments.
- Medications without current COFEPRIS and relevant authority permits.
- Gene/molecular tests except the defined genomic studies benefit.
- Gene therapies and orphan drugs except the defined benefit.

Practical meaning:

"New" or "advanced" does not mean covered. Authorization, official approval and
specific benefit wording matter.

### 8. Dental, Vision, Hearing and Podiatric Limits

Excluded:

- Dental, alveolar, gingival and maxillofacial treatments from disease except
  defined dental benefit contexts.
- Refractive eye diseases.
- Glasses, contact lenses and external hearing devices.
- Podiatric care.
- Orthopedic shoes, insoles and similar items.

Practical meaning:

Routine dental/vision/hearing needs are not the core coverage, even though
limited dental and accident dental benefits exist.

### 9. Preexisting Conditions

Excluded:

- Preexisting diseases and injuries, except where the declared preexistence
  clause applies.

Practical meaning:

Coverage does not automatically make previous conditions covered. Declaration,
time without symptoms/treatment/gastos, and type of condition matter.

### 10. Particular Benefit Exclusions

Many benefits include specific exclusions. Examples:

- Reproduction-assisted maternity excludes omitted notice, certain prior
  fertility-control treatments, gender-change treatment history, use of another
  person's eggs, complications from assisted reproduction treatment and
  infertility diagnostic/treatment costs.
- Hernias exclude diastasis of rectus abdominal muscles and certain
  abdominoplasty-related scenarios.
- Hyperbaric chamber excludes aesthetic/sports uses and diabetic foot.
- Fetal surgery excludes non-listed procedures, procedures abroad,
  reproduction-assisted maternity cases, experimental/non-indicated procedures,
  unmet documentation/authorization and certain added pathologies.
- Dental basic excludes any treatment not listed, dental medication and
  prevention not required by covered disease.

Practical meaning:

Coverage names can be misleading. A benefit title is not the same as unlimited
coverage.

## Advisor Risk Translation

Unsafe advisor statement:

"If it is medically necessary, it is covered."

Safer advisor statement:

"Medical necessity is required, but it is not enough. The event also needs to
fit the policy conditions, avoid exclusions, satisfy waiting periods and meet
documentation and authorization requirements."
