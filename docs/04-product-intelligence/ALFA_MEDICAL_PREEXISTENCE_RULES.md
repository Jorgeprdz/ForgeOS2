# Alfa Medical Preexistence Rules

Status: DISCOVERY ONLY

## AM-PRE-001

Description: A disease or injury is preexisting when documentary or diagnostic
evidence shows it existed before policy contracting.

Required Inputs:
- Application date.
- Medical records.
- Diagnostic tests.
- Prior diagnosis.
- Prior expense evidence.
- Declaration in application.

Conditions:
- Any listed evidence path can establish preexistence.

Exceptions:
- Dispute can go to private medical arbitration if insurer denies as
  preexisting.

Unknowns:
- Operational evidence threshold.

Source:
- Alfa Medical Nuevos, II Enfermedad o Lesion Preexistente.

## AM-PRE-002

Description: Declared preexistence may be covered from the fourth year.

Required Inputs:
- Declaration in initial application.
- Continuous coverage duration.
- Symptom/treatment/expense history.
- Condition category.

Conditions:
- Must be specifically declared.
- Must have four years of uninterrupted coverage.
- Must satisfy no signs/treatment/expenses requirement.
- Must not be expressly excluded.

Exceptions:
- Excluded categories listed in declared-preexistence clause.

Unknowns:
- How declaration specificity is evaluated.

Source:
- Alfa Medical Nuevos, V. Preexistencia Declarada.

## AM-PRE-003

Description: Declared preexistence requires three continuous years without
expenses, treatment, signs or symptoms.

Required Inputs:
- Medical history.
- Expense history.
- Treatment history.
- Symptom/sign evidence.

Conditions:
- No expenses, treatment, signs or symptoms for three continuous years under
  policy with insurer.

Exceptions:
- Any evidence during the period can block the rule.

Unknowns:
- Whether routine monitoring counts as treatment.

Source:
- Alfa Medical Nuevos, V.

## AM-PRE-004

Description: Express exclusions override declared-preexistence eligibility.

Required Inputs:
- Condition.
- Exclusion map.
- Particular exclusions.

Conditions:
- Even if declared, condition must not be expressly excluded.

Exceptions:
- Specific clause or endorsement may alter; human review required.

Unknowns:
- Endorsement conflicts.

Source:
- Alfa Medical Nuevos, V; VII.

## AM-PRE-005

Description: Certain categories are excluded from declared-preexistence
coverage.

Required Inputs:
- Diagnosis category.
- Medical classification.

Conditions:
- Declared preexistence rule does not apply to listed categories including
  chronic-degenerative, cardiovascular, respiratory, diabetes/metabolic,
  hypertension, autoimmune, renal insufficiency, cancer, liver disease,
  HIV/AIDS, osteomuscular, spine, knee, central/peripheral nervous system and
  cerebrovascular conditions.

Exceptions:
- None identified in source, unless endorsement says otherwise.

Unknowns:
- Classification boundaries for multi-system disease.

Source:
- Alfa Medical Nuevos, V.

## AM-PRE-006

Description: Recognition of antiquity does not create retroactive preexistence
coverage.

Required Inputs:
- Prior policy evidence.
- Recognized antiquity field.
- Event date.
- First symptoms/expense date.

Conditions:
- Recognition may reduce waiting periods only.
- It does not imply retroactivity or coverage for preexisting diseases.

Exceptions:
- None identified.

Unknowns:
- How prior coverage continuity is audited.

Source:
- Alfa Medical Nuevos, VI.3.

## AM-PRE-007

Description: Preexistence denial can trigger arbitration pathway.

Required Inputs:
- Insurer denial reason.
- Preexistence dispute.
- Parties' agreement to arbitration.

Conditions:
- If insurer denies due to preexistence, insured may go to private medical
  arbitration by agreement.

Exceptions:
- Requires arbitration agreement.

Unknowns:
- Operational process and timelines.

Source:
- Alfa Medical Nuevos, II Enfermedad o Lesion Preexistente.

## AM-PRE-008

Description: Congenital/genetic born-outside-vigency claims require prior
evidence review.

Required Inputs:
- Insured born in or outside policy vigency.
- Diagnosis date.
- Awareness evidence.
- Prior expenses.

Conditions:
- Coverage from third year only if before third year there was no diagnosis,
  no awareness/desapercibida evidence and no expenses.

Exceptions:
- Prior evidence blocks coverage.

Unknowns:
- How "desapercibida" is proven.

Source:
- Alfa Medical Nuevos, IV.4.
