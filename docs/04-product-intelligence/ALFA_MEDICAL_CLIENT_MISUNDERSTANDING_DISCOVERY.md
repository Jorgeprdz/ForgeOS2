# Alfa Medical Client Misunderstanding Discovery

Status: DISCOVERY ONLY

This document identifies likely misunderstandings based on Alfa Medical
conditions and existing Forge GMM sales-story analysis. These are not claims
about any specific client; they are advisor education risks.

## Misunderstanding 1: "Everything medical is covered"

Correction:

Alfa Medical covers covered accidents, illnesses, maternity events and defined
benefits under policy conditions. It does not cover every medical expense.

Practical meaning:

An expense can be medically real and still be excluded, below deductible,
outside waiting period, outside territory, above tabulator, lacking
documentation or tied to a non-covered event.

## Misunderstanding 2: "If the doctor prescribes it, the insurer pays"

Correction:

Prescription and medical necessity matter, but they are not enough. The event
must be a covered claim and the expense must be procedent under policy rules.

Practical meaning:

Doctor recommendation does not override exclusions, waiting periods,
authorization requirements or catalog limits.

## Misunderstanding 3: "Deductible is paid every visit"

Correction:

Deductible applies by covered accident or illness and can behave differently
depending on whether the policy has deductible unico, anual or reinstalable.

Practical meaning:

The client needs to understand the event/siniestro concept, not just a "per
visit" idea.

## Misunderstanding 4: "Once I pay deductible, I do not pay more"

Correction:

Coinsurance applies after deductible unless the benefit or event has a
different rule. The coinsurance cap may limit exposure, but special rules can
modify or remove cap behavior.

Practical meaning:

The client may still pay deductible, coinsurance, hospital-level adjustments,
non-covered charges and differences above catalog/usual/reasonable values.

## Misunderstanding 5: "I can go to any hospital with the same cost"

Correction:

The insured can choose providers freely, but choosing a hospital level different
from the contracted level can change coinsurance and cap treatment.

Practical meaning:

Freedom of choice is not equal to identical financial responsibility.

## Misunderstanding 6: "Accidents have no waiting period, so every accident is covered"

Correction:

Covered accidents can apply from day one, but the event must meet the accident
definition and avoid exclusions. First expense timing matters. Alcohol, drugs,
racing, professional high-risk activity or intentional contexts can affect
coverage.

Practical meaning:

The facts around the accident matter as much as the injury.

## Misunderstanding 7: "Preexisting conditions are never covered"

Correction:

Declared preexisting conditions may become covered from the fourth year only if
specific requirements are met. Several chronic or serious categories remain
excluded from that declared-preexistence benefit.

Practical meaning:

Preexistence is not a simple yes/no. Forge must know declaration, symptoms,
gastos, treatments, condition type and continuity.

## Misunderstanding 8: "Preexisting conditions are always covered after enough time"

Correction:

Not all preexisting conditions become covered. The declared-preexistence clause
has exclusions for specific chronic, cardiovascular, respiratory, metabolic,
autoimmune, renal, cancer, liver, HIV/AIDS, musculoskeletal, spine, knee,
nervous system and vascular-cerebral categories.

Practical meaning:

Time alone is not proof of coverage.

## Misunderstanding 9: "Maternity is automatic"

Correction:

Maternity requires age, coverage continuity, timing, territory and
documentation conditions. Reproduction-assisted maternity has a different and
more restrictive set of requirements.

Practical meaning:

Pregnancy coverage must be reviewed before the client assumes reimbursement or
indemnity.

## Misunderstanding 10: "Foreign care is included"

Correction:

Foreign care generally depends on optional coverages and specific benefit
conditions. Programmed vs non-programmed care changes deductible, coinsurance,
cap and reimbursement behavior.

Practical meaning:

The client must not assume an Alfa Medical national plan behaves like an
international plan.

## Misunderstanding 11: "Dental, checkups and mental health are normal GMM benefits"

Correction:

Dental basic is limited to listed services through dental providers in
agreement. General checkups and broad psychiatric/psychological treatments are
excluded under the reviewed conditions.

Practical meaning:

Daily wellness and preventive needs are not the core promise of Alfa Medical.

## Misunderstanding 12: "Advanced medicine is always covered"

Correction:

Advanced treatments such as genomic studies, fetal surgery, gene therapies,
orphan drugs, ECMO, stem cells and hyperbaric chamber are covered only in
defined situations with strict conditions.

Practical meaning:

The more specialized the treatment, the more evidence, authorization and
benefit-specific review Forge should require.
