PURPOSE

Document conceptual recommendations for the next narrative evolution of the
current GMM sales presentation.

These are not implementation instructions.
These are not slide rewrite instructions.
These are not visual design instructions.

RECOMMENDATION 1: MOVE THE PROSPECT'S QUESTION EARLIER

Current story begins with:

"What is GMM?"

The prospect's real question is closer to:

"What happens financially if something serious happens?"

Future narrative exploration should test whether the presentation becomes
stronger when the financial consequence is established before the technical
definitions.

RECOMMENDATION 2: TREAT PARTICIPATION EXAMPLES AS CORE STORY, NOT LATE SUPPORT

Slides 12 and 13 appear to be the strongest narrative assets.

They should be considered the conceptual center of the story because they show:

- total medical expense
- client responsibility
- insurer responsibility
- illness vs accident difference
- why deductible and coinsurance matter

This does not mean redesign them now.

It means future narrative architecture should not treat them as afterthoughts.

RECOMMENDATION 3: USE DEFINITIONS TO EXPLAIN CONSEQUENCE

Current flow:

Definition first, consequence later.

Possible future narrative principle:

Consequence first, definition as explanation.

This would help the prospect understand why terms like sum insured, deductible,
coinsurance and hospital level matter.

RECOMMENDATION 4: PRESERVE TRUST-BUILDING TRANSPARENCY

The waiting-period and exclusion slides are trust-building assets because they
show boundaries.

The narrative question is not whether to remove them.

The narrative question is where they belong so they do not interrupt the first
emotional understanding of the problem.

RECOMMENDATION 5: MAKE "WITHOUT GMM" AND "WITH GMM" CONCEPTUALLY CLEAR

The deck currently implies this contrast through examples.

Future story evolution should make the contrast easier to perceive:

Without GMM:

- medical event can become financial shock
- hospital choice may become constrained by liquidity
- savings and family stability may be exposed

With GMM:

- covered expenses can transfer substantially to insurer
- client responsibility becomes more understandable
- plan design becomes a choice about access, risk and liquidity

This is conceptual analysis, not copy.

RECOMMENDATION 6: REDUCE PRICE SHOCK BY BUILDING VALUE BEFORE QUOTE

Slide 14 introduces the quote.

The quote is stronger when the prospect has already internalized:

- what risk is being transferred
- what the client would otherwise absorb
- why deductible changes price
- why hospital level matters
- why accident and illness examples differ

Future versions should make sure value is fully felt before price appears.

RECOMMENDATION 7: KEEP EDUCATION, BUT ANCHOR IT IN STORY

GMM requires education.

The issue is not that there are definitions.

The issue is that definitions are currently the early spine of the narrative.

Future narrative architecture should preserve education but make it serve the
story:

Problem -> Consequence -> Mechanism -> Decision

RECOMMENDATION 8: AVOID TURNING THE DECK INTO A PRODUCT MANUAL

The presentation should not become:

- a conditions-general summary
- a compliance manual
- a full product encyclopedia
- a list of all benefits
- a technical training deck

It should remain a client decision narrative.

RECOMMENDATION 9: DO NOT OVERCORRECT INTO FEAR

A stronger story does not mean fear-based selling.

The ideal tone should be:

- clear
- serious
- human
- financially concrete
- protective
- calm

The goal is understanding, not panic.

RECOMMENDATION 10: FUTURE TESTING QUESTION

Future advisor testing should ask:

"At what point did the client say, 'Now I understand why this matters'?"

If that moment happens only at the example slides, the narrative is probably
starting too technically.

FINAL RECOMMENDED NEXT STEP

Run a future PAQ for GMM Sales Story Architecture using the existing deck as
source material.

That future PAQ may explore structure, but should still avoid visual redesign
until the narrative spine is approved.
