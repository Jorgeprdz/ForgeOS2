# Alfa Medical Coverage Map

Status: DISCOVERY ONLY

This map translates Alfa Medical coverage areas into practical advisor
understanding. It is not a claims decision, not a legal interpretation and not
an implementation model.

## Coverage Reading Rule

For every coverage question, Forge must evaluate:

- Exact product: Alfa Medical.
- Active policy and insured.
- Policy caratula values.
- Territory.
- Sum insured.
- Deductible.
- Coinsurance.
- Coinsurance cap.
- Hospital level.
- Tabulator/catalog.
- Waiting period.
- Exclusions and particular exclusions.
- Required documentation.
- Whether the event is medically necessary and prescribed.

## Core Coverage Areas

| Area | Discovery status | Practical meaning |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Hospitalization | Covered, conditional | Hospital services are covered for a covered claim, including standard private room, operating room, recovery room, emergency room, ICU, medications, blood products, lab and imaging, subject to plan, hospital level, territory and usual/reasonable limits. |
| Surgery | Covered, conditional | Medical and surgical professional fees are covered under the medical honorarium catalog. Multiple procedures and multiple surgeons have special limits. |
| Emergency care | Covered, conditional | Urgent events are covered when they meet the policy definition of medical urgency and are not excluded. Some coinsurance hospital-level adjustments do not apply in medical urgency. |
| Specialist care | Covered, conditional | Specialist care is covered when medically necessary, legally authorized and related to a covered claim. Some high-specialty procedures require authorization or second valuation. |
| Diagnostic studies | Covered, conditional | Laboratory, gabinete and imagenologia are covered when prescribed, necessary and related to a covered claim. General checkups are excluded. |
| Medications | Covered, conditional | Medications prescribed for a covered claim are covered when accompanied by pharmacy invoice and prescription and authorized for distribution in Mexico. Pharmacy choice affects coinsurance. |
| Intensive care | Covered, conditional | ICU is included in hospital services for covered claims, subject to plan, territory and usual/reasonable rules. Foreign coverage may have specific daily limits. |
| Cancer treatment | Covered, conditional | Cancer-related care may be covered if the condition is covered and waiting periods are satisfied. Breast gland diseases including breast cancer have a third-year waiting-period rule. Reconstructive breast surgery has a specific benefit limit and authorization requirement. |
| Accidents | Covered, conditional | Covered accidents begin day one if they meet the accident definition. First expense after 30 days may cause the event to be treated as illness rather than accident. Some accident benefits require first attention within 30 days. |
| Maternity | Covered, conditional | Maternity applies only under specific age, continuity, timing and documentation conditions. The benefit has distinct rules for years 1 to 3 and year 4 onward. |
| Reproduction-assisted maternity | Covered, conditional | Covered only under specific age, diagnosis/family condition, method, embryo count, medical institution and notice requirements. Several related expenses remain excluded. |
| Rehabilitation | Partially covered | Up to 30 sessions per covered claim are covered when prescribed and necessary. Additional sessions require prior valuation and authorization. Specialized rehabilitation institution stay and complementary services are not covered. |
| International coverage | Optional / conditional | Basic Alfa Medical is territorial. Foreign care requires optional coverage or specific assistance benefits. Programmed vs non-programmed foreign attention changes deductible, coinsurance and cap rules. |
| Additional benefits | Conditional | Includes assistance services, ambulance, dental basic, protection patrimonial, transplants, genomic studies, fetal surgery, hyperbaric chamber and other defined benefits, each with its own limits and exclusions. |

## Basic Covered Expense Categories

The Condiciones Generales identify Gastos Medicos Amparados as medically
necessary expenses for a covered claim, including:

- Medical professional fees.
- Anesthesiologist fees.
- Surgical assistant fees.
- Postoperative consultations inside the stated surgical fee context.
- Hospital services.
- Operating room, recovery room and emergency room.
- ICU.
- Medications and surgical supplies.
- Blood, plasma and derivatives.
- Laboratory, gabinete and imaging.
- Specialized treatments such as radiotherapy, chemotherapy, hydrotherapy and
  inhalotherapy.
- Rehabilitation sessions, subject to session limit and authorization rules.
- Nurse care, subject to hour limit and medical necessity.
- Homeopathic and chiropractic treatment, when medically necessary and applied
  by legally authorized professionals.
- Prostheses and orthopedic equipment, subject to limits, authorization and
  useful-life rules.
- Extrahospital recovery equipment, subject to authorization and limits.
- Medical equipment for ambulatory assistance after an acute event.
- Second medical opinion.
- ECMO, subject to covered disease, notice, second valuation and provider
  certification.

## Conditional / High-Risk Coverage Areas

These coverage areas need special attention because they are not simple "yes":

- Maternity.
- Reproduction-assisted maternity.
- Congenital/genetic diseases for insureds born outside policy vigency.
- Nose and paranasal sinus events.
- Hernias.
- Bariatric surgery.
- Genomic studies.
- Stem cells.
- Hyperbaric chamber.
- Knee, shoulder and spine surgery.
- Fetal surgery.
- Gene therapies and orphan drugs.
- Dental basic.
- Foreign care.
- Catastrophic diseases abroad.
- Student/temporary worker abroad.
- HIV/AIDS optional coverage.

## Advisor Translation

The advisor should not say:

"Alfa Medical covers all hospital expenses."

The safer practical statement is:

"Alfa Medical can cover medically necessary expenses from covered accidents,
illnesses and defined benefits, but each event must pass the policy conditions:
waiting periods, exclusions, territory, documentation, deductible, coinsurance,
hospital level and the contracted caratula values."
