## Product Purpose

Technical rule:

The insurer covers medically necessary expenses prescribed by the treating
physician for recovery from covered accident, illness, childbirth or cesarean,
within the stipulated territory and policy conditions.

What this means for the client:

The product helps pay major medical costs when a covered health event happens,
but only under the rules of your policy.

What Forge must not say:

"Any medical bill is covered."

## Deductible

Technical rule:

The deductible is the initial amount charged to the insured for each covered
illness or accident, identified in the policy caratula.

What this means for the client:

Before the insurer participates, you may need to absorb the first part of the
covered expense.

What Forge must not say:

"The insurer pays from peso one."

## Coinsurance

Technical rule:

Coinsurance is the percentage the insured pays from covered expenses after
deductible.

What this means for the client:

After the deductible, you may still share part of the bill with the insurer.

What Forge must not say:

"Once deductible is paid, you are done paying."

## Coinsurance Cap

Technical rule:

The cap is the maximum insured participation by coinsurance for each covered
claim, subject to policy conditions.

What this means for the client:

There may be a ceiling to your coinsurance exposure, but not every special case
uses that ceiling the same way.

What Forge must not say:

"Your maximum payment is always deductible plus cap."

## Hospital Level

Technical rule:

Alfa Medical classifies hospitals as Practico, Integro and Pleno.

What this means for the client:

The hospital you choose can change your financial participation if it does not
match your contracted level.

What Forge must not say:

"Hospital level is just a preference."

## Waiting Period

Technical rule:

Certain diseases and benefits become covered only after continuous coverage has
passed a stated time.

What this means for the client:

Having an active policy does not mean every condition is covered immediately.

What Forge must not say:

"Your policy is active, so the condition is covered."

## Accident

Technical rule:

An accident is external, sudden, fortuitous and violent, independent of the
insured's will, and not consequence of prior illness or voluntary act. If the
first expense occurs after 30 days, the event is not treated as accident.

What this means for the client:

The facts and timing around the injury matter. Not every injury is processed as
an accident.

What Forge must not say:

"Any injury is an accident."

## Preexisting Condition

Technical rule:

A preexisting illness or injury can be identified through declaration, medical
records, prior diagnosis, diagnostic tests or prior medical expenses.

What this means for the client:

If the condition existed before the policy, it may not be covered, even if the
claim happens later.

What Forge must not say:

"If you did not mention it, it does not count."

## Declared Preexistence

Technical rule:

Declared preexisting illnesses or injuries may be covered from the fourth year
only if requirements are met and the condition is not in excluded categories.

What this means for the client:

Some declared prior conditions may become eligible over time, but not all.

What Forge must not say:

"After four years every preexisting condition is covered."

## Maternity

Technical rule:

Maternity requires age, continuous coverage, timing, territory and documentation
conditions. Years 1 to 3 differ from year 4 onward.

What this means for the client:

Pregnancy coverage needs a careful review of dates and policy conditions before
assuming what will be paid.

What Forge must not say:

"Maternity is automatically included."

## Foreign Coverage

Technical rule:

Foreign benefits depend on optional coverage and whether care is programmed,
catastrophic, urgent, student/temporary worker related or assistance based.

What this means for the client:

Care abroad can be covered only under specific optional benefits and may change
what you pay.

What Forge must not say:

"Your GMM works the same abroad."

## Medical Necessity

Technical rule:

Covered expenses must be medically necessary and prescribed, but also must not
be excluded and must satisfy benefit conditions.

What this means for the client:

A doctor saying it is needed is important, but the policy still has coverage
rules.

What Forge must not say:

"Doctor prescription guarantees payment."

## Documentation

Technical rule:

Expenses must be supported with fiscal receipts, prescriptions, clinical files,
diagnostic studies, authorizations or other required evidence depending on the
benefit.

What this means for the client:

The paper trail matters. Missing documents can put the claim at risk.

What Forge must not say:

"The medical event alone is enough."
