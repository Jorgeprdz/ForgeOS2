# Forge GMM Story Structure Discovery

Status: DISCOVERY ONLY

No redesign. No Canva. No PowerPoint. No new slides. No visual changes. No
sales copy rewrite. Narrative analysis only.

## 1. Executive Summary

The current presentation structure is:

1. Product category
2. Emotional promise
3. Product concepts
4. Coverage limitations
5. Additional benefits
6. Financial examples
7. Quote

This is a reasonable educational structure.

As a sales narrative, the strongest elements appear late. The financial-impact
examples likely deserve earlier conceptual importance because they answer the
prospect's real question:

"What happens to me financially if something serious happens?"

## 2. Current Story Arc

Current arc:

Product -> Definitions -> Rules -> Benefits -> Examples -> Quote

Narrative effect:

The prospect is asked to understand GMM before emotionally needing GMM.

## 3. Prospect-Centered Story Arc

A more prospect-centered story would conceptually emphasize:

Risk -> Consequence -> Protection Logic -> Choices -> Quote

This is not a redesign instruction. It is a narrative discovery finding.

The difference:

Current:
"Here is the product and how it works."

Prospect-centered:
"Here is the financial problem this product solves, and now the mechanics make
sense."

## 4. The Strongest Slides Appear Too Late

Slides 12 and 13 are likely the strongest narrative slides because they show
financial transformation.

They appear after:

- product title
- broad protection promise
- sum insured definition
- hospital levels
- deductible definition
- coinsurance definition
- waiting periods introduction
- first-year waiting period details
- third-year waiting period details
- fourth-year waiting period details
- additional benefits

That means the prospect must process many concepts before seeing the clearest
reason to care.

## 5. Why Participation Examples Matter

The participation examples work because they translate abstract concepts into
money.

They connect:

- total expense
- deductible
- coinsurance
- client responsibility
- insurer participation
- illness vs accident difference

This is the narrative bridge between product education and client action.

## 6. Why Accident Examples Matter

The accident example is especially strong because it shows a different outcome
than illness.

It answers:

- What happens if this is an accident?
- Does the deductible still matter?
- How much does the client pay?
- How much does the insurer absorb?

It makes GMM feel practical instead of theoretical.

## 7. Why Financial Impact Examples Matter

Financial impact examples create the clearest conversion logic:

Without the product:

- the medical event may become a financial event
- savings, liquidity and family stability may be exposed

With the product:

- part of the financial shock transfers to the insurer
- client responsibility becomes more understandable
- the annual premium becomes easier to evaluate

This is the emotional-economic core of GMM.

## 8. Narrative Answers by Question

What story is currently being told?

GMM is a protective product with technical concepts, coverage rules, benefits
and quote options.

What story is the prospect experiencing?

The prospect experiences a technical lesson that eventually becomes a financial
consequence story.

What story would create greater understanding, engagement and conversion?

A story that makes the consequence visible earlier, then uses the existing
concepts to explain how the protection works.

## 9. Current Structure Classification

Product-first:
Yes.

Concept-first:
Yes.

Story-first:
No.

Problem-first:
No.

The presentation becomes problem-aware late through the examples.

## 10. Narrative Risk

The main risk is not that the presentation lacks important content.

The main risk is that the client receives important content before they feel
why that content matters.

This can create:

- passive listening
- technical fatigue
- delayed urgency
- price sensitivity at quote stage

## 11. Narrative Opportunity

The existing deck already contains the likely emotional engine:

- illness participation
- accident participation
- financial responsibility contrast
- quote options

The next evolution should explore whether these elements can become the
conceptual backbone of the story.

This PAQ does not approve new slides or redesign.

## 12. Six-Month Memory Analysis

If a prospect only remembered three ideas from this presentation six months
later, they would likely remember today:

1. GMM has deductible, coinsurance and hospital levels.
2. Accidents and illnesses can affect what the client pays.
3. The quoted annual cost depends on deductible and insured people.

Ideally, they should remember:

1. A serious medical event can become a financial shock without protection.
2. GMM turns an unpredictable medical expense into a more understandable client
   responsibility.
3. The right plan is not just a price; it is a decision about hospital access,
   liquidity and family protection.

## 13. Final Discovery

The current deck teaches the product.

The next narrative evolution should help the prospect feel the problem earlier,
then understand the product as the solution architecture.
