## 2. Truth Model Review

Finding evaluated:

"Verdad Local Predictiva"
vs
"Verdad Institucional Historica"

Decision:
APPROVED

Architectural meaning:

Forge must distinguish between two kinds of truth:

- Local Predictive Truth:
  - What Forge can infer from local evidence, observed behavior, current events, pipeline, activity and rules.
  - Useful for action, risk detection, forecast, coaching and early intervention.
  - May be probable, directional or confidence-based.

- Institutional Historical Truth:
  - What the carrier, institution, report, commission statement or official source has already confirmed.
  - Useful for audit, historical explanation, payment, reporting and official reconciliation.
  - Usually arrives later.

Why approved:

- Forge already accepts that facts, interpretations, forecasts and recommendations are different.
- Reporting lag has already been identified as a major architectural issue.
- Forecasts are suggestions, not facts.
- Production Events are facts, but some official facts may arrive late through institutional reports.

Should it become an official architecture principle?

Yes, but as an extension of existing principles, not as a new domain.

Recommended principle wording:

Forge must separate local predictive intelligence from institutional historical confirmation.

Impact on Forecast:

- Forecast must use local predictive truth with explicit confidence.
- Forecast must never present predicted outcomes as confirmed institutional truth.
- Forecast should improve before official reports arrive, but must label uncertainty.

Impact on Compensation:

- Compensation must distinguish estimated compensation from confirmed compensation.
- Payment and official commission statements remain institutional historical truth.
- Economic Motivation may use estimates only with rules, evidence and confidence.

Impact on Conservation:

- Conservation may detect local risk before official LIMRA or IGC report arrives.
- Official index reports remain institutional historical truth.
- Lag must be explicit.

Impact on Business Planning:

- Business Planning must use local predictive truth to act early.
- Business Planning must not treat plans or forecasts as confirmed outcomes.

Ratification:

- APPROVED as architecture distinction.
- APPROVED for documentation.
- NOT a new domain.


## 3. Reporting Lag Review

Finding evaluated:

Risk of coupling Forge to institutional reporting lag.

Decision:
APPROVED

Is it a real risk?

Yes.

Why:

- LIMRA and IGC may be reported with lag.
- Commission statements may arrive after production events.
- Compensation may be paid after evaluation period.
- Reinstatements and corrections may appear after close.
- Official reporting may not reflect current business reality.

Architectural risk:

If Forge waits only for official reporting:

- Advisor receives late warnings.
- Manager receives late intervention signals.
- Business Planning becomes reactive.
- Conservation risk appears after action window is already damaged.

If Forge ignores official reporting:

- Forge may invent truth.
- Compensation and audit become unreliable.
- Historical explanations become unsafe.

Correct architecture:

Forge must support both:

- Local current view.
- Official reported view.

Each view must carry:

- Source.
- Evidence.
- Period.
- Confidence.
- Reporting time.
- Effective time.
- Correction time if applicable.

Should it be documented formally?

Yes.

Where it impacts:

- Conservation Intelligence.
- Compensation Intelligence.
- Manager Compensation Intelligence.
- Forecast Intelligence.
- Business Planning Intelligence.
- Advisor Experience.

Ratification:

- APPROVED as formal architecture risk.
- APPROVED as required documentation item.
- NOT a separate domain.


## 4. Mick Ownership Review

Findings evaluated:

"Mick evalua ejecucion."
"Mick no posee tareas."

Decision:
APPROVED

What Mick owns:

- Behavior Intelligence.
- Execution signals.
- Activity completion evidence.
- Consistency signals.
- Follow-up discipline.
- Prospecting cadence.
- Coaching responsiveness as behavior.
- Task completion patterns.
- Inactivity risk.
- Execution gaps.
- Behavior trends.
- Relationship Activation behavior signals.
- Adherence to planned actions.

What Mick does not own:

- Task definition.
- Business plan ownership.
- Recruitment stage ownership.
- Advisor development stage ownership.
- Productivity metrics.
- Compensation calculations.
- Contest qualification.
- Conservation metrics.
- Manager compensation rules.
- Relationship Capital.
- Market Natural.
- P200.
- Policy lifecycle.
- Client portfolio ownership.

Important boundary:

Mick can evaluate whether a task was executed.
Mick does not own why the task exists.

Example:

Business Planning says:
This advisor needs 20 contacts this week.

Mick says:
The advisor executed 8 of 20, inconsistently, with missed follow-up.

Recruitment says:
Candidate flow is at risk.

Advisor Development says:
Development is stalled because execution is not creating field learning.

Productivity says:
Activity is not converting to output.

Why approved:

- Advisor Development already depends on Mick for behavior evidence.
- Partner Intelligence consumes Mick signals to evaluate development and unit health.
- Advisor Experience depends on behavior and learning states.
- Mick must remain structural, not motivational.

Ratification:

- APPROVED as domain ownership correction.
- Mick should be formalized before being used heavily in Productivity or Business Planning.
- NOT a task engine.
- NOT a motivational module.


## 5. Capture Once Review

Finding evaluated:

"Capture Once antes de Decision Intelligence"

Decision:
APPROVED

Meaning:

Forge should not ask users to manually capture the same fact multiple times.

If Forge can infer, import, extract or derive a reliable datum, it should not ask the user to recapture it.

Decision Intelligence depends on clean capture because duplicated capture creates:

- Conflicting facts.
- Duplicate candidates.
- Duplicate clients.
- Conflicting policy data.
- Conflicting activity records.
- Broken attribution.
- Low trust in recommendations.

Should it become an official principle?

Yes.

It is already consistent with the constitutional amendment and should remain official.

Architecture implications:

- SourceSystem must be preserved.
- EvidenceRecord must be preserved.
- ProvenanceRecord must be preserved.
- Manual override must not erase original evidence.
- Imported files should become source evidence, not parallel truth.
- Reports should be generated from Forge when Forge is the source of operational truth.

Impact on Advisor Experience:

- Benvenu must not become a long manual form.
- Clippy must not ask for data Forge already knows.
- Value Before Work depends on Capture Once.

Impact on Recruitment:

- Same candidate should not be captured repeatedly as prospect, referral and candidate without identity resolution.

Impact on Compensation:

- Commission data from reports should not require manual re-entry if evidence exists.

Ratification:

- APPROVED as official principle.
- APPROVED as precondition for reliable Decision Intelligence.
- NOT a new domain.


## 6. Economic Incentives Review

Finding evaluated:

Contest Intelligence
Compensation Intelligence
Manager Compensation
down to
Economic Incentives

Decision:
REVIEW REQUIRED

Valid part:

Contest, Compensation and Manager Compensation all influence economic behavior.

They collectively shape:

- What advisors pursue.
- What managers prioritize.
- What Partners develop.
- What actions feel urgent.
- What gaps matter economically.

Invalid or risky part:

Collapsing these domains into "Economic Incentives" can erase critical ownership boundaries.

Contest Intelligence owns:

- Contest qualification.
- Contest gaps.
- Contest groups.
- Contest policy count.

Compensation Intelligence owns:

- Advisor commission and payment explanation.
- Bonus accrual and payment explanation.
- Adjustments and reversals.

Manager Compensation Intelligence owns:

- Manager and Partner compensation rule interpretation.
- Productivity bonus.
- Connection.
- Development.
- Alta Partner.
- Apoyos.

Economic Motivation owns or may own:

- Translation of gaps into economic impact when evidence, rules and confidence exist.

Risk:

If "Economic Incentives" becomes an umbrella domain too early, it may:

- Duplicate Compensation.
- Override Contest ownership.
- Hide Manager Compensation rules.
- Produce money estimates without evidence.
- Become a motivational layer detached from facts.

Recommended ratification:

- APPROVE the observation that incentives shape behavior.
- DO NOT approve Economic Incentives as a domain yet.
- Keep under REVIEW REQUIRED.

Potential future interpretation:

Economic Incentives may become a lens, map or documentation section, not necessarily a domain.

Ratification:

- REVIEW REQUIRED.
- Not blocked for PAQ-09.
- Must not be implemented.


## 7. Relationship Capital Review

Finding evaluated:

Relationship Capital equals Market Natural.

Decision:
REJECTED

Why rejected:

Prior PAQs already separated these concepts.

Relationship Capital:

- Broader asset.
- Includes relationships, trust, access, communities, referrals, CI, social graph, professional graph and indirect access.
- Can exist beyond immediate natural market.

Market Natural:

- Conceptual layer inside Relationship Capital.
- Represents reachable initial market.
- Represents visible or accessible relationship field.
- Can be discovered through P200.

P200:

- Market Natural Discovery Artifact.
- Evidence of declared relationship inventory.
- Not Recruitment.
- Not Relationship Capital itself.

Relationship Activation:

- Use of relationship assets.
- Converts potential access into action.

Correct chain:

Relationship Capital
Market Natural
P200 Evidence
Relationship Activation
Recruitment or Sales Prospecting

Risk if accepted:

- Recruitment may retake ownership of P200.
- Market Natural may be overtreated as total relationship value.
- Business Planning may overestimate capacity from raw P200.
- Advisor Development may ignore broader relationship capital.

Ratification:

- REJECTED.
- Relationship Capital and Market Natural remain separate.


## 8. Precontract Lifecycle Review

Finding evaluated:

Precontract Lifecycle equals Advisor Lifecycle.

Decision:
REJECTED

Why rejected:

Precontract is a lifecycle before or around formal advisor entry.
Advisor Lifecycle begins or becomes official after signing or career start depending on the Rule Pack.

They may overlap operationally, but they are not the same.

Precontract Lifecycle may include:

- Candidate connected.
- Key activation.
- Official precontract window.
- Requirements.
- Policies.
- Exams.
- Folios.
- Precontract progress.
- Key expiration.
- Reactivation.
- Multiple precontract cycles.

Advisor Lifecycle may include:

- Signed advisor.
- Career Month.
- Career stage.
- Asesor en Desarrollo.
- Nuevo Profesional.
- Senior advisor.
- Partner.
- Manager.
- Director.

Risk if accepted:

- Career Month could start incorrectly.
- Precontract events could be treated as advisor status.
- Development could begin too early or too late.
- Contest eligibility could be wrong.
- Compensation eligibility could be wrong.

Correct boundary:

Recruitment produces candidate flow.
Precontract manages readiness and official entry process.
Career Intelligence owns official career status and stage.
Advisor Development transforms signed advisor into productive capacity.

Ratification:

- REJECTED.
- Precontract Lifecycle and Advisor Lifecycle must remain separate.

## 10. Product Truth Boundary Review

Findings evaluated:

Product Truth.
Underwriting Boundary.

Decision:
APPROVED

Meaning:

Forge must distinguish product truth from underwriting truth.

Product Truth:

- What product documentation says.
- Coverage.
- Benefits.
- Exclusions.
- Product rules.
- Product availability.
- Product structure.
- Commission schedule where applicable.

Underwriting Boundary:

- Whether a specific person qualifies.
- Whether risk is accepted.
- Whether exclusions apply to that client.
- Whether premium changes after underwriting.
- Whether medical, financial or occupational review changes the case.

Why it represents a real risk:

If Forge confuses Product Truth with underwriting outcome, Forge may:

- Promise coverage incorrectly.
- Overstate eligibility.
- Invent underwriting acceptance.
- Mislead advisors.
- Mislead clients.
- Create compliance risk.

Impact on Product Intelligence:

- Product Intelligence must explain documented product facts.
- It must not guarantee underwriting results.

Impact on Advisor Experience:

- Clippy may explain product features.
- Clippy must not imply approval or guaranteed coverage.

Impact on Compensation:

- Compensation should depend on issued/paid facts and rules, not assumed product eligibility.

Impact on Business Planning:

- Forecast must account for underwriting uncertainty when relevant.

Ratification:

- APPROVED as architecture boundary.
- APPROVED as risk.
- NOT a new domain.

Human Identity Node

Decision:
APPROVED WITH BOUNDARY

Architectural issue:

The same human can appear as:

- Prospect.
- Candidate.
- Client.
- Referral.
- Advisor.
- Manager.
- Partner.
- Director.
- Center of Influence.
- Beneficiary.
- Payor.
- Policy owner.

Useful pattern:

Yes.

Forge must avoid treating these as separate humans.

Central entity:

CommercialPerson already covers this need.

Therefore:

"Human Identity Node" is useful as a pattern or explanation.
It should not become a new entity if it duplicates CommercialPerson.

Is it overengineering?

It becomes overengineering if it creates another identity layer.

It is not overengineering if it reinforces:

- Durable person identity.
- Multiple roles.
- Historical role changes.
- Shared identity across Recruitment, Sales, Policy, Compensation and Relationship domains.

Ratification:

- APPROVED as pattern.
- REJECTED as new entity.
- CommercialPerson remains the canonical identity primitive.
