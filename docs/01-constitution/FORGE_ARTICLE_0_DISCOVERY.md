# FORGE Article 0 Discovery

Status: ARCHITECTURE DISCOVERY

Scope:

- No implementation.
- No code.
- No UI.
- No engines.
- No schemas.
- No Build Tree changes.
- No Constitution modification.
- No Skynet modification.
- No ratification.

## 1. Objective

Determine whether Forge requires an Article 0 above Skynet.

Skynet answers:

"What must no intelligence ever be allowed to do?"

Article 0 asks:

"What is Skynet protecting?"

## 2. Working Hypothesis

Forge exists to strengthen human judgment, not replace it.

Forge may:

- augment judgment
- inform judgment
- teach judgment
- strengthen judgment
- accelerate learning

Forge must not:

- cultivate dependency
- become the final authority on human potential
- replace professional judgment
- replace human responsibility
- reduce human growth into machine approval

## 3. Existing Constitutional Roots

This discovery is not isolated.

Existing constitutional material already includes:

- Forge augments advisors.
- The advisor retains final decision authority.
- The system augments, never replaces human judgment.
- The goal is growth, not dependency.
- Forge should develop commercial judgment, not only guide isolated decisions.
- If Forge only tells users what to do, it may improve short-term execution
  while weakening long-term judgment.

Article 0 may therefore be an organizing parent principle, not a new product
idea.

## 4. Is This a Constitutional Truth, Skynet Law or Layer Above Skynet?

Discovery answer:

Article 0 is likely a layer above Skynet.

Reason:

Skynet Laws constrain intelligence systems.

Article 0 explains why those constraints exist.

Skynet protects evidence, dignity, purpose, anti-reduction and non-manipulation.

Article 0 protects the deeper objective:

Human judgment must become stronger because Forge exists.

## 5. Does Forge Require Article 0?

Yes, as a candidate.

Forge requires Article 0 if its intelligence systems become powerful enough to
create dependency.

Without Article 0, Forge might optimize for:

- more correct recommendations
- faster answers
- more automation
- higher short-term execution
- less friction

Those are useful, but incomplete.

Forge must optimize for:

- better human judgment
- better professional responsibility
- better decision quality
- better learning
- better independence over time

## 6. Relationship Between Constitution, Article 0, Skynet and Intelligence Systems

Recommended conceptual hierarchy:

```text
Article 0
└── Forge Constitution
    └── Skynet Governance Layer
        ├── Skynet Laws
        ├── Contextual Policies
        └── Intelligence Systems
```

Article 0:

Defines the highest purpose of Forge governance.

Forge Constitution:

Defines identity, principles, domains and constitutional truth.

Skynet:

Defines non-negotiable constraints all intelligence systems must obey.

Intelligence Systems:

Interpret facts, generate insights and recommend actions within constitutional
limits.

## 7. Can a System Become Harmful by Being Too Successful?

Yes.

A system can become harmful if it is so useful that it removes the need for the
user to practice judgment.

Risk pattern:

```text
Better recommendation
↓
Less user reasoning
↓
More dependency
↓
Weaker judgment
↓
More reliance on system
```

In that pattern, short-term performance improves while long-term professional
capacity weakens.

Forge must avoid that failure mode.

## 8. Human Judgment as Protected Constitutional Value

Human judgment preservation should be a protected constitutional value.

Reason:

Career OS, Professional Development, Professional Excellence, Readiness and
Skynet all assume the human professional must continue growing.

If Forge replaces judgment, it defeats Career OS.

If Forge strengthens judgment, it fulfills Career OS.

## 9. Evidence That Supports the Hypothesis

Evidence supporting Article 0:

- Existing Constitution says Forge augments rather than replaces advisors.
- Existing Constitution says advisor retains final decision authority.
- Existing Constitution says intelligence should transfer progressively to the
  user.
- Amendment v1.1 says Forge should develop commercial judgment over time.
- Professional Development discovery says growth requires interpreted action.
- Skynet discovery says intelligence requires constraints before being trusted.

## 10. Evidence That Would Reject or Limit the Hypothesis

Evidence that could reject or limit Article 0:

- If Forge's intended role becomes pure automation rather than development.
- If users are not expected to grow judgment through use.
- If manager or institutional control becomes the true product objective.
- If domains require fully automated decisions without human responsibility.
- If "advisor-first" is abandoned.

No current discovery supports those outcomes.

## 11. Dependency Protections if Forge Becomes More Capable

Protections should include:

- explanations that reveal reasoning
- evidence labels
- uncertainty visibility
- user-owned final decision
- progressive teaching
- prompts that ask the user to reason before accepting
- learning-by-doing workflows
- development feedback
- no hidden total authority
- no replacement of professional responsibility
- no automatic escalation from recommendation to action without context

Forge should move from:

"Do this because Forge says so."

To:

"Here is the evidence, here is the reasoning, here is the recommended action,
and here is what you should learn from it."

## 12. Article 0 Candidate Wording

Candidate:

Forge exists to strengthen human judgment, not replace it.

Expanded candidate:

Forge may augment, inform, teach and accelerate professional judgment, but must
never cultivate dependency, replace human responsibility or become the final
authority on human potential.

This is not ratified.

## 13. Discovery Verdict

CANDIDATE FOR ARTICLE 0.

Reason:

The principle appears above Skynet because it defines what Skynet is protecting.

Skynet governs intelligence behavior.

Article 0 governs the purpose of governance itself.

No ratification occurs in this document.
