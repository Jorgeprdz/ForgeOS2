FORGE DEVELOPMENT LOCK CANDIDATES

Status:
CONSTITUTIONAL LOCK CANDIDATES / NOT APPROVED

Purpose:
List candidate constitutional statements for Professional Development. These are
not ratified.

LOCK CANDIDATE 1

Professional Development is a first-class architectural concept.

Reason:
Career OS must understand how people grow, not only where they are or what
excellence looks like.

Risk:
Requires strong boundaries to avoid motivational scoring and surveillance.

LOCK CANDIDATE 2

Development is caused by interpreted action over time.

Reason:
Growth requires action, feedback, interpretation and adjustment.

Risk:
May be oversimplified if treated as a fixed formula.

LOCK CANDIDATE 3

Information is not development.

Reason:
Content exposure does not prove capability or behavior change.

Risk:
Learning products often mistake completion for growth.

LOCK CANDIDATE 4

Experience must be interpreted to become development.

Reason:
Experience can create wisdom or bad habits depending on feedback and reflection.

Risk:
Harder to measure than tenure.

LOCK CANDIDATE 5

Purpose is context, not pressure.

Reason:
Purpose can sustain growth but must not become coercion or motivation scoring.

Risk:
Purpose Snapshot misuse is a serious trust risk.

LOCK CANDIDATE 6

Managers develop people by diagnosing bottlenecks, not merely inspecting
results.

Reason:
Manager development requires evidence, coaching and next actions.

Risk:
Manager dashboards may drift into surveillance.

LOCK CANDIDATE 7

Accountability must preserve dignity.

Reason:
Accountability accelerates growth only when it supports ownership without shame.

Risk:
Performance pressure can masquerade as accountability.

LOCK CANDIDATE 8

Growth is non-linear.

Reason:
Development includes plateaus, setbacks, recovery and reactivation.

Risk:
Systems may overreact to short-term dips.

LOCK CANDIDATE 9

Professional Development must not be reduced to one score or public ranking.

Reason:
Growth is multi-dimensional and contextual.

Risk:
Rankings distort behavior and create pressure.

LOCK CANDIDATE 10

Mick contributes development evidence but does not define the whole development
model.

Reason:
Mick sees behavior patterns, while development also includes feedback,
reflection, purpose, manager support, environment and responsibility.

Risk:
Behavior data may be over-weighted because it is easiest to observe.

LOCK CANDIDATE 11

Business Planning translates development priorities into operating commitments.

Reason:
Plans should help a person practice the next development behavior.

Risk:
Planning can become static paperwork if not connected to action.

LOCK CANDIDATE 12

Forge accelerates growth by improving the development loop, not by applying
pressure.

Reason:
Acceleration should come from clarity, friction reduction, feedback,
interpretation and better coaching.

Risk:
False urgency and motivational theater may feel faster but damage trust.

RECOMMENDED LOCK ORDER

Priority 1:
Information is not development.

Priority 2:
Experience must be interpreted to become development.

Priority 3:
Purpose is context, not pressure.

Priority 4:
Accountability must preserve dignity.

Priority 5:
Professional Development must not be reduced to one score or public ranking.

Priority 6:
Professional Development is a first-class architectural concept.

WHY THIS ORDER

Protect the human boundaries first.

Then lock the concept.

If Forge locks Professional Development without these guardrails, the concept
could drift into pressure, surveillance or training theater.

FINAL VERDICT

CANDIDATE FOR LOCK.

Reason:
Professional Development is necessary for Career OS, but final lock should wait
for evidence contracts, ownership, manager visibility rules and no-ranking
protections.
