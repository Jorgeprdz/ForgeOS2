# Forge Ethical Boundary Review

Status: CHECKPOINT REVIEW

Documentation only. No implementation. No code. No engines. No schemas. No UI.
No Build Tree changes. No architecture modifications.

## 1. Executive Summary

The discoveries create useful decision insight, but also ethical risk.

Readiness, friction and Action Safety could be misused to pressure people into
compliance.

The ethical boundary is:

Forge may support understanding, agency and responsible action.

Forge must not optimize manipulation, pressure, coercion or blind compliance.

## 2. Article 0 Review

Article 0 candidate:

Forge exists to strengthen human judgment, not replace it.

Implication:

Decision discoveries should help people reason better, not make Forge the final
authority on what they should do.

Boundary:

Forge must preserve human responsibility and agency.

## 3. Human Judgment Preservation Review

Human Judgment Preservation requires that users do not become dependent on
Forge for every professional judgment.

Implication:

Readiness, Friction and Action Safety should teach advisors and managers to
interpret human decisions more responsibly.

Boundary:

Forge should not hide evidence behind conclusions.

## 4. WALL-E Problem Review

The WALL-E problem warns that systems can become so effective that humans stop
developing judgment.

Implication:

Forge must not make advisors, managers or clients more compliant but less
thoughtful.

Boundary:

Decision support should strengthen reasoning, not bypass it.

## 5. Skynet Governance Review

Relevant Skynet candidates:

- Evidence Before Judgment
- Human Dignity Must Be Preserved
- Purpose Is Context, Not Pressure
- Production Is Evidence, Not Proof
- No Single Score Represents Human Growth

Implication:

Human decision signals must be evidence-based and must not become scores,
labels or pressure triggers.

Boundary:

No intelligence should turn readiness or friction into a verdict about a person.

## 6. Decision Clarity First Review

Decision Clarity First means Forge should help people understand what to do,
why, and with what evidence.

Implication:

The goal is not more action at any cost.

The goal is better action through clearer understanding.

## 7. Boundaries Against Manipulation

Forge must not:

- exploit hidden fears
- convert Action Safety into urgency pressure
- treat friction as weakness
- use readiness signals to push harder
- convert human hesitation into a sales tactic
- optimize for compliance
- hide uncertainty
- frame Forge outputs as authority
- bypass spouse, partner or decision-maker alignment

## 8. Boundaries Preserving Agency

Forge should preserve:

- informed choice
- voluntary action
- ability to pause
- ability to disagree
- ability to ask for evidence
- clear distinction between recommendation and decision
- human ownership of commitment
- respect for non-action when non-action is responsible

## 9. Ethical Verdict

The discoveries are valuable only if bounded by agency, dignity, evidence and
judgment preservation.

Future implementation should be blocked until these concepts are validated and
ethical safeguards are defined.
