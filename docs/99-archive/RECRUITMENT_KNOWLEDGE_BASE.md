## Section 1: Recruitment Philosophy

### What A Manager Really Looks For

A manager is not only looking for a person who wants a job. The desired recruit has the potential to become an independent advisor who can create activity, follow a proven process, generate trust, build a market, recover from rejection, and keep executing after the excitement of the opportunity fades.

Core manager question:

- Can this person become productive through training, activity, market access, and coaching?

The manager is looking for evidence of:

- Producer potential.
- Entrepreneurial mindset.
- Money motivation tied to activity.
- Coachability.
- Natural market access.
- Referral development ability.
- Character and integrity.
- Learning speed.
- Self-management.
- Resilience under rejection.

### Activity vs Capacity To Generate Activity

Activity is a countable output:

- Calls.
- Contacts.
- Referrals obtained.
- Initial interviews.
- Closing interviews.
- Requests / applications.
- Paid policies.
- 25-point activity completion.

Capacity to generate activity is the underlying ability to keep producing those outputs:

- Has enough market to call.
- Can ask for referrals.
- Can follow scripts.
- Can recover from rejection.
- Can organize agenda.
- Can work without constant external push.
- Can accept manager correction.

Forge should not confuse one busy week with durable activity capacity.

### Market vs Market Size

Market size is the number of names available.

Market quality is whether those names are useful for advisor activity.

Market accessibility is whether the candidate can actually reach them.

Market response probability is whether those people are likely to answer, accept a meeting, refer, or buy.

A large list without trust, access, solvency, need, or decision power is not a strong market. A smaller but high-trust, high-access, high-quality market may be more valuable.

### Why RDA Is The Best Source

RDA means Referido de Asesor. In recruitment and early advisor development, RDA is valuable because it can identify people who may already have:

- A relationship bridge.
- Trust through a known person.
- Higher probability of taking a meeting.
- Better market quality than cold outreach.
- Better fit for advisor recruitment or commercial prospecting.

RDA is also evidence that the candidate understands the business is referral-driven and not only based on personal charisma.

### Why Cold Recruitment Has More Risk

Cold recruitment has more risk because the manager has less evidence about:

- Character.
- Trustworthiness.
- Local reputation.
- Natural market quality.
- Coachability.
- Activity discipline.
- Reason for considering the opportunity.

Cold recruitment may still work, but it needs stronger validation through interviews, assignments, market proof, and follow-through.

## Section 2: Hard Factors

Hard factors are context signals. They are not automatic acceptance or rejection rules.

### Age

Description:

- Candidate life stage and potential commercial maturity.

Evidence:

- Date of birth or age collected during initial interview.

Expected impact:

- Helps manager understand maturity, energy, urgency, and career timing.

Risks:

- Age missing.
- Candidate is outside the expected recruiting profile and requires manager review.
- Manager uses age as a shortcut instead of evaluating evidence.

### Marriage / Household Context

Description:

- Household situation, support system, and decision context.

Evidence:

- Candidate states marital status, who they live with, or relevant household structure.

Expected impact:

- Helps detect stability, decision dependencies, and who may influence the career decision.

Risks:

- Household context missing.
- Candidate has unresolved family or partner alignment.
- Manager assumes marital status alone predicts success.

### Years Living In Town

Description:

- Local roots and relationship density.

Evidence:

- Candidate states where they live, how long they have lived there, and where they lived before.

Expected impact:

- Strong local roots can support trust, warm market access, and referrals.

Risks:

- Little local history.
- Recent move with no compensating network.
- Local roots exist but relationships are weak or inaccessible.

### Career

Description:

- Candidate work history and commercial exposure.

Evidence:

- Current job, prior jobs, first job story, professional background, sales or business experience.

Expected impact:

- Commercial, service, management, entrepreneurial, or relationship-heavy careers may transfer well into advisor activity.

Risks:

- Career history missing.
- Candidate has no evidence of ownership, client contact, or activity discipline.
- Candidate wants career upside but rejects sales behavior.

### Employment

Description:

- Current work status and activity base.

Evidence:

- Candidate explains where they work, what they like, what they dislike, and why they may change.

Expected impact:

- Current activity can indicate discipline and urgency.

Risks:

- Employment status missing.
- Unstable employment with no ownership.
- Second job conflict that blocks training, calls, meetings, or follow-through.

### Dependents

Description:

- Economic responsibility and urgency context.

Evidence:

- Candidate states whether they have economic dependents.

Expected impact:

- Dependents may strengthen urgency and income motivation, but can also increase risk if transition planning is weak.

Risks:

- Financial pressure without realistic ramp plan.
- Family decision makers not aligned.
- Candidate cannot tolerate variable early income.

### Income History

Description:

- Prior income baseline and evidence of economic ambition.

Evidence:

- Highest annual income, current income, first-year target, five-year target.

Expected impact:

- Helps manager compare income ambition with previous achievement and realistic activity requirements.

Risks:

- No income target.
- Unrealistic target without activity plan.
- Low money motivation for a production-based career.

### Market Access

Description:

- Candidate ability to reach people who know and trust them.

Evidence:

- Natural market list, Project 200, best 30 prospects, first 5 RDA, referral names, relationship categories.

Expected impact:

- Determines whether candidate can begin activity quickly and build momentum.

Risks:

- Candidate knows people but cannot access them.
- Large list without trust or response probability.
- Candidate resists asking for referrals.

## Section 3: Vital Factors

### Mental Agility

Definition:

- Ability to understand the business, learn scripts, process feedback, explain ideas, and adapt without losing discipline.

How to detect:

- Candidate answers self-awareness questions clearly.
- Candidate explains why natural market works.
- Candidate understands what, how, and to whom the business sells.

Positive signals:

- Learns quickly.
- Connects concepts.
- Gives specific examples.
- Understands process before judging it.

Negative signals:

- Confused after repeated explanation.
- Cannot explain career fit.
- Rejects methods without understanding them.

### Drive

Definition:

- Internal push to pursue goals, produce activity, and keep moving through difficulty.

How to detect:

- Motivation for considering the career.
- Unfinished goals and what must change.
- Competitiveness.
- Past success pattern.

Positive signals:

- Clear goals.
- Competitive examples.
- Ownership of unfinished goals.
- Willingness to work for income upside.

Negative signals:

- Passive language.
- Blames others.
- Wants results without activity.

### Energy

Definition:

- Capacity to sustain prospecting, learning, interviews, and social activity.

How to detect:

- Hobbies, extracurriculars, work rhythm, interview presence, follow-through.

Positive signals:

- Active lifestyle.
- Consistent engagement.
- Shows urgency and pace.

Negative signals:

- Low initiative.
- Avoids activity.
- Enthusiasm only in conversation, not assignments.

### Money Motivation

Definition:

- Healthy desire to improve income and willingness to tie income to performance.

How to detect:

- Income expectation, highest annual income, first-year target, five-year income target.

Positive signals:

- Specific income goals.
- Connects money to effort and career growth.
- Understands variable compensation.

Negative signals:

- Avoids money discussion.
- Wants high income without prospecting.
- Needs guaranteed employment economics.

### Character

Definition:

- Reliability, ownership, honesty, and behavior under pressure.

How to detect:

- Failure recovery story, first job story, weakness discussion, how others describe the candidate.

Positive signals:

- Owns mistakes.
- Learns from failure.
- Keeps commitments.
- Shows respect for process and people.

Negative signals:

- Blames consistently.
- Manipulates or exaggerates.
- High energy with weak ethics.

### Integrity

Definition:

- Trustworthiness and alignment with professional responsibility.

How to detect:

- Consistency between claims and evidence, references, boss/friend descriptions, respect for client trust.

Positive signals:

- Honest self-description.
- Protects relationships.
- Understands client responsibility.

Negative signals:

- Inflated claims.
- Disregard for client trust.
- Uses relationships carelessly.

### Success History

Definition:

- Evidence that the candidate has completed meaningful goals before.

How to detect:

- Success pattern, school/work achievements, first job, prior income, goals completed.

Positive signals:

- Repeated wins.
- Completed hard goals.
- Can explain what drove success.

Negative signals:

- No clear achievement pattern.
- Many unfinished goals with no correction.
- Success depended only on external structure.

### Retention Potential

Definition:

- Likelihood that the candidate can stay through training, early rejection, and precontract pressure.

How to detect:

- Five-year vision, career motivation, family alignment, recovery from failure, process acceptance.

Positive signals:

- Long-term career view.
- Understands ramp period.
- Accepts coaching and structure.

Negative signals:

- Short-term job shopping.
- Wants quick money only.
- Family or second job conflict blocks continuity.

## Section 4: Coachability

Coachability is the candidate's willingness and ability to become productive through a proven system.

Positive signals:

- `followsProcess`: accepts the sequence before trying to redesign it.
- `acceptsFeedback`: listens to manager correction and applies it.
- `completesAssignments`: finishes readings, lists, forms, calls, and preparation.
- `memorizesScripts`: learns scripts before personalizing them.
- `attendsInterviews`: arrives to interviews and follow-up meetings consistently.

Red flags:

- `wantsOwnSystem`: wants to replace the system before learning it.
- `ignoresProcess`: treats training, scripts, agenda, or activity tracking as optional.
- `missesAssignments`: does not complete readings, Project 200, RDA, scripts, or forms.
- `missesInterviews`: no-shows, repeated reschedules, or low respect for manager time.

Operational evidence:

- Readings completed before selection interview.
- Project 200 started.
- Best 30 prospects identified.
- First 5 RDA identified.
- Scripts reviewed or memorized.
- Agenda and activity records filled.
- Role-play and clinic participation.

## Section 5: Interview Intelligence

### INITIAL_INTERVIEW

Objective:

- Start relationship, gather identity/context, detect early fit, and explain career opportunity at a high level.

Expected evidence:

- Personal, professional, academic, and hobby context.
- Residence and household context.
- Current employment.
- Income expectation.
- Five-year vision.
- Early referral or dream-team names.

Red flags:

- Vague identity.
- No income expectation.
- No future vision.
- Weak or inaccessible market.
- Resistance to questionnaire or process.

Advancement criteria:

- Candidate is open to the opportunity.
- Basic context is complete enough.
- No severe character/process risk appears.
- Candidate can move to selection interview with profile/readings.

### SELECTION_INTERVIEW

Objective:

- Evaluate motivation, vital factors, market understanding, referral orientation, and process fit.

Expected evidence:

- Candidate read materials.
- Candidate discussed career with relevant decision support if needed.
- Candidate explains why they are considering the career.
- Candidate discusses unfinished goals, first job, failure recovery, competitiveness, income goals, best friend/boss feedback, and success pattern.
- Candidate understands what is sold, how it is sold, and to whom it is sold.

Red flags:

- Did not read assigned material.
- No ownership of failure or unfinished goals.
- Rejects scripts.
- Does not understand natural market trust.
- No money motivation.
- Cannot name people or referral sources.

Advancement criteria:

- Motivation is specific.
- Vital factors are credible.
- Candidate accepts scripts and process.
- Natural market/referral signals are workable.
- No severe character or integrity concern.

### CAREER_INTERVIEW

Objective:

- Sell the career as a business opportunity and verify commitment after explaining training, compensation, and path.

Expected evidence:

- Candidate understands the advisor path, precontract stage, training, cédula requirements, activity expectations, and compensation structure.
- Candidate can connect the career to personal goals.
- Candidate accepts that income depends on activity and production.

Red flags:

- Likes income upside but avoids activity.
- Wants freedom without accountability.
- Treats training as optional.
- Rejects precontract or licensing requirements.
- Decision support remains unresolved.

Advancement criteria:

- Candidate wants the actual career, not only the idea of income.
- Candidate accepts training and activity structure.
- Candidate is ready for operational start or additional interview.

### ADDITIONAL_INTERVIEW

Objective:

- Begin operational execution and validate first preparation behaviors.

Expected evidence:

- Project 200 progress.
- Best 30 prospects.
- First 5 RDA.
- Script memorization or role-play.
- Agenda discipline.
- Assignment completion.

Red flags:

- No market list.
- No RDA.
- Scripts not memorized.
- Missed assignments.
- Excuses instead of correction.

Advancement criteria:

- Candidate completes first operational assignments.
- Manager sees coachability and activity readiness.
- Candidate can transition toward precontract preparation.

## Section 6: Market Strength

### Project 200

Definition:

- Broad list of people the candidate knows or can access.

What Forge should capture:

- Count.
- Relationship categories.
- Contactability.
- Trust level.
- Qualification notes.

### Project 30

Definition:

- The best 30 qualified prospects selected from the broader market list.

What Forge should capture:

- Why each person is a strong prospect.
- Whether they are contactable.
- Whether they fit qualification criteria.
- Priority order.

### 5 RDA

Definition:

- First five advisor referral candidates or referral sources.

What Forge should capture:

- Name.
- Why the person may be an option.
- Relationship source.
- Probability of introduction.
- Whether the person can create more names.

### Natural Market

Definition:

- People who know the candidate and are easier to contact because trust already exists.

Evaluation dimensions:

- Size.
- Quality.
- Accessibility.
- Response probability.
- Referral potential.

### NASAT Framework

The manual frames qualified prospect thinking through NASAT-like criteria:

- Need: person may benefit from protection, savings, retirement, health, or risk planning.
- Assurance / insurability: person appears reasonably insurable or has relevant protection context.
- Solvency: person can start and maintain a long-term protection or savings project.
- Access: person knows the advisor or can be reached.
- Takes decision: person can decide or materially influence the financial decision.

Forge should treat NASAT as market quality evidence, not as a guarantee.

### Market Size

Evidence:

- Project 200 count.
- Project 30 count.
- Contact list completeness.

Risk:

- Big list without qualification.

### Market Quality

Evidence:

- NASAT fit.
- Trust level.
- Solvency.
- Need.
- Decision power.

Risk:

- Names exist but are weak prospects.

### Accessibility

Evidence:

- Phone/email/social access.
- Warm relationship.
- Referral bridge.

Risk:

- Candidate knows names but cannot call them.

### Response Probability

Evidence:

- Relationship warmth.
- Referral bridge.
- Prior willingness to answer.
- Candidate confidence calling them.

Risk:

- Market may ignore calls or refuse meetings.

## Section 7: Success Indicators

Early positive signals:

- Self-management: fills agenda, tracks activity, arrives prepared.
- Initiative: starts Project 200, asks questions, creates names without being pushed.
- Activity generation: produces calls, referrals, initial interviews, and closing interviews.
- Learning speed: understands scripts, products, sales cycle, and natural market logic quickly.
- Coachability: applies feedback and follows process.
- Hunger: clear money motivation and career ambition.
- Execution: completes assignments and converts intent into action.

Manager interpretation:

- Success is not only enthusiasm. It is repeated evidence of activity, learning, market access, and coachability.
- Activity is not only an outcome; it is an early predictor of survival in recruitment, precontract and advisor ramp-up.
- Mick / Behavior Intelligence should consume these signals to detect habits, discipline and execution patterns before production results appear.

## Section 8: Failure Indicators

Early negative signals:

- Inactivity: no calls, no names, no agenda discipline.
- No market response: names exist but do not answer or trust is weak.
- Second job conflict: candidate cannot attend training, call, or meet prospects.
- Low commitment: candidate remains interested but does not act.
- Poor follow-through: assignments are late or incomplete.
- Repeated missed tasks: readings, Project 200, RDA, scripts, forms, interviews.
- Low coachability: ignores feedback, rejects scripts, wants own system too early.

Manager interpretation:

- Failure usually appears before production failure. It appears first as missed assignments, weak market evidence, low activity, or resistance to correction.

### Production Indicators

- Few or no citas.
- No solicitudes.

### Required Signal Terms

25 points:

- Activity tracking tool to determine whether the advisor's day was productive through prospecting and high-productivity activity.
- 25 points is a behavior signal. It shows whether the candidate or advisor is building the habit of productive activity, not only whether a final sale happened.

RDA:

- Referido de Asesor; strong source for recruiting and market leverage.

Llamadas:

- Calls made to contact natural market or referrals.

Referidos:

- Names obtained through relationships; recurring fuel of the business.

Citas:

- Scheduled or completed meetings.

Solicitudes:

- Applications/requests created from closing activity.

Pólizas pagadas:

- Paid policies; production evidence, not just intent.

## Section 10: Recruitment Decision Signals

These are evidence requirements for future Forge decision support. They are not implemented logic.

### ADVANCE

Evidence Forge would need:

- Hard factors complete enough for manager confidence.
- Vital factors show drive, money motivation, character, integrity, and retention potential.
- Candidate follows process and completes assignments.
- Market is workable in size, quality, access, and response probability.
- Candidate accepts scripts, training, and manager feedback.
- No severe character, integrity, or commitment risk.

Decision meaning:

- Candidate should move to the next recruitment phase.

### WATCH

Evidence Forge would need:

- Candidate has potential but evidence is incomplete, mixed, or not yet proven.
- Some signals are strong but another dimension needs validation.
- Market, coachability, motivation, or family/decision alignment needs another manager read.

Decision meaning:

- Candidate may continue, but manager should inspect specific risks before advancing.

### COACH

Evidence Forge would need:

- Candidate has potential but needs manager intervention before advancing.
- Coachability is present enough to justify development.
- Weakness is correctable through assignments, market work, script practice, or activity discipline.

Decision meaning:

- Assign coaching, not automatic advancement.

### REJECT

Evidence Forge would need:

- Severe character or integrity risk.
- Very low coachability.
- Repeated missed assignments/interviews.
- No viable market access and no willingness to build it.
- Low activity commitment.
- Candidate rejects core career mechanics.

Decision meaning:

- Do not advance the candidate in the current attempt. Preserve history for future reentry review if appropriate.

## Signals Detected

- Manager seeks producer potential, not just interest in a job.
- RDA and natural market are central to both recruiting and production.
- Market quality requires need, solvency, access, decision power, and trust.
- Project 200, best 30 prospects, and first 5 RDA are operational readiness signals.
- 25 points represents daily activity discipline.
- Scripts memorized are a coachability and process signal.
- First job, failure recovery, unfinished goals, competitiveness, and income goals are vital-factor evidence.
- Precontract readiness depends on activity, learning, and production signals.
- Large market without coachability or execution remains risky.

## Gaps Found

- Full Career Interview and Additional Interview scripts are not fully represented in the extracted interview source; career PDFs focus heavily on opportunity, training, compensation, and contracting.
- Current Interview schema phase names do not match the business phases documented here.
- Evidence contracts now exist for `questionEvidence[]`, `interviewEvidence`, `marketEvidence`, `rda`, `project200` and `precontractActivityEvidence`, but no engine consumes them yet.
- Candidate Assessment weights are foundation heuristics and not yet moved to Organization Profile / Office Rules Config.

- RDA now has a base contract, but no RDA scoring or lifecycle engine exists yet.
- Project 30 and 5 RDA are documented operational concepts, but only Project 200 has a dedicated base schema today.

1. Build `interview-knowledge-extraction-engine` only after interview phase contracts are updated.
