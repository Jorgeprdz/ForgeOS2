# Forge Client Presentation Simulation

## Presentation for the Advisor

### Slide 1: Case Snapshot

This case has two documents:

- A quote for Lariza Saenz, age 23.
- A policy caratula for Franco Villanueva Najera, age 0.

The documents do not appear to belong to the same insured.

Advisor action:

Confirm whether the quote is the correct proposal for this issued policy.

### Slide 2: What Was Proposed

The quote proposed:

- Alfa Medical Integro.
- National coverage.
- $170,000,000 sum insured.
- $25,000 deductible.
- 10% coinsurance.
- $97,000 coinsurance cap.
- Gamma tabulator.
- Annual premium: $47,388.27.
- Optional accident deductible elimination.

### Slide 3: What Was Issued

The policy issued:

- Alfa Medical Integro.
- National coverage.
- $160,000,000 sum insured.
- $40,000 deductible.
- 10% coinsurance.
- $85,000 coinsurance cap.
- Gamma tabulator.
- Monthly payment form.
- Total shown: $25,921.82.
- CEDA active: accident deductible elimination.

### Slide 4: Key Differences

Important changes:

- Different insured.
- Different start date.
- Different zone.
- Lower sum insured.
- Higher deductible.
- Lower coinsurance cap.
- Different premium/payment structure.
- Protection Patrimonial not covered in issued policy.

### Slide 5: Client Conversation

Say this plainly:

"The policy you actually own is the caratula. The quote helped compare a
possible scenario, but the caratula is what tells us what was issued."

Then confirm:

- Is Franco the intended insured?
- Is the $40,000 deductible acceptable?
- Did you expect Protection Patrimonial?
- Did you expect foreign coverage?
- Do you understand when accident deductible elimination applies?

### Slide 6: Advisor Follow-Up

Immediate follow-up questions:

- Was the Lariza quote attached by mistake?
- Was this quote only an example?
- Is there a matching quote for Franco?
- Did the client approve the $40,000 deductible?
- Were optional coverages intentionally declined?

## Presentation for the Client

### Slide 1: What You Bought

You bought Alfa Medical Integro for Franco.

It is a national medical-expense policy designed to help with large covered
medical bills in Mexico.

### Slide 2: Your Main Protection

Your policy shows:

- $160,000,000 protection limit.
- National territory.
- Gamma medical tabulator.
- Integro plan.

In plain language:

This is meant to protect the family from a major medical bill, not from every
small medical expense.

### Slide 3: What You Still Pay

You may still pay:

- The first $40,000 of a covered event.
- 10% of covered expenses after that.
- Up to the coinsurance cap shown in the policy.
- Costs that the policy does not recognize or does not cover.

### Slide 4: Accident Protection

You added CEDA:

Cobertura de Eliminacion de Deducible por Accidente.

Plain language:

If Franco has a covered accident and the accident meets the policy rules, this
can remove the deductible for that accident.

It does not mean every accident has zero cost.

### Slide 5: What Is Not Active

Your caratula shows:

- VIH: not covered.
- Protection Patrimonial: not covered.

Plain language:

If you expected those benefits, ask your advisor now. The caratula does not
show them as active.

### Slide 6: What To Watch

Pay special attention to:

- Any treatment outside Mexico.
- Any hospital choice.
- Any condition that may have started before the policy.
- Any missing documents during a claim.
- Any benefit you remember from the quote but do not see in the caratula.

### Slide 7: Best Next Step

Ask your advisor:

"Can you confirm that this is the final policy we intended to issue, and can
you walk me through the differences from the quote?"
