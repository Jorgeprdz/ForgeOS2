FIRST WOW MOMENT CANDIDATES

Status:
ARCHITECTURE DISCOVERY / NOT IMPLEMENTED

Selection rule:
The winning candidate must create immediate value in less than 30 seconds
without asking for new data, showing a CRM dashboard or pretending to know
something Forge cannot prove.

1. ONE RELATIONSHIP WORTH REVIEWING

Concept:
Forge surfaces one client, prospect or relationship that deserves attention.

Example:
"Forge found one relationship worth reviewing today."

Explainable reason:
"There is recent activity, but no clear next action recorded."

Why it could wow:
It feels human and practical. It turns stored context into an immediate
decision.

Required information:
- One relationship record or imported context.
- Recent activity or absence of next action.
- Evidence label.

Risk:
If evidence is weak, it can feel invented.

Verdict:
Best primary candidate.

2. ONE NEXT ACTION FOUND

Concept:
Forge presents a single next action based on existing context.

Example:
"Your next useful move may be a follow-up message to Ana."

Explainable reason:
"Last interaction exists, but there is no follow-up outcome."

Why it could wow:
It immediately answers "what do I do now?"

Required information:
- Contact or opportunity context.
- Last interaction.
- Missing or stale next action.

Risk:
Could feel too operational if presented like a task manager.

Verdict:
Strong candidate if tone stays human.

3. ONE OVERDUE FOLLOW-UP SIGNAL

Concept:
Forge notices one follow-up gap.

Example:
"This relationship may be waiting on you."

Explainable reason:
"A follow-up was expected, and no completed action appears after it."

Why it could wow:
It shows Forge protects commercial consistency.

Required information:
- Follow-up expectation.
- Activity history.
- Completion status.

Risk:
Can feel accusatory if copy is not restrained.

Verdict:
Useful, but should avoid shame.

4. ONE POLICY OR RENEWAL SIGNAL

Concept:
Forge surfaces one policy-related attention point.

Example:
"There may be one policy moment worth reviewing."

Explainable reason:
"A policy event exists near the current period."

Why it could wow:
It turns operations into an advisor action.

Required information:
- Policy context.
- Relevant date/event.
- Evidence source.

Risk:
Could become too technical or dashboard-like.

Verdict:
Good if policy data is reliable.

5. ONE OPPORTUNITY FROM EXISTING CONTEXT

Concept:
Forge identifies one potential opportunity signal.

Example:
"Forge found one conversation that could become an opportunity."

Explainable reason:
"There is a positive interaction signal and no active opportunity recorded."

Why it could wow:
It connects conversation intelligence with revenue generation.

Required information:
- Conversation or interaction context.
- Opportunity absence/presence.
- Confidence label.

Risk:
Must not invent intent or financial value.

Verdict:
Powerful, but higher risk.

6. ZERO-DATA HONEST FIRST WOW

Concept:
If Forge has no reliable context, it says so and shows what will happen next
without pretending.

Example:
"I do not have enough context yet to point to a real opportunity. I will not
invent one."

Follow-up:
"Let's start with where you want to go."

Why it could wow:
Honesty creates trust. It proves Forge will not fake intelligence.

Required information:
- No reliable data state.

Risk:
Less visually exciting.

Verdict:
Best fallback when context is missing.

7. IMPORTED CONTEXT COMES ALIVE

Concept:
Forge briefly shows that existing records are not just stored; they can become
signals.

Example:
"These are not just records. They can become decisions."

Explainable reason:
"Forge can connect relationships, activity, policies and next actions when
evidence exists."

Why it could wow:
It reframes data as intelligence.

Required information:
- Imported records or demo context.

Risk:
Can become too abstract if no concrete action appears.

Verdict:
Good visual support, weaker as the whole moment.

8. TOMORROW'S AGENDA PREVIEW

Concept:
Forge shows a calm preview of one meaningful action for tomorrow.

Example:
"Tomorrow can start with one clear move."

Explainable reason:
"This action comes from existing relationship or policy context."

Why it could wow:
It creates a feeling of order and relief.

Required information:
- Date/session context.
- One eligible action.

Risk:
Can look like a calendar feature instead of Forge intelligence.

Verdict:
Secondary candidate.

REJECTED EXPERIENCE TYPES

Reject:
- Profile setup wizard.
- "Complete your profile" flow.
- Onboarding checklist.
- Product tour.
- Dashboard reveal.
- KPI wall.
- Motivation quiz.
- Sales goal input.
- Compensation target input.
- Purpose Snapshot before Future You Conversation.
- Any experience that needs multiple required fields.
- Any experience that promises income, productivity or transformation.

RECOMMENDED SHORTLIST

1. One Relationship Worth Reviewing.
2. One Next Action Found.
3. Zero-Data Honest First Wow.

The recommended primary candidate is One Relationship Worth Reviewing because
it is advisor-first, human, explainable and immediately actionable.
