SUMMARY

The constitutional candidates discovered so far are coherent, but many repeat
the same underlying protections in different language.

The dominant pattern is:

Forge is not a CRM because it does not merely store records.

Forge is moving toward Career OS because it interprets career context, evidence,
development, excellence and readiness into responsible decisions and action.

TOP CONSOLIDATED FAMILIES

1. Universal Truths

- Forge is not a CRM, dashboard collection, database or sales tracker.
- Forge converts context into decisions and action paths.
- Evidence must precede judgment.
- Human dignity must be preserved.
- Purpose is human context, not pressure.

2. Career Truths

- Forge is a Career Operating System candidate.
- Career Stage is first-class.
- Career Stage, Operating State and Leadership Stage are separate.
- Professional Excellence is first-class.
- Professional Development is first-class.
- Readiness is first-class.
- Potential, Readiness and Excellence are distinct.

3. SMNYL Contextual Truths

- SMNYL is a human development business expressed through financial services.
- Financial advisory often operates in latent demand.
- Advisors help clients recognize future problems before present crisis.

4. Ethical Boundaries

- Manager Intelligence develops people, not surveils them.
- No single score should represent human growth.
- Production is evidence, not proof.
- Title, tenure, compensation, confidence, team size and charisma are not proof.
- Local hierarchy belongs in organization profiles or rule packs.
- Latent demand must not justify manipulation.

5. Product Principles

- Value before work.
- Show before ask.
- Development before reporting.
- Context before metric.
- Stage-aware interpretation.
- Learning by doing.

OVERLAPS

Overlap 1:
Professional Excellence and Readiness.

Difference:
Excellence describes quality inside responsibility.
Readiness describes responsible ability to handle a specific responsibility.

Risk:
Excellent performance in one responsibility can be mistaken for readiness for
another.

Overlap 2:
Professional Development and Readiness.

Difference:
Development explains growth mechanism.
Readiness governs responsibility assignment.

Risk:
Growth trend can be mistaken for current readiness.

Overlap 3:
Career Stage and Leadership Stage.

Difference:
Career Stage is professional maturity/lifecycle.
Leadership Stage is organizational responsibility.

Risk:
Manager/Subdirector/Director can be mistaken for career maturity.

Overlap 4:
SMNYL human development truth and Forge Core.

Difference:
SMNYL context explains a specific financial advisory environment.
Forge Core must remain universal.

Risk:
SMNYL-specific truth could be hardcoded into Forge Core.
