# Interview Knowledge Base v1.0

Source reviewed:

- `/storage/emulated/0/Download/Guion Entrevistas.pdf`

Purpose:

Convert the interview script into structured Forge knowledge for future Interview Intelligence.

This document is not an engine. It does not score candidates, approve candidates, or create workflow logic.

## Core Principles

- Interview Intelligence supports manager judgment; it does not replace it.
- Interview notes must preserve observed evidence, not invented facts.
- The interview process evaluates both candidate quality and career fit.
- Market access is critical, but Forge must not assume that a large market guarantees success.
- RDA / referral development and natural market quality are critical signals for advisor viability.
- The Career Interview is selling the career, not only evaluating the candidate.
- The Additional Interview is the start of operational execution.

## Interview Phases

### INITIAL_INTERVIEW

Purpose:

- Open the recruitment process.
- Build rapport and explain the career opportunity at a high level.
- Capture basic candidate identity and context.
- Detect early fit, motivation, income expectation, life context, and natural market/referral potential.
- Prepare the candidate for the next process steps and profile questionnaire.

Required inputs:

- Candidate full name.
- Phone.
- Email.
- Date of birth.
- Personal, professional, academic and hobby context.
- Residence and household context.
- Current work situation.
- Income expectation.
- Five-year vision.
- Initial referral / dream team names if available.

Questions:

- Who are you personally, professionally, academically, and what are your hobbies?
- Where do you live and who do you live with?
- Do you rent, own, or live with family?
- How long have you lived there and where did you live before?
- What is your last degree or academic level?
- Do you have economic dependents?
- Where are you working now?
- If it depended on you, what income would you expect?
- What do you like most and least about what you do today?
- How do you visualize yourself in the next five years?
- Who would be the first people you would invite if you were building your own team?

Expected evidence:

- Candidate can describe themselves beyond the CV.
- Candidate has a clear income expectation.
- Candidate can articulate future vision.
- Candidate has enough local/social context to begin market validation.
- Candidate reacts with interest to entrepreneurial income, growth, quality of life, and training.
- Candidate can name possible referrals or team candidates.

Red flags:

- No clear income expectation.
- No five-year vision or only vague future language.
- Weak local roots with no compensating market signal.
- Little curiosity about the career.
- Resistance to questionnaire or process.
- Cannot name people for referral/team exercise.
- Treats the role only as employment, not entrepreneurial career.

Next action:

- Send profile questionnaire.
- Confirm receipt and instructions.
- Schedule SELECTION_INTERVIEW if candidate remains viable.
- If information is missing, collect it before advancing.

### SELECTION_INTERVIEW

Purpose:

- Start the formal selection process.
- Evaluate motivation, ambition, perseverance, success pattern, money motivation, recovery from failure, and validation style.
- Answer the candidate's first practical questions: what is sold, how it is sold, and to whom it is sold.
- Validate whether the candidate wants and can do the work.
- Introduce market natural, referrals, scripts, and sales cycle.

Required inputs:

- Confirmation that candidate read assigned materials.
- Candidate reaction to readings.
- Whether the candidate discussed the opportunity with an important person.
- Motivation for considering the career.
- Career priorities.
- Unfinished goals.
- Childhood/school/extracurricular history.
- First job story.
- Strengths and weaknesses.
- Failure recovery story.
- Competitiveness.
- Internal/external validation style.
- Highest annual income.
- First-year and five-year income goals.
- Best friend description.
- Best boss/supervisor description.
- Success pattern.
- Natural market estimate.
- Referral openness.
- Script/process willingness.

Questions:

- Why are you willing to consider this career?
- What matters most to you in a career and how would it benefit you?
- What goals have you not completed yet, why are they incomplete, and what would need to change?
- What was school like in childhood and adolescence, including extracurriculars?
- What was your first job, how old were you, and why did you start working?
- What are your biggest strength and weakness?
- Tell me about a time you failed at something important; how did you handle it and what did you learn?
- Are you competitive?
- Do you consider yourself lucky?
- How do you know when you have done a good job?
- What is the highest annual income you have had?
- What would be a realistic first-year income goal in a new career?
- What income do you want five years from now?
- Who is your best friend and how would that person describe you?
- Who was your best boss or supervisor and how would they describe you?
- What are your life successes or success pattern?
- Is there anything else that would help me know you better?
- How many people do you know in your natural market?
- Why is natural market more effective than starting with a cold client book?

Expected evidence:

- Candidate has specific reasons for considering the career.
- Candidate links career priorities to personal benefit.
- Candidate has ambition and self-awareness around incomplete goals.
- Candidate can explain recovery from failure.
- Candidate shows competitiveness without entitlement.
- Candidate has money motivation grounded in realistic goals.
- Candidate understands trust as the reason for natural market.
- Candidate accepts that referrals are fuel for the business.
- Candidate understands scripts as a process to master before personalizing.

Red flags:

- Avoids responsibility for unfinished goals.
- Weak or defensive failure recovery.
- No evidence of competitiveness.
- Only external validation and no self-standard.
- Unrealistic income expectations with no activity logic.
- Low money motivation.
- Rejects scripts before learning them.
- Does not understand natural market or referrals.
- Says the sales cycle sounds unacceptable.
- Cannot identify who would receive their call or meeting.

Next action:

- Advance to CAREER_INTERVIEW if motivation, coachability, and market signals are strong enough.
- Use ADDITIONAL_INTERVIEW or extra manager review if signals are incomplete or contradictory.
- Reject or pause if core character, motivation, process, or market risks are severe.

### CAREER_INTERVIEW

Purpose:

- Sell the advisor career with more depth.
- Explain compensation, training, development, and career path in operational terms.
- Validate whether the candidate wants the actual career after understanding the business.
- Confirm commitment to activity, scripts, natural market, referrals, and manager-led development.

Required inputs:

- Selection interview notes.
- Candidate motivation and income goals.
- Candidate market/referral signals.
- Candidate response to what/how/to-whom sales explanation.
- Candidate willingness to learn scripts and follow process.
- Candidate questions or objections.

Questions:

- What part of the career is most attractive to you now?
- What concern do you still have about this career?
- What income goal would make this worth the effort?
- What activity level are you willing to commit to?
- Who in your natural market would you contact first?
- Are you willing to learn the scripts before adapting them?
- Who needs to support or approve this decision with you?

Expected evidence:

- Candidate can explain why the career fits their goals.
- Candidate understands that income is tied to activity and execution.
- Candidate accepts training and manager guidance.
- Candidate can identify initial market actions.
- Candidate has enough commitment to enter operational preparation.

Red flags:

- Likes the income upside but avoids activity.
- Wants freedom without accepting accountability.
- Wants to create their own system before learning the proven one.
- Cannot name first contacts or referrals.
- Treats training as optional.
- Has unresolved decision dependencies.

Next action:

- Move to ADDITIONAL_INTERVIEW when candidate is selected and ready to begin operational preparation.
- Assign manager follow-up if decision support or family alignment is needed.
- Stop process if the candidate rejects core career mechanics.

Source note:

- `Guion Entrevistas.pdf` announces that later meetings explain training and compensation, but the extracted PDF does not include a complete Career Interview script. Details above are structured from the explicit purpose implied by the source and should be validated against the full Career Interview script before engine implementation.

### ADDITIONAL_INTERVIEW

Purpose:

- Begin operational execution after selection.
- Convert interest into concrete preparation.
- Validate compliance with first assignments.
- Start readiness around market list, RDA/referrals, scripts, and process behavior.

Required inputs:

- Candidate selected or under final review.
- Career interview outcome.
- Natural market list status.
- Project 200 / known-person list status.
- Referral/RDA quality.
- Script memorization status.
- First assignments completed or pending.
- Manager observations.

Questions:

- Did you complete the assigned market list?
- How many people are in your initial list?
- Which contacts are easiest to approach first and why?
- Which referrals or RDA conversations can open better markets?
- Which scripts did you memorize or practice?
- What part of the process feels hardest right now?
- What specific assignment will you complete before the next meeting?

Expected evidence:

- Candidate completes assignments.
- Candidate brings a usable market list.
- Candidate can distinguish warm, trusted contacts from weak contacts.
- Candidate shows willingness to practice scripts.
- Candidate accepts manager correction.
- Candidate is ready to enter precontract or additional preparation.

Red flags:

- Incomplete market list.
- No RDA/referral development.
- Avoids script practice.
- Misses assignments or interviews.
- Gives excuses instead of correcting behavior.
- Confuses enthusiasm with execution.
- Large market but no process discipline.

Next action:

- Move to precontract preparation if assignments and manager review are positive.
- Assign coaching if there is potential but execution is weak.
- Hold or reject if coachability and operational compliance are poor.

Source note:

- The source PDF does not include a full Additional Interview section. The operational framing comes from the source's emphasis on next meetings, natural market, referrals, scripts, and process readiness. It should be validated against the full Additional Interview script before engine implementation.

## Candidate Signals Extracted

### Income Expectation

Evidence:

- Candidate states desired income or expected income if it depended on them.
- Candidate gives first-year and five-year income goals.
- Candidate has prior income reference.

Assessment dimensions:

- `vitalFactors.moneyMotivation`
- `vitalFactors.drive`
- `candidateAssessment.confidence`

Risks:

- No income goal.
- Unrealistic income goal without activity commitment.
- Low money motivation for a commission-driven career.

### Five Year Vision

Evidence:

- Candidate describes where they see themselves in five years.
- Candidate connects career to long-term growth.

Assessment dimensions:

- `vitalFactors.retentionPotential`
- `vitalFactors.drive`
- `vitalFactors.successHistory`

Risks:

- No future vision.
- Future vision is incompatible with advisor career.

### Hobbies

Evidence:

- Candidate shares hobbies, social interests, discipline, or community involvement.

Assessment dimensions:

- `hardFactors.career`
- `vitalFactors.energy`
- `marketQuality.networkStrength`

Risks:

- Isolated lifestyle with weak network signal.
- No evidence of discipline or social exposure.

### Motivation

Evidence:

- Candidate explains why they are considering the career.
- Candidate identifies what matters in a career and why.

Assessment dimensions:

- `vitalFactors.drive`
- `vitalFactors.moneyMotivation`
- `vitalFactors.retentionPotential`

Risks:

- Motivation is vague or only escape-based.
- Candidate wants benefits without accepting tradeoffs.

### Unfinished Goals

Evidence:

- Candidate names incomplete goals, why they remain incomplete, whether they still matter, and what must change.

Assessment dimensions:

- `vitalFactors.successHistory`
- `vitalFactors.drive`
- `vitalFactors.character`

Risks:

- Blames only external causes.
- No ownership or corrective plan.

### First Job

Evidence:

- Candidate explains first job, age, and reason for working.

Assessment dimensions:

- `vitalFactors.successHistory`
- `vitalFactors.drive`
- `vitalFactors.character`

Risks:

- No work history ownership.
- Avoids responsibility or effort.

### Failure Recovery

Evidence:

- Candidate explains a meaningful failure, response, and learning.

Assessment dimensions:

- `vitalFactors.character`
- `vitalFactors.retentionPotential`
- `candidateAssessment.risks`

Risks:

- Defensive explanation.
- No learning.
- Repeated avoidance pattern.

### Competitiveness

Evidence:

- Candidate states whether they are competitive and gives examples.

Assessment dimensions:

- `vitalFactors.drive`
- `vitalFactors.energy`
- `vitalFactors.successHistory`

Risks:

- No competitive drive.
- Entitlement without work ethic.

### Money Motivation

Evidence:

- Candidate talks clearly about income goals, prior income, desired first-year income, and five-year income.

Assessment dimensions:

- `vitalFactors.moneyMotivation`
- `vitalFactors.drive`

Risks:

- Avoids money discussion.
- Income expectation is disconnected from activity.

### Internal / External Validation

Evidence:

- Candidate explains how they know they did a good job.

Assessment dimensions:

- `vitalFactors.character`
- `vitalFactors.mentalAgility`
- `candidateAssessment.confidence`

Risks:

- Needs only external validation.
- No personal standard for quality.

### Natural Market

Evidence:

- Candidate can estimate people they know.
- Candidate understands trust as the reason for starting with natural market.

Assessment dimensions:

- `marketQuality.project200Size`
- `marketQuality.networkStrength`
- `marketQuality.appointmentPotential`

Risks:

- Small or low-trust natural market.
- Does not understand trust-based access.

### Referrals

Evidence:

- Candidate can name people to invite or refer.
- Candidate understands referrals as business fuel.

Assessment dimensions:

- `marketQuality.advisorReferralsCount`
- `marketQuality.networkStrength`
- `marketQuality.appointmentPotential`

Risks:

- Cannot name referral candidates.
- Resists referral conversations.

### Project 200

Evidence:

- Candidate starts or can build a list of known people.
- Candidate can segment family, friends, school, work, and other networks.

Assessment dimensions:

- `marketQuality.project200Size`
- `marketQuality.prospectCount`
- `candidateAssessment.confidence`

Risks:

- Project 200 not started.
- Large list with weak relationship quality.

### Scripts Memorized

Evidence:

- Candidate accepts that scripts exist for the sales cycle.
- Candidate agrees to learn scripts before personalizing them.

Assessment dimensions:

- `coachabilitySignals.memorizesScripts`
- `coachabilitySignals.followsProcess`
- `coachabilitySignals.acceptsFeedback`

Risks:

- Rejects scripts.
- Wants to personalize before mastering.
- Treats process as optional.

## Notes For Future Interview Intelligence Foundation

Potential outputs:

- `phase`
- `observedSignals`
- `missingEvidence`
- `redFlags`
- `candidateAssessmentInputs`
- `recommendedNextAction`
- `managerNotes`
- `confidence`

Potential phase mapping:

- `INITIAL_INTERVIEW` maps to identity, hard factors, early motivation, and early market signals.
- `SELECTION_INTERVIEW` maps to vital factors, coachability, money motivation, natural market, and process fit.
- `CAREER_INTERVIEW` maps to career commitment, objections, activity acceptance, and compensation/training understanding.
- `ADDITIONAL_INTERVIEW` maps to operational readiness, assignments, Project 200, RDA/referral quality, and scripts.

Important implementation boundaries:

- Do not score from a single question alone.
- Do not treat a large market as automatic success.
- Do not treat income ambition as sufficient without coachability and execution.
- Do not overwrite interview history; preserve each interview event.
- Do not convert interview phases into hiring decisions without manager review.
- Do not invent RDA, market size, referrals, scripts memorized, or income history.

## Gaps Detected

- The source PDF contains detailed INITIAL_INTERVIEW and SELECTION_INTERVIEW material, but does not include full CAREER_INTERVIEW and ADDITIONAL_INTERVIEW scripts.
- The term `RDA` is not explicit in the extracted source, though referral development and natural market are central.
- `Project 200` is implied by the natural-market count/list exercise, but the exact term is not present in the extracted source.
- Current `schemas/interview.schema.json` uses generic phase names and does not yet include the requested business phases: `INITIAL_INTERVIEW`, `SELECTION_INTERVIEW`, `CAREER_INTERVIEW`, `ADDITIONAL_INTERVIEW`.
- There is no dedicated schema yet for `observedSignals`, `questionEvidence`, or `assessmentDimensionMapping`.

## Recommendations

1. Update `schemas/interview.schema.json` in a future PAQ to support the four business interview phases.
2. Create canonical interview fixtures for each phase before implementing engines.
3. Add `questionEvidence[]` to preserve question, candidate answer summary, detected signal, and assessment dimension.
4. Add an Interview Intelligence foundation engine only after the full Career and Additional scripts are available.
5. Connect Interview Intelligence outputs to Candidate Assessment as structured inputs, not as direct hiring decisions.
