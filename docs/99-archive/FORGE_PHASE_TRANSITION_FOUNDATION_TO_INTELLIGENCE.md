## 1. Executive Summary

### Forge began with a practical commercial problem

Advisors needed a better way to follow up, communicate with prospects, remember what mattered, and take the next right action.

Forge was not born as a CRM.
Forge was not born as an AI platform.
Forge was not born as a dashboard.
Forge was not born as a reporting system.

Forge began as an attempt to solve commercial follow-up, prospect communication, advisor consistency and decision quality.

### The original problem was not

"Where do we store data?"

### The original problem was

"What should the advisor do next, with whom, and why?"

That origin still matters.

As the project evolved, Forge expanded from conversation and relationship support into a broader Sales Operating System and Decision Intelligence System.

Forge discovered that helping advisors sell more requires more than reminders, notes or dashboards.

### It requires

Commercial identity.
Relationship context.
Production facts.
Evidence.
Provenance.
Operational clocks.
Metric ownership.
Rule interpretation.
Compensation clarity.
Forecast separation.
Planning discipline.
Advisor experience.
Behavior intelligence.

### The Foundation Phase existed to answer a necessary question

What must be true underneath Forge so that future intelligence domains can be built without duplicating logic, inventing data, mixing clocks or contaminating Core with carrier-specific rules?

That work is now sufficiently complete.

During the Foundation Phase, Forge achieved:

A Shared Commercial Model.
A hardened identity and attribution model.
An evidence and provenance foundation.
A multi-clock period model.
A metric ownership doctrine.
A RuleSnapshot model.
A commercial events taxonomy.
A formal Rule Pack separation principle.
A Foundation Lock final review.
A Constitution Amendment v1.1 proposal.
A ratification and foundation closure review.

The project is now ready to transition from designing its foundations to building and formalizing intelligence domains on top of them.

### The next phase is

### Intelligence Domains Phase

This does not mean implementation is automatically approved.
It means the conceptual foundation is stable enough that future work should build on it instead of reopening it.

## 4. Major Architectural Discoveries

The Foundation Phase changed Forge's direction through several major discoveries.


4.1 Forge Core is not Rule Pack

### Discovery
Forge Core contains universal primitives.
Rule Packs contain carrier, channel and commercial-model interpretations.

### Why it changed the product
Forge can now support multiple carriers, distribution channels, contests, compensation plans, career systems and recognition models without modifying Core.


4.2 Production Events are Facts

### Discovery
Production Events are commercial facts.
Contest, Compensation, Career, Conservation and Manager Intelligence are interpretations over those facts.

### Why it changed the product
Forge no longer confuses an event with a result.
A paid policy is not a contest qualification.
A commission event is not the same thing as production.
A bonus is an interpreted result, not a raw fact.


4.3 One Metric, One Owner

### Discovery
Every metric needs a single conceptual owner.

### Why it changed the product
Forge avoids inconsistent dashboards, mismatched compensation explanations, duplicate policy counts and competing LIMRA or IGC values.


4.4 Capture Once

### Discovery
Forge must become the place where work happens, not a place where users re-enter work already done elsewhere.

### Why it changed the product
This shifts Forge from a reporting companion to an operational system.
Reports become generated outputs.
Manual capture becomes a last resort.


4.5 Forecasts Are Suggestions, Facts Are Facts

### Discovery
Forecasts can guide decisions, but they cannot be treated as confirmed truth.

### Why it changed the product
This protects trust and prevents future Forecast Intelligence from overstating certainty.


4.6 Goal to Forecast to Gap to Actions

### Discovery
Planning must become executable.

### Why it changed the product
Forge should not merely show goals.
Forge should translate goals into forecasts, gaps and actions.


4.7 Advisor Development over Dashboard Reporting

### Discovery
Forge's goal is not to report performance.
Forge's goal is to develop better advisors, managers and partners.

### Why it changed the product
This raises the bar for every intelligence domain.
A dashboard that does not create better decisions, behavior or judgment is insufficient.


4.8 Causal Clarity

### Discovery
The advisor must understand the causal chain between action and result.

### Why it changed the product
Forge should explain why two policies, a premium gap, a qualification threshold, a bonus or a conservation risk matters.
This converts information into commercial reasoning.

## 7. Implementation Guidance

The next phase must preserve foundation discipline.

The following should be avoided.


7.1 Do not reopen Foundation Lock without strong evidence

Foundation should not be reopened because a domain feels difficult.

Reopening requires evidence that the foundation cannot explain a real scenario without distortion.


7.2 Do not introduce carrier logic into Forge Core

Carrier-specific terms, contests, rules, compensation formulas, activity targets and recognition models belong in Rule Packs.

Core must remain universal.


7.3 Do not duplicate metrics

Every metric has one conceptual owner.

Domains may consume metrics.
They must not recalculate official metrics they do not own.


7.4 Do not create new owners for existing metrics

If a metric already has an owner, future domains must request or consume that metric.

They must not create parallel truth.


7.5 Do not skip Rule Packs

If a business interpretation depends on carrier, channel, contest, compensation, career, promotion, KPI, activity or recognition rules, it must go through Rule Pack context.


7.6 Do not build dashboards without intelligence

A dashboard is not enough.

### Every surface should move toward

Meaning.
Decision.
Action.
Economic clarity.
Causal understanding.


7.7 Do not confuse forecasts with confirmed outcomes

Forecasts must not be shown as facts.

Predicted income is not paid income.
Projected qualification is not confirmed qualification.
Expected production is not production.


7.8 Do not ask users to capture data Forge can already know

Capture Once should become a product review gate.

Manual capture must be justified by decision value and evidence necessity.


7.9 Do not hide uncertainty

Unknown must remain unknown.

Low confidence must be visible.
Missing evidence must be acknowledged.
Assumptions must be labeled.


7.10 Do not create dependence without development

Forge should guide users and develop their commercial judgment.

The system should transfer reasoning progressively to advisors, managers and partners.

The real problem was decision quality.

The Foundation Phase gave Forge the universal primitives, clocks, events, evidence, metric ownership and rule interpretation model required to reason safely.

Forge now enters the Intelligence Domains Phase.

Build the best advisor for advisors.
