## 1. Executive Summary

Recruitment Intelligence is the Forge domain responsible for explaining whether a Partner, manager or commercial organization is creating enough future advisor capacity through candidate flow.

It does not begin with Relationship Capital.
It does not begin with Market Natural.
It does not begin with P200.

Recruitment Intelligence consumes those layers.

Recruitment begins when an existing or discovered relationship is activated with explicit candidate-generation intent.

### The hardened causal boundary is

Relationship Capital
Market Natural
P200 Evidence
Relationship Activation
Candidate Flow
Recruitment Funnel
Precontract Handoff

Recruitment Intelligence exists because future commercial growth does not start with compensation, productivity or production. It starts earlier, when the organization generates enough viable candidates who can become advisors.

### The operating phrase

"Si reclutas, nada mas importa.
Si no reclutas, nada mas importa."

is not a slogan inside Forge.

Architecturally, it means:

If candidate flow is insufficient, later domains will starve.
If candidate quality is poor, development will absorb the failure.
If source quality is weak, forecast confidence declines.
If recruitment stops, future productivity risk increases.

### Recruitment Intelligence answers

Do I have enough candidate flow?
Where is candidate flow coming from?
Which sources are producing viable candidates?
Where does the funnel break?
Which candidates are stuck?
Which conversion is deteriorating?
Which Partner is creating future advisor capacity?
Which recruitment behavior predicts future shortage?
What recruitment gap threatens future productivity?

### Recruitment Intelligence enables decisions such as

Activate more warm sources.
Re-engage Centers of Influence.
Ask more Magic Questions.
Reduce dependence on cold sources.
Coach a Partner on interview conversion.
Prioritize stuck candidates.
Protect future productivity by restoring candidate flow.

### Final hardening conclusion

Recruitment Intelligence is ready for ARCHITECTURE LOCK at the domain-boundary level.

SMNYL Agency 2026 recruitment vocabulary, stages and thresholds belong to the Rule Pack.
Forge Core must only preserve the universal primitives, events, ownership and interpretation boundaries.


## 2. Recruitment Definition

### Recruitment Intelligence is

The domain that transforms activated relationship access into measured candidate flow, funnel conversion, recruitment risk and future advisor capacity.

It explains whether the organization is consistently creating enough potential advisors, from the right sources, with enough quality, at the right pace, to sustain future growth.

### Recruitment Intelligence starts when

A relationship, market contact, referred person, Center of Influence, Magic Question result, nominated person, observed person or cold contact is activated with the specific intent of generating a candidate for the advisor career.

### Recruitment Intelligence ends when

The candidate reaches the formal handoff into Precontract, or the candidate exits, stalls, is disqualified or is abandoned.

Recruitment Intelligence may consume downstream signed outcomes because signed outcomes validate upstream recruitment quality.

However, Recruitment Intelligence does not own the full Precontract lifecycle, advisor contract state, development process, productivity, compensation or career progression.

### Recruitment Intelligence is not

Relationship Capital.
Market Natural.
P200.
Generic Sales Prospecting.
Relationship Activation as a whole.
Precontract Intelligence.
Development Intelligence.
Productivity Intelligence.
Compensation Intelligence.
Forecast Intelligence as a whole.
Business Planning Intelligence.
Partner Intelligence as a whole.

Recruitment Intelligence is not merely "reclutamiento" as an administrative activity.

### It is a decision intelligence domain that explains

Candidate flow.
Candidate source quality.
Interview conversion.
Pipeline health.
Recruitment risk.
Future advisor capacity.
Recruitment contribution to Partner growth.

### Universal definition

Recruitment Intelligence owns the interpretation of candidate-generation activity after relationship activation and before formal precontract ownership.

### Rule Pack definition

Specific labels such as EI, ES, EC, EA, Conectado, Firmado, NOM, CI, RDA, OP, PM, FF and any qualifying thresholds belong to the active Rule Pack.

Forge Core must not hardcode those meanings as universal business law.

## 8. Source Intelligence Hardening

Source Intelligence inside Recruitment must evaluate both volume and quality.

Source count alone is not sufficient.


NOM

### Meaning
Nominados.

### Semantics
Named people nominated or identified through known context.

### Typical warmth
Medium to warm, depending on who nominated and why.

### Trust transfer
Possible, but not automatic.

### Strength
Can produce candidate flow when nomination carries context and credibility.

### Weakness
May become a raw-name bucket without activation or quality.

### Expected conversion
Should outperform pure cold source when nomination contains real relational context.

### Risk
Inflated nomination volume without candidate movement.

### Leading indicators
NOM-to-EI conversion.
NOM source freshness.
NOM trust path clarity.


CI

### Meaning
Centro de Influencia.

### Semantics
A person or node with influence, credibility and access to a community or group.

### Typical warmth
High potential warmth.

### Trust transfer
High when CI credibility is real and introduction is direct.

### Strength
Can open access to multiple candidates or communities.

### Weakness
CI can stop referring, provide low-fit names or become inactive.

### Expected conversion
Should be measured by introduced candidates, EI conversion, connected conversion and signed quality.

### Risk
Treating CI count as value without introductions or outcomes.

### Leading indicators
Active CI count.
CI contact recency.
CI introduction rate.
CI-to-EI conversion.
CI-to-connected conversion.


RDA

### Meaning
Referido de Asesor.

### Semantics
Candidate referred by an advisor.

### Typical warmth
Warm when advisor credibility transfers.

### Trust transfer
Medium to high depending on advisor relationship and credibility.

### Strength
Can produce candidates with clearer career context.

### Weakness
Advisor may refer for obligation, not quality.

### Expected conversion
Should be measured by referred-to-interview and referred-to-connected conversion.

### Risk
Advisor referral volume without quality.

### Leading indicators
RDA-to-EI conversion.
RDA-to-connected conversion.
RDA source advisor quality.


OP

### Meaning
Observacion Personal.

### Semantics
Person identified by direct observation.

### Typical warmth
Variable.

### Trust transfer
Low to medium unless relationship exists.

### Strength
Can identify talent outside existing circles.

### Weakness
May rely on subjective impression without evidence.

### Expected conversion
Variable, should be validated by stage movement.

### Risk
False positives based on appearance, confidence or superficial traits.

### Leading indicators
OP-to-contact completion.
OP-to-EI conversion.
OP drop reason pattern.


PM

### Meaning
Pregunta Magica.

### Semantics
Network expansion pattern that asks an interviewee or contact who they would invite to a similar opportunity.

### Typical warmth
Variable.

### Trust transfer
Depends on the relationship between referrer and referred person.

### Strength
Creates candidate network loops.

### Weakness
Can generate many names with low quality if asked mechanically.

### Expected conversion
Must be evaluated by yield and candidate quality, not PM count.

### Risk
Treating PM as only a source code.

### Leading indicators
PM ask rate.
PM names per ask.
PM-to-EI conversion.
PM-to-connected conversion.
PM referral depth.


FF

### Meaning
Fuente Fria.

### Semantics
Cold source with little or no prior trust path.

### Typical warmth
Low.

### Trust transfer
Low or absent.

### Strength
Can supplement pipeline when warm sources are insufficient.

### Weakness
Usually higher friction and lower trust.

### Expected conversion
Must be measured separately from warm sources.

### Risk
High FF dependency can hide weak Relationship Capital activation.

### Leading indicators
FF ratio.
FF-to-EI conversion.
FF-to-connected conversion.
Cost or effort per viable candidate.


Source Intelligence rules

Warm source ratio is more important than raw source count.
Trust path clarity is more important than source label.
Source-to-stage conversion is more important than source volume.
Source quality must be learned from outcomes.
Forge must not assume a source is good without evidence.

Recruitment conversion must explain movement between stages, not merely count completed activities.


EI to ES

### Meaning
Initial interview produced enough fit, interest or qualification to continue.

### Decision enabled
Improve initial filtering.
Improve invitation quality.
Coach first interview execution.

### Risk revealed

EI count.
EI-to-ES conversion.

## 10. Recruitment Risk Model

Recruitment Risk is owned by Recruitment Intelligence.

It identifies threats to future candidate flow, connected candidates, signed advisors and future advisor capacity.


Risk: Many names, few interviews

### Meaning
Relationship or source inventory is not being converted into candidate conversations.

### Possible causes
Low activation.
Poor contact execution.
Weak candidate fit.
P200 used as static list.
No trust transfer.

### Early signals
High P200 or name count with low EI.
Low contact completion.
Low appointment request.


Risk: Many interviews, few connected

### Meaning
Interview activity is not producing precontract movement.

### Possible causes
Poor candidate quality.
Weak selection.
Weak career explanation.
Poor economic clarity.
Low candidate commitment.

### Early signals
EI-to-ES drop.
EC-to-EA drop.
EA-to-connected drop.
Repeated no-shows.


Risk: Many connected, few signed

### Meaning
Recruitment handoff is not becoming advisor conversion.

### Possible causes
Precontract issue.
Expectation mismatch.
Weak commitment.
Operational friction.
Development gap.

### Recruitment position
Consumes this risk but must not own the entire downstream failure.


Risk: High dependence on FF

### Meaning
Candidate flow relies heavily on cold sources.

### Possible causes
Underused Relationship Capital.
Inactive CI.
Low PM.
Weak Market Natural activation.

### Early signals
Rising FF ratio.
Declining warm source ratio.
Low CI or RDA production.


Risk: Low PM

### Meaning

## 13. Red Team Review

### Scenario 1
100 candidates, 0 signed.

### What breaks
If Recruitment treats candidate count as success, the model fails.

### Correct interpretation
Candidate volume is not recruitment success.
Recruitment must evaluate source quality, conversion, precontract handoff and signed outcome.

### Limit
Recruitment can identify upstream weakness or handoff weakness.
It cannot own all precontract failure.


### Scenario 2
10 signed, 0 productivity.

### What breaks
If Recruitment claims success based only on signed count, later productivity failure is hidden.

### Correct interpretation
Recruitment succeeded in candidate conversion but may have produced low-quality advisor capacity.
Productivity and Development must evaluate post-signing performance.
Recruitment should consume productivity feedback for future source quality, but not own productivity.


### Scenario 3
Many PM, bad quality.

### What breaks
If PM is treated as automatically valuable, Forge rewards noise.

### Correct interpretation
PM must be measured by yield and quality.
Names per PM is not enough.
PM-to-EI, PM-to-connected and PM-to-signed matter.


### Scenario 4
Many connected, little development.

### What breaks
Recruitment may appear successful while Development fails.

### Correct interpretation
Connected is a handoff, not final success.
Recruitment must preserve connected-to-signed and post-signing feedback, but Development owns the next phase.


### Scenario 5
Much Relationship Capital, zero activation.

### What breaks
If Relationship Capital is treated as recruitment capacity, Forge overestimates future candidate flow.

### Correct interpretation
Recruitment consumes only activated relationship access with candidate intent.
Mick should read inactivity as behavior risk.


### Scenario 6
Much P200, zero interviews.

### What breaks
If P200 is treated as recruitment artifact, Forge falsely reports recruiting strength.

### Correct interpretation
P200 is Market Natural evidence.
Recruitment begins only when P200 relationships are activated for candidate flow.


### Scenario 7
Many signed, high desertion.

### What breaks
If Recruitment ignores retention or early productivity feedback, it may keep feeding poor candidate profiles.

### Correct interpretation
Retention and development are downstream, but Recruitment should consume feedback to evaluate source and interview quality.


### Scenario 8
Cold sources produce more signed advisors than warm sources.

### What breaks
If Forge assumes warm sources always outperform cold sources, it becomes biased.

### Correct interpretation
Trust Transfer is a principle candidate, not a guarantee.
Source quality must be learned from evidence.


### Scenario 9
Candidate appears through two Partners.

### What breaks
Duplicate identity and attribution ambiguity can corrupt source metrics and Partner credit.

### Correct interpretation
CommercialPerson identity, CommercialAttribution and provenance must resolve duplicates without overwriting history.


### Scenario 10
Manager changes during recruitment.

### What breaks
If current manager is applied retroactively, historical recruitment attribution becomes false.

### Correct interpretation
Assignments must be effective-dated.
Recruitment metrics must preserve historical manager and Partner context.


### Scenario 11
Rule Pack changes interview definitions.

### What breaks
Historical conversion metrics become invalid if old stages are recalculated with new rules.

### Correct interpretation
RuleSnapshot must preserve stage definitions and evaluation rules.


### Scenario 12
Excel data is incomplete.

### What breaks
Forecasts may appear more precise than evidence supports.

### Correct interpretation
Recruitment must show confidence limits.
Missing dates, sources or stage outcomes reduce confidence.


### Red Team conclusion

The model holds if and only if Forge preserves the boundaries:

P200 is upstream evidence.
Activation is transversal capability.
Recruitment starts at candidate-intent activation.
Connected is a boundary handoff.
Signed is downstream outcome consumed by Recruitment.
Productivity and compensation remain outside Recruitment ownership.
Rule Packs define specific stage meanings.
