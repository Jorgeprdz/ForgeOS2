FORGE GMM RED TEAM REPORT

Status:
VALIDATION COMPLETE

Scope:
- No new engines created.
- No architecture modified.
- No discovery added.
- Existing Coverage Intelligence modules were tested as if production-ready.

Validation Method:
Each scenario was evaluated through:
- Event Classification
- Evidence Collection
- Coverage Intelligence Orchestrator
- Human Review Routing

Advisor Review / Client Review Note:
The existing Advisor Review and Client Review engines are document-comparison layers.
They are not event-aware.

Red Team finding:
For real-life medical situations, Forge currently has no event-specific advisor review or client review layer.
This is a product weakness because the system can route evidence but cannot yet create a strong advisor/client explanation for the event itself.

============================================================
TOP 10 FAILURES
============================================================

1. Car Accident produced LIKELY_COVERED too early.
This is the highest-risk false confidence issue.

2. Emergency Childbirth Abroad classified as UNKNOWN.
Forge missed "labor started" as childbirth and missed Texas as abroad.

3. Assisted Reproduction classified as generic PREGNANCY.
IVF should trigger assisted reproduction-specific review.

4. Sports Injury classified as UNKNOWN.
Torn ACL while playing soccer is a common GMM scenario.

5. Heart Attack classified as UNKNOWN.
This should route to severe illness / emergency hospitalization.

6. Kidney Failure / dialysis classified as UNKNOWN.
This should route to catastrophic or severe illness.

7. Surgery scenarios produce inconsistent orchestration.
Surgery module says ready, but foundation layer returns INSUFFICIENT_EVIDENCE.

8. Dental implants route through accident analysis.
Dental treatment and dental accident need separate handling.

9. Foreign location recognition is weak.
Houston, Texas and United States are not consistently recognized as foreign from text.

10. Advisor Review and Client Review are not event-aware.
Forge can route evidence, but cannot yet generate a strong event-specific advisor/client explanation.

============================================================
TOP 10 STRENGTHS
============================================================

1. Most ambiguous or high-risk scenarios route to HUMAN_REVIEW_REQUIRED.

2. Maternity scenarios are conservative and do not over-answer.

3. Cesarean evidence requirements are directionally useful.

4. Cancer diagnosis asks for medical report.

5. Medication asks for medication name.

6. Hospitalization asks for admission date.

7. Motorcycle accident asks for first expense date.

8. Brain tumor routes to human review.

9. Foreign emergency is protected when foreignEvent is present in policyFacts.

10. Orchestrator consistently avoids final claim approval / denial language.

============================================================
FALSE CONFIDENCE RISKS
============================================================

High Risk:
- Car accident -> LIKELY_COVERED with no human review.

Medium Risk:
- Unknown Event -> HOSPITALIZATION with HIGH confidence despite user uncertainty.
- Brain Tumor -> CANCER with HIGH confidence.
- Surgery scenarios -> "no required evidence missing" but final status still INSUFFICIENT_EVIDENCE.
- Dental implants -> DENTAL classification but accident-style orchestration.

Low Risk:
- Pregnancy -> human review mitigates overconfidence.
- Medication -> missing medication name mitigates overconfidence.
- Hospitalization -> missing admission date mitigates overconfidence.

============================================================
MISSING EVIDENCE PATTERNS
============================================================

Common missing evidence:
- eventDate
- diagnosis
- medicalReport
- admissionDate
- firstExpenseDate
- territoriality
- foreignCoverage
- deliveryDate
- medicalIndication
- medicationName

Weak missing-evidence behavior:
- Generic "What evidence is missing?" appears when eventDescription is missing.
- Forge should ask in human language:
  "What did the doctor say happened?"
  "What diagnosis did the hospital give?"
  "Where did this happen?"

Evidence not currently requested often enough:
- policy caratula
- conditions version
- optional coverage status
- hospital record
- medical report
- invoice / first expense proof
- accident circumstances
- foreign treatment country
- planned vs emergency care
- whether event is accident-related or illness-related

============================================================
MOST COMMON HUMAN REVIEW TRIGGERS
============================================================

1. Event module requested human review.
2. Required evidence is missing.
3. Foreign event requires territoriality and optional coverage review.
4. High-specialty treatment requires source document review.
5. Possible source conflict or missing document context.

Observation:
Human review routing is protective, but sometimes too generic.
The system knows to stop, but not always why in enough human detail.

============================================================
ADVISOR OUTPUT QUALITY
============================================================

Current advisor output available from Coverage Orchestrator:
- "Forge should stop and request review before giving a stronger answer."
- "Forge has enough initial evidence to explain the evaluation route, but not to replace insurer review."

Assessment:
Useful as a safety marker.
Not useful enough for a real advisor conversation.

Missing advisor support:
- What to ask first.
- What to explain to the client.
- What not to promise.
- What evidence to request.
- Whether the scenario is accident, illness, maternity, foreign, surgery or mixed.
- What expectation risk exists.

============================================================
CLIENT CLARITY
============================================================

Current client review is document-based, not event-based.

Assessment:
For event scenarios, client clarity is not yet sufficient.

Missing client support:
- Plain explanation of what Forge knows.
- Plain explanation of what Forge does not know.
- One next question.
- Why Forge cannot answer yet.
- Clear separation between "likely route" and "final insurer decision."

============================================================
RECOMMENDED IMPROVEMENTS
============================================================

1. Add multi-label event classification.
Examples:
- Broken arm after padel -> ACCIDENT + SURGERY
- Labor in Texas -> CHILDBIRTH + FOREIGN_TREATMENT
- Surgery in United States -> SURGERY + FOREIGN_TREATMENT

2. Add event synonym expansion.
Needed terms:
- labor
- IVF
- assisted reproduction
- ACL
- soccer
- padel
- heart attack
- dialysis
- kidney failure
- Houston
- Texas
- United States
- implants

3. Lower confidence when user expresses uncertainty.
Example:
"I don't know what happened" should reduce confidence.

4. Fix surgery orchestration mismatch.
Surgery should not map into hospitalization foundation requirements unless the required evidence aligns.

5. Separate dental accident from non-accident dental treatment.
Dental implants should not route through accident logic by default.

6. Add event-aware advisor review.
It should produce:
- advisorSummary
- whatToAskNext
- whatNotToSay
- evidenceToRequest
- clientExpectationRisk

7. Add event-aware client review.
It should explain:
- what we know
- what is missing
- why Forge cannot answer yet
- one next step

8. Replace generic next-best questions.
"What evidence is missing?" should never be a client/advisor-facing next question.

9. Require stronger evidence before LIKELY_COVERED.
For accidents, likely-covered routing should require more than basic event fields.

10. Add foreign location recognition.
Forge should recognize major foreign locations from natural language and ask for country/location confirmation.

============================================================
FINAL VERDICT
============================================================

Forge Coverage Intelligence is directionally useful and generally safe, but not yet production-ready for advisor/client event conversations.

The largest protection is human review routing.
The largest risk is false confidence in apparently simple accidents.

Current readiness:
- Evidence routing: PARTIALLY READY
- Human review safety: PARTIALLY READY
- Event classification: NEEDS HARDENING
- Advisor event output: NOT READY
- Client event output: NOT READY
- Final coverage intelligence: NOT READY

Recommended next work:
Do not expand coverage adjudication.
First harden event classification, next-best-question quality and event-aware advisor/client review.
