1. PURPOSE

The purpose of 001B is to define the constitutional vocabulary Forge uses to describe uncertainty, limitations, deficiencies and support conditions affecting propositions.

001B exists to preserve visibility of uncertainty before any proposition is evaluated, validated, consumed, escalated or used to derive recommendations.

001B does not determine truth.

001B does not determine validation outcomes.

001B does not determine handling behavior.

001B does not determine recommendations.

001B does not determine automation.

The objective of 001B is uncertainty visibility.

──────────────────────────────────────

2. CONSTITUTIONAL FOUNDATION

Forge does not fundamentally govern:

- PDFs
- OCR outputs
- CRM records
- reports
- spreadsheets
- emails
- notes
- raw evidence artifacts

Those artifacts may contain evidence.

Forge ultimately evaluates propositions supported by evidence.

Evidence supports propositions.

Ownership governs propositions.

Validation evaluates propositions.

Recommendations may derive from propositions.

──────────────────────────────────────

3. DEFINITION OF PROPOSITION

A proposition is any evaluable statement that may be supported, unsupported, partially supported, contradicted, unresolved or uncertain.

Examples:

"The policy contains AVE."

"The policy is active."

"The premium has been paid."

"The current UDI value is X."

"There is cancellation risk."

"There is a follow-up opportunity."

"There is coaching context."

"Projected compensation may be X."

A proposition is not:

- a document
- a PDF
- an OCR extraction
- a workflow
- a task
- a recommendation
- a UI event
- an implementation artifact

──────────────────────────────────────

7. WHY 001B EXISTS

001B exists to prevent hidden uncertainty.

The constitutional risk is not uncertainty.

The constitutional risk is invisible uncertainty.

Examples:

Forecast presented as fact.

Quote presented as policy.

Issued presented as paid.

Compensation projection presented as payment.

Relationship signal presented as permission.

NBA presented as mandate.

NASH presented as client intent.

Manager context presented as enforcement.

Economic motivation presented as pressure.

Each constitutional failure above originates from uncertainty becoming invisible.

001B exists to preserve visibility.

──────────────────────────────────────

8. SUPPORT PRINCIPLE

Support is the body of evidence available to sustain, challenge, qualify or contradict a proposition.

Support is not truth.

Support is not validation.

Support is not recommendation authority.

Support is not decision authority.

Truth cannot exceed available support.

Validation cannot exceed available support.

Recommendations cannot exceed available support.

Outputs cannot exceed available support.

──────────────────────────────────────

9. CORE SUPPORT CONDITIONS

UNKNOWN

Definition:

Available support is insufficient to determine proposition status.

Constitutional Risk:

Unknown support treated as known.

──────────────────────────────────────

PARTIAL

Definition:

Support exists but remains incomplete.

Constitutional Risk:

Incomplete support treated as complete.

──────────────────────────────────────

STALE

Definition:

Support exists but freshness cannot be assumed.

Constitutional Risk:

Outdated support treated as current.

──────────────────────────────────────

CONFLICTING

Definition:

Available support sources disagree.

Constitutional Risk:

Silent conflict resolution.

──────────────────────────────────────

INVALID

Definition:

Support exists but cannot be safely consumed.

Examples:

- unreadable
- corrupted
- structurally unusable
- unverifiable

Constitutional Risk:

Invalid support treated as usable.

──────────────────────────────────────

11. CONSTITUTIONAL INVARIANTS

The following invariants are mandatory.

Unknown support must never appear as known support.

Partial support must never appear as complete support.

Stale support must never appear as current support.

Conflicting support must never be silently resolved.

Invalid support must never appear as usable support.

Truth must never exceed support.

Validation must never exceed support.

Recommendations must never exceed support.

Hidden uncertainty is a constitutional failure.

──────────────────────────────────────

001B does not:

- validate propositions
- approve propositions
- reject propositions
- generate recommendations
- generate tasks
- generate workflows
- generate automation
- generate scoring
- generate rankings
- generate enforcement
- determine ownership
- determine handling behavior
- determine decision authority

──────────────────────────────────────

13. CONSTITUTIONAL THESIS

Forge does not manage certainty.

Forge manages visibility of uncertainty affecting proposition support.

Support limitations must remain visible.

Support deficiencies must remain visible.

Support conflicts must remain visible.

Support freshness limitations must remain visible.

Hidden uncertainty is a constitutional failure.

──────────────────────────────────────

14. FINAL THESIS

Ownership governs propositions.

Support conditions describe the state of support available to propositions.

Validation evaluates propositions.

Handling responds to support deficiencies.

Recommendations may derive from evaluated propositions.

Truth cannot exceed support.

- exceed available support
